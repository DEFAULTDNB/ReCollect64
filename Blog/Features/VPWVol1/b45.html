New VPW64 Mod!

Mod Your Socks Off

Initially announced on October 11th 2024, via the Wrestling Modders, Inc. Twitter, and the Legends of Modding website, nWo Mark has produced the first in a series of quality of life updates for the Japanese wrestling title Virtual Pro Wrestling 64, subtitled "Volume 1: Shit We Wanted to Add!"

 

This is part 1 of what will be an ongoing series of quality of life updates for Virtual Pro Wrestling 64. In this "version 1" I've updated the faces and attires of most of the WCW and nWo roster, added Harlem Heat as alternate skins, and fixed up a few other things such as Vader, Bam Bam Bigelow & Aja Kong. There's a ReadMe file included that covers most of the specifics aswell.

This mod is intended to be used with the 'Star Trinket' English translated version of the Virtual Pro Wrestling 64 ROM. If you do not no where to find that ROM...Google.

Other than that, just drop the texture folders and save files into the appropriate directories & let the fun commence!

The V1 mod features a slew of new textures that overwrite existing characters faces and attire, as well as arena textures to give them a far more realistic or modern aesthetic. The package includes:

Emulation Anxiety

YouTube user @AmitDabydeen posted a short video showcasing Ric Flair vs. Lex Luger, while using the original ROM, though it is possible to use the Virtual Pro Wrestling 64 ROM that has been translated into English by @StarTrinket, if you know where to look.

Virtual Pro Wrestling 64 is essentially the Japanese counterpart to THQ'sWCW vs. nWo: World Tour, which released in Europe and North America; however, VPW64 features an expansive roster of fighters and organisations including WCW, nWo, NJPW, AJPW, UWF, DOAW, and MPW, and it even features legends such as Terry Funk, Tiger Mask, Mahummad Ali, and Mike Tyson! VPW64 will celebrate its 27th anniversary since release on December 19th, 2024!

Currently, the game runs on emulators primarily, though it is possible to use these files to replace textures within the ROM and play it through a repro/flash cart on real hardware.

For those intrigued, the recommended emulator to use is Project 64 with GLide64 or Rice plugins to render the multitude of polished up textures correctly.
