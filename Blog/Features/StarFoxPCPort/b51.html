Star Fox 64 PC Port Announced

Flights of Fancy

Following on from the previous article about Star Fox 64's restored modes, Elarix revealed that a PC port of the beloved N64 flight-based shoot-em-up is being put together ready for the holiday season!

The news was revealed on Elarix's Bluesky account on November 23rd, and was bolstered by an accompanying link to Star Fox 64 speedrunner Rakanai's two-hour-long Twitch stream VOD, which can be downloaded or viewed HERE.

 

The PC Port of Star Fox 64 has been officially announced! Hopefully releasing this holiday, it'll be the best way to play the best Star Fox game!

- Wide and Ultra wide screen
- Unlimited FPS
- HD
- Plus a ton more improvements TBA!

Based on the recently completed Star Fox 64 1:1 matching decompilation project by Sonicdcer, which builds the 1.1 version of the 'starfox64.us.rev1.z64' ROM. Sonicdcer (Sonic Dreamcaster) and KiritoDv are spearheading the port using the Libultraship porting library from the HM64 community, and the project's executable is currently going under the name "Starship", which is a fitting tribute to the original SNES Star Fox's codename and point of influence, Namco's "Starblade".

Lylat Phwoars!

During the stream, Rakanai demonstrates Starships silky smooth 60+ FPS, ultra widescreen support, the ability to change controller mapping for up to four controllers, adjustable stick sensitivity to match the feel of the N64, as well as showing options to alter aspect ratios and internal resolutions of the port to give it superior HD treatment throughout. The GUI also shows graphic settings to change MSAA, FPS target, font replacement, texture replacement & filtering, and has drop-downs for further enhancements and cheats.

Contacting Sonic Dreamcaster, I was told that the port will also include optional previous bug restoration, it will be fully moddable through audio and visual replacement, has LOD (level of detail) settings, cheats, a level select, and more by way of these menus.

One small thing we didn't see in action is the VS mode, which Rakanai briefly fired up but then closed it down. When asked about multiplayer, Sonic Dreamcaster said that it was low priority and would probably be disabled for the first release. Regardless, I do hope that the team will eventually get that feature included in the future for some frantic galactic online battles!

With a release window targeting the holiday season, we should have a fantastic Christmas gift here by way of the PC port, and I will definitely be checking this one out once it lands!
