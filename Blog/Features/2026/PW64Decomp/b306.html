Pilotwings 64 Decompiled

Winging its Way

As of this week, Pilotwings 64 has been 100% decompiled with 1:1 matching code, and over 1790 functions have been boiled down to their raw source code. The project, which has only become publicly visible recently, is spearheaded by Garrett Smith (GCSmith) and another 7 contributers. GCSmith's GitHub also appears to have the Superman 64 decompilation and recompilation firmly underway.

The hopes for a PC port as well as Linux, Mac, PS Vita, Dreamcast, and Nintendo Switch are now immense, and though it appears to be underway, it will take time for recompilations and native projects to take flight and evolve into a playable state. Watch this space for updates!

Smooth Landing

Pilotwings 64 was developed as a flight simulation title by Paradigm Studios with input from Shigeru Miyamoto himself. The game was one of the first three titles released for the Nintendo 64 in Japan on June 23, 1996, and received high critical acclaim for its realistic physics and varied missions and environments.

Currently, the only way to officially play this game is via Nintendo's "Expansion Pak" as part of the online service on the Nintendo Switch and Nintendo Switch 2 consoles. Pilotwings 64 released on NSO in 2022. You could also dig out your cartridges and replay Pilotwings 64 on your Analogue 3D or the upcoming M64, or, alternatively, you could fire up your legally obtained dumps on an emulator such as Ares or RMG.
