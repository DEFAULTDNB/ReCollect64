Casting Fresh Light

James Lambert and Team Ultra Rare are big names in the Nintendo 64 scene. Collectively, their forays into the homebrew arena include Telocation Gemini (2020), Mission Lost Control and Wizard of the Board (2021), Styx (2022), and the incredible Portal 64 demake (2024), and most recently, they blew our minds wide open with Junk Runner 64 (2026).

Together they have been increasingly pushing the boundaries of the system and demonstrating just how incredible modern mechanics and techniques can encapsulate homebrew gaming on a system that is set to celebrate its 30th birthday this month!

His latest project, named Spellcraft, was first unveiled in 2024 and is a fantasty RPG with creating and casting your own spells using the correct combination of runes/symbols as the core mechanic. His most recent video, a technical review of ModRetro's FGPA system, the M64, also dropped a couple of seconds of fresh WIP footage of Spellcraft.

Evolution and Revolution

Based on his GitHub repository, we can infer that the game will feature a z-lock targeting system, possibly akin to Ocarina of Time's; a plethora of enemy types that either swarm in groups, can fly, travel underground, or attack from range; and have abilities such as parrying or zombie-like health-draining mechanics. As well as magical attacks that fill the screen, the protagonist will be able to dodge, counter, block, and parry, as well as fire back incoming projectiles. We should even see mini-games, including a basketball-themed one.

Assuming the same principles from Junk Runner 64's engine are employed, we could see little to no loading times, vibrantly diverse and expansive open-world environments and dungeons to explore without any fog dulling anything down, and a fleshed-out cutscene engine framing the action and conveying the narrative in between bouts of adventuring. The physics system and camera appear very similar to one another too, which would make quite a lot of sense if they had been developed alongside each other from 2024 until now.

Given the pedigree of the homebrew that has come before it and the advances made in each entry to date, Spellcast is undoubtedly going to be a technical marvel, but when it is set to release remains a mystery. With teaser footage, a couple of screenshots here and there, and very little public information yet, the game definitely brings back the nostalgia of reading N64 magazines and trying to picture what the full game will be like based on relatively little solid information.

I love this feeling of awe and wonder, and I can't wait to find out more. Will we see a release before next year? Keep your eyes peeled for more info!
