64DD Prototype Spotted

Testing Times

A non-functional Nintendo 64DD prototype has just sold over on Mercari that, according to Yakumono, aka Luigiblood, reveals a missing drive controller and a possible additional flash-memory slot.

Oh okay a prototype #64DD was sold today. This is non functional, the drive controller is missing (and IPL ROM as well though that's easily dealt with). But the board shows an extra chip slot on the last image, and it looks like maybe Flash memory...? (on the bottom left) That's news to me.

[image or embed]

— Yakumono (@luigiblood.bsky.social) July 19, 2026 at 1:24 PM

This model is completely translucent, showing all of its innards in their beautifully circuit-boarded attire, and the disks themselves are also clear with no metal shutter attached to protect it from dust ingress. What is on that disk remains unclear, though it may just be a blank or a dummy unit for mechanical testing.

Selling for ¥1,100,000 (£5000), this unit wasn't cheap. The last prototype that was discovered was the previously unseen USA region console add-on in 2016. This unit is valued at $10,000-$20,000 USD, which is slightly more than this latest version's $6700 price tag, though the rarity of the NTSC-U lot check prototype variant with an English language IPL is far greater than that of a regular Japanese pre-production model.

It is always interesting to see pre-production hardware, especially after nearly 30 years. It's usually quite rare for a Nintendo 64 prototype item to be known about, let alone find its way up for sale on the open market. Who knows what other incredible N64-related goodies might appear down the line?
