BM64 Recomp Released

Having a Blast

Two days ago, RevoSucks released the first version of Bomberman 64: Recompiled, a project that utilises the work of the current Bomberman 64 decompilation project by Bomberhackers and turns it into something fully playable on your PC, Mac or Linux systems, including Steam Deck!

With high FPS support, sharper visuals, widescreen and modern controls, this comes as a welcome addition to the current list of recompiled projects, with Bomberman 64 remaining a fan favourite even after its release some 29 years ago in September 1997.

An Explosive Start

To enjoy the first release of recompilation, all you need to do is supply a legally obtained Bomberman 64 (U) *.Z64 ROM, with the MD5: 093058ECE14C8CC1A887B2087EB5CFE9, drag and drop it into the folder and run the executable with the bomb icon. It's that simple.

Bomberman 64 features 24 stages across 6 distinct worlds, as well as a fantastic multiplayer mode, though to my knowledge, multiplayer is not implemented into this or any recompiled release as of yet, due to the core N64: Recompiled project not supporting multiple controllers. Once that is developed, I have no doubts that it will be implemented in future releases for all compatible recompiled games.

Notably, RevoSucks has a range of decomp projects within their GitHub repositories, including Pokemon Stadium 1 and 2 as well as Bomberman Hero. Could we be seeing more recompiled games from RevoSucks in the future? Let's wait and see!
