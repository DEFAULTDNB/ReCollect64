Some Surprises Here! ~ May 30, 2026
M64 Games Leak

Leaked Liveries

Just two days before the planned announcements regarding ModRetro's M64 FPGA Nintendo 64 console, a press kit as well as all four of the games planned to be released at launch in July have seemingly leaked onto the internet.

Palmer Luckey's company have managed to bag some interesting titles, namely Acclaim/Probes's Extreme-G/Extreme-G 2 (under the possible double pack guise of Extreme-G Turbo Fusion), and Argonaut /Ubisoft's Buck Bumble. Along with these two, a re-release of Bitmap Bureau's Xeno Crisis appears to be coming, as well as Phoboslab, aka Dominic Szablewski's Xibalba 64 is a surprise addition.

Modern Retro

What's interesting about these releases is that a recent AMA hosted on Reddit revealed that the four games were to be presented on Monday, June 1st, along with "I can confirm new games immediately and many more are locked all the way through end of 2027 so far". They also went on to add that "there will be new 2D games, as well as games from other consoles never before seen on the N64". There was also talk of ports of NES/SNES/PS1 games to N64 format, the possibility of 3DO games, and that there will ne N64 games released with improvements, such as combining two part sequel games into one.

I predicted that the "combining a two part sequel into one" would be Banjo-Kazooie and Banjo-tooie on one cartridge, namely because they recently were recently announced as a double pack for the Evercade Nexus. Instead, in a shock move, we appear to have Extreme-G Turbo Fusion, which I can only imagine is a compilation of the two N64 Extreme-G titles crammed onto one cartridge. I suppose this will use a custom menu to select which game to boot, which we have seen in chinese bootlegs before for compilations such as the Turok Trilogy and the Snowboard Kids games, for example.

Within the AMA there was also mention of no controller coming with the console, options to dim, turn off or change the colour of the M64's light bar, they are exploring netplay and online multiplayer, flashcart support is there for SummerCart64 and ED64 X5 series (but X7 series is being ironed out), higher overclocking options thanks to passive cooling efficiency, and that a BT dongle will be required to use older controllers that only support Bluetooth classic, as opposed to BLT

I'm avidly looking forward to the actual, non-leak, reveal on Monday to see if anything that was leaked today was bait, or something else, and I plan to cover any more revelations as they are revealed.

UPDATE: 01/06/2026 - Extreme-G Turbo Fusion is confirmed to be a double pack and not a whole new game, and the M64 console ($199), wireless pro controller ($89.99) and games are set to ship on July 28th!
