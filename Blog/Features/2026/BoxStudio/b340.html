BoxStudio Visual Editor

Visual Vibes

ExpansionPak, a group of ROM hackers and modders on GitHub, are delving into the decompiled sources of various ROMs, and instead of natively porting them to alternate platforms, they are using it to craft a fully kitted-out visual toolset that can create modifications directly based on decompilation code and documentation. All of this can then be easily repacked back into console-compatible ROMs for testing and, ultimately, enjoying!

HackerSM64 for Super Mario 64 is the first game and decomp base supported, but the project aims to support HackerOOT for TLOZ: Ocarina of Time and ZeldaRet's MM for TLOZ: Majora's Mask. It is definitely plausible that it may support further decomp projects in the future, following the success and feedback of the initial aforementioned titles.

Built to Last

While the initial workflows are still in early development, BoxStudio's approach of modifying raw code from one branch and inserting it back into the ROM instead of the traditional methods of almost blindly poking at bytes, unless you're extremely familiar with the game's internal workings, is extremely exciting. The prospect of playing high-quality ROM hacks on real hardware (or cycle-accurate emulators, such as Ares) is wondrous.

With ROM hackers having access to these types of toolchains and seeing it come together through a fully rendered and textured viewport, the process of adding, modifying, and debugging should be far more streamlined and straightforward. With this fresh set of tools that significantly lowers the barrier to entry to ROM hacking in general, and a new generation of developers with fresh ideas, we could see even more incredible creations come to fruition very quickly.
