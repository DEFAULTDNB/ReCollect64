Project Neon Coming to N64

Sneaky Peeky ~ May 12, 2026

Arcade Awesomeness

A couple of days ago I spotted something over on Fullset's feed that seems rather interesting. As a huge Nintendo 64 fan and avid coin-op kid back in the 90s, seeing a freshly devised retro-inspired 2D shmup heading to the 64-bit console in 2026 is extremely exciting.

Project Neon is a 1-2 player shoot 'em up that was originally developed for Neo Geo hardware (MVS/AES) when it was first unveiled for preorder in 2024. The colourful pixel-art graphics, vertical/TATE mode display and coop mechanics mean that this title might just show us more of what the N64 was capable of in terms of 2D action. It's noteworthy that the WIP N64 port of Project Neon is seen running horizontally, rather than vertically, too. check it out:

Neon Dreams

As unlikely as it is, I would love to see a frantic 4-player mode, specifically for the N64 iteration of this game, just because the Nintendo 64 is more than deserving of having all four controller ports tickled in this day and age; plus, imagine tackling screen-filling bosses as a squad!

The port is still technically unannounced, but to see it in development means that unless something goes drastically wrong it should hopefully see the light of day at some point, though how long it will take beyond the projected November 2026 release date of the Saturn and Dreamcast versions remains a mystery.
