Mario's Modules 2 Released

By Rovertronic ~ Apr 25, 2026

Reinvigorated ROM Hack

Less than five months after Rovert's initial teaser for the sequel to his awesome ROM hack Mario's Modules, which itself released in February last year, today he has released a second, enhanced version entitled Mario's Modules 2.

The first game was incredible, with 19 modules to collect and combine, 10 stars to find and a series of taxing puzzles to solve. I absolutely adored it for what it was, a stunning ROM hack which puts a refreshing spin on and turns the original game in on itself, making something totally unique. This latest version features 15 stars, so please, check out the updated trailer below:

 

Mario’s Modules 2 is a hack where you collect primitive elements called “modules” throughout the game and use them to construct entirely new movesets for Mario.

The hack features two modes:

Manusanctuary Escape - A 15-star campaign designed to introduce you to the mechanics and core systems.

Crystal Quest - A roguelike mode where you have three lives and must complete three procedurally generated levels before facing the final boss.

Along the way, you can collect special vanity modules that let you customize Mario’s appearance however you like. Additionally, each run is generated from a unique seed, ensuring that every playthrough looks and feels a little different.

The hack also features a fully original OST made by ornevelder.

More Mario Madness

The UI has been overhauled to be more intuitive, and there are new modules and combos to discover and experiment with. The addition of passive sockets and "If" statements also brings a lot of possibilities to the table in terms of permutations and variables for each module you're using. There are also modular enemies this time too, which combines a couple of the enemies together into larger threats for you to have to deal with.

The latest features of this version (Version 1.00 of Mario's Modules 2 at the time of writing) also gift us a brand new mode, which utilises procedurally generated levels to drive you up the wall. In "Crystal Quest" you now have just three lives to complete three randomly generated levels and beat the boss in a roguelike-style mode. Every single run will feel different thanks to RNG (Random Number Generation) to seed the games' dungeons. There are mystery boxes too which further randomises your experience, and the revised and refreshed modules you'll find along the way will let you build more complex moves and even fully automate your acrobatic skillset!

Along the way you'll find purple vanity modules too. These allow you to customise Mario in a variety of ways, including converting him into automaton Peach, mixing your own colours for overalls and adding niche cosmetics to fully make your character your own. All of this keeps you searching high and low for more secrets and more ways to style your ancient automaton during your ploy to escape.
