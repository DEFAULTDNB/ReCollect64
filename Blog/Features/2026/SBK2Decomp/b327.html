Snowboard Kids 2 Decompiled

Shredding Source Code

News has spread today that Snowboard Kids 2 has officially been 100% decompiled with 1:1 matching code, according to Chris Lewis's (CDLewis) GitHub account and his handy tracking site, Chrislewis.au.

The wait now begins to see who will be the first to provide a source port or static recompilation of the game, which many view as the "Mario Kart on ice" of snowboarding games for the system. Personally, I was never a fan of the game, but I would definitely be tempted to replay it more with 60 FPS, ultrawide screen support and upscaled textures!

Gnarly Games

Snowboard Kids 2 is the 1999 sequel to 1997's Snowboard Kids, developed by Racdym and published by Altus. Interestingly, though the game came out in PAL and NTSC-U/J formats, the PAL release was exclusively sold in Australia, making it a hugely difficult title for PAL collectors to obtain across the pond in Europe.

Commanding nearly £350 for a cart-only copy and a staggering £1700 for a boxed complete copy. Sealed copies, which are the most difficult to come by, have been seen to go for over £4000, and graded copies for over £5000. I managed to score a cart-only copy a couple of years ago for just £120, before the prices really started to rocket.
