I realise what the date is and no, this is not an April fools! While scrolling yesterday afternoon, I came across something that I thought looked pretty damn snazzy, so I delved deeper. TikTok user DAVIDXGAMESmx (David Brito) has embarked on a mission to integrate a screen into the front of an N64 flash cart, enabling you to see which game is loaded on the additional LCD screen!

The technology is smart, but the implementation is smarter. Having previously worked on a built-in display for the Xbox 360 and doing various mods from internalising a BlueRetro into an N64, building his own memory/rumble pak with a screen that indicates mode, soldering HDMI mods and converting an N64 to run via USB-C, as well as experimenting and flashing PicoCarts and EverDrive 64 clones, the evolution of this product is incredible to see.

REV A is a great POC

Retailing for $1200 (MEX) on the Coleccionistas De Nintendo Facebook group, thats around £50 + shipping and fees, you get a 128mb FPGA and real hardware-compatible PicoCart with a 3D-printed shell that has the screen built into it. The firmware itself is customised to display the correct label per whichever game is selected, and the ability to flash the ROM and upload art is done via the USB-C port on the RPi Pico itself.

One slight OCD-style issue I have with this is that the label screen ratio is incorrect, and so the artwork is either cut off or has been adapted to fit, plus there is a little light bleed behind the curved corners of lighter-coloured shells. I also wonder about the long-term use when constantly drawing power to the screen. If these minor imperfections could be corrected, I think I would be purchasing one immediately to play with.

Overall, I think the Screendrive 64 is a stellar project, but inevitably, uploading just one game at a time gets tiring, especially with a library of 388 titles on the 64-bit console. It would be great to see this kind of screen come to an ED64 or SummerCart64, because that way you could have your entire library at your fingertips and nostalgically displayed at the same time.
