M64 X Hyperkin Pads Revealed

Mix & Match ~ Mar 25, 2026

Third-Party Treat

With a release date for the long-awaited ModRetro's M64 console still up in the air, today ModRetro has given us a little insight as to whom they are officially partnering with when it comes to peripherals and accessories.

It comes as little surprise that Hyperkin, one of the biggest names in third-party pads and adaptors for the Nintendo 64 across the years, has been selectively crafting controllers for their upcoming console to "match the console" and "master the game".

First-Party Fright

When the official wireless ModeRetro M64 controller was revealed in November 2025, it didn't knock my socks off, to be honest. Gone were the curves of the OG N64 controller, which is understandable given Nintendo's patent on the shape; however the angular design and the sketchy-looking D-pad had me a little concerned.

Hyperkin's 10ft-corded offering is nothing new; their Admiral controller has been doing the rounds since 2019, and these new versions simply align with the M64's various colour schemes to seamlessly integrate their products as a wired option for buyers of Palmer Luckey's upcoming 4K FPGA clone console.

Having never experienced a Hyperkin controller for the N64 (other than the Hyperkin Hall-Effect joystick replacement), but having read up on what consumers have said about them, I also fear for the stiff D-pad on this version too. Hopefully they will have slightly redesigned it now for the 2026 editions to increase the quality based on feedback.
