Castlevania Legacy Recomp Released

Reanimating ROMs

Recompilations are coming thick and fast these days, and this time around it's Konami's Castlevania Legacy of Darkness (December 1999), the expanded and definitive edition of Castlevania (January 1999). This updated version fixed camera issues, buffed framerates, rebalanced levels, added in more cutscenes, and added 2 more characters to the roster.

Fliperama86 has been working on this publicly since January this year, making strides in June by finally getting gameplay working, albeit in a glitchy state. Now, his most recent posts state that it's in a working and playable condition on macOS, and there is an experimental PC build too. The current release, though a little buggy, features up to 4K resolution, widescreen (clipping/culling issues), mappable controls, and enhanced framerates.

Coding with Claude

Notably, this is yet another recompilation that deploys the use of AI helpers to take over menial tasks. Just how much the artificial intelligence has been used in this remains to be seen, but AI is definitely a useful tool when it comes to beginning the process of creating a recompilation, at very least.

Like it or not, AI is here to stay, but as long as AI assists and doesn't completely take over the full production of each project, I don't see why it can't be used in some small capacity. The true test is for developers who rely on AI to properly update and fix any issues that arise, because beyond blindly stringing things together, AI doesn't really know the inner workings and nuances a human would need to know to produce easy to maintain, working, and adaptable code.

As usual, I have added links to the recomp GitHub page and the latest release on the front page of this site, but please remember to scan and check any executables you run on your devices before use, as with anything on the internet, it's always better to be safe than sorry.
