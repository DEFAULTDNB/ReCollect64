JPN Exclusive Zool Translated

Classic Creature Comforts

As many N64 players outside of Japan might not be aware, Zool Maju Tsukai Densetsu was a non-Pokemon-like creature-training RPG that deals with some rather dark themes, namely murder, violence against children, substance abuse, cruelty and animal experimentation, and the plight of the natural world.

Like many JPN-exclusive games, this one never gained a lot of airtime in Europe or the USA because it was a tough one to figure your way through without intricate knowledge of the Japanese language. This week, Zoinkity and Whowasphone404 have provided the world with a full English translation patch for the ROM, a translated manual and guide, as well as a 15-second 90s-style trailer for the game, plus a whole bunch of additional tidbits of information and previously unseen content.

Zoinkity has also added SRAM save slots within the ROM patch, a shop to rename characters, and has added a slew of QOL updates that sees 16 major bugs fixed, such as incorrect assets being displayed and even your save game being accidentally deleted. He has also implemented improvements that increase walking and text speed and the ability to skip lengthy cut scenes by pressing the Start button.

Lost in Translation

Containing 168 creatures and following the story of a boy named Leo, 1999's Zool (not Zoor) by Pandora Box and Imagineer was always an enigma to us Western players. Many assumed it to be a simple Pokemon clone, yet it really isn't. Using a visual combination of 3D locations and 2D character sprites, the battles and traversal are more akin to Final Fantasy; charming the creatures involves using a magical flute to orchestrate automated battles, and the bestiary can be completed over iterative playthroughs, rather than all in one run.
