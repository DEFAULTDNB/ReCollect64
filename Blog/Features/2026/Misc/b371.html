P.U.Tb Survival Racing WIP

Ultimate Transhuman Racing

Following their incredible work on other N64 homebrew titles such as Driving Strikers 64, Doki Doki Literature Club 64, and SpaceWaves, to name but a few, Spooky Iluha has recently teased their next N64Brew Game Jam entry long prior to this year's contest even being announced.

With elements of films like The Running Man (1987), and inspiration from games like Carmageddon (1997), there is a heady mix of action and violence with the ultimate goal of winning your freedom.

 

P.U.Tb is a short arcade survival racer set in a world where AI has long taken almost everything useful away from humans, except for one thing: unpredictability.

You are a human driver sent into a televised experimental series. The race is controlled by the AI director P.U.Tb, obsessed with ratings, audience retention, and the "perfect show format." Every lap it changes the rules, throws new tasks at you, and makes sure the audience never gets bored.


I've made a short racing game P.U.Tb for this year's gamejam, made with pyrite64

Inspired by Blur/Carmageddon games

This is a post-jam version that fixes some bugs and adds a few things

Do keep in mind that for this game AI was used for quite a few assets/code due to time constraints

No expansion pak is required to play the game, although recommended

AI Assisted Apocalypse

While the game looks incredible and has a wonderfully post-apocalyptical vibe to it akin to the BattleTanx games, Spooky Iluha has immediately drawn attention to the usage of AI to craft this, rather than not address the currently trending elephant in the room. Spooky Iluha is no stranger to coding on the N64, so why the need for AI to assist? The game is about AI assuming control, so is it an ironic masterstroke that it uses AI at all?

As an independent coder who is working within their free time to craft unique projects, is it reasonable that they deployed the use of AI to generate assets and code towards this project? I would say that if that person isn't artistic or isn't able to succinctly call upon an artistic community to collaboratively craft a project together in a timely, then yes, why shouldn't someone use generative AI to at least make assets to help propel their vision in their own time?

AI is becoming more mainstream, and more projects are using it to achieve things quickly. I have no doubts that Spooky Iluha has used AI wisely in this particular project and that the codebase they're maintaining is majoritively human-derived with a few AI requests helping here and there. It will be interesting to see how this entry goes down in the upcoming Game Jam contests; I shall be avidly watching the reaction.
