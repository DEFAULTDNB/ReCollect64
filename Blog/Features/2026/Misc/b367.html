Not Monkeying Around

Today, Dreamcast developer Falco Girgis has shared the first hardware capture of the fan-favourite N64 racer and debatable Mario Kart 64 beater Diddy Kong Racing running on none other than the SEGA Dreamcast; notably, with sound!

The oddest thing about this early version in particular is that currently no PC port or recompilation currently (publicly) exists for DKR. Usually these release substantially sooner than the beginnings of a DC port being crafted, so this one appears to break the rules and races away with them!

Holy SHIT! It's finally happening, guys!!! The Nintendo 64's definitive kart racer, Diddy Kong Racing, is coming to the Sega Dreamcast!!!

This is a direct hardware capture of my very first preview of the initial build, and it already runs this well, and looks this good! 🙇‍♂️

Dream-karts

The Dreamcast port is apparently being handled by BRUCE, the previously unnamed creator of the Ocarina of Time DC port. Exactly who this mysterious N64-porting figure is remains shrouded in mystery, but regardless, he seems to listen to what people want, and he certainly gets things done.

The decompilation effort sits at 97% for adventure one and 65% for adventure two which is astounding progress given the complexity of Rare games and the undocumented microcode involved. A PC port could therefore also appear sometime soon, as we all know that a complete decompilation is not strictly essential to build a recompilation or PC port, though no announcements have been made at this time.

Expect updates here as and when anything releases, because this is one of my all-time faves, and I will definitely be looking forward to seeing this progress across every platform is can possibly be ported to.
