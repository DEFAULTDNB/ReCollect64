Variants, Oddities & Mysteries

Mexico/South America

When the Nintendo 64 was released, there were more than a few regional variants produced for specific countries. Throughout my ongoing odyssey to collate all the necessary media for the ReCollect64 Collectors App, I have noted plenty of variations of games that I previously had no idea existed, including one of my biggest discoveries, the previously uncatalogued SCN Scandinavian viariant of Rampage World Tour.

In recent weeks I have fixed, added, and removed plenty of variants, but having reached "M" in my database, I noted one game variant that was particularly hard to find. Monaco Grand Prix was released in the USA, Canada, and also the much more sparse MSA (Mexico/South America) region. Though there is only one known ROM version of the NTSC cartridge, and they're all coded NUS-NMGE-USA, the outer packaging varies a little regionally, so I sought to document each one individually for completeness.

I first heard about a "Spanish-backed" variant of the game back in 2023; however, this illusive version proved extremely difficult to locate media for, and so I threw it over to the one person I knew could assist me in the hunt, my good friend JosetteTrinkets, who asked whether I had some other examples of Spanish-backed variants.

Having shared a bunch of previously found images as examples (below), we noted a few things. Firstly, each of these titles are Ubisoft published games (then known as Ubi Soft, no, it's not a Mandela Effect). Secondly, most Ubisoft-published game had a Brazilian release through Gradiente, except for a handful, and that handful just so happened to be the ones that we were finding "Spanish-backed". Last of all, we noted that Tonic Trouble is the only game on that list that hasn't had a Gradiente release, nor has it shown up as a Spanish-backed variant yet!

Follwoing this, one individual image was located for Monaco Grand Prix. The quality is exceptionally low, though most obscure variant images are, so this will have to do for now. If anyone can assist in the scanning and sharing of better quality media for these variants, please feel free to message me at any time because preservation is critical for these obscure versions, as the soft, frail cardboard containers won't last forever.

(Thanks to JosetteTrinkets' unique ability to seek-out incredibly niche things for the Nintendo 64, we finally have an image to go alongside an otherwise rare/unseen game variant!)

The Ubisoft Anomaly

Double-checking my data, Ubisoft released 11 individual games on the Nintendo 64 between 1997 and 2001, with a couple of regional variants within that shortlist. These titles were All Star Tennis (Yannik Noah All Star Tennis), Batman Beyond (Batman of the Future), Buck Bumble, Donald Duck (Goin' Quackers/Quack Attack), F1 Pole Position (Human Grand Prix), F1 Racing Championship, Monaco Grand Prix/Monaco Racing Simulation 2/Racing Simulation 2, Rayman 2: The Great Escape, Rocket: Robot on Wheels, S.C.A.R.S. and Tonic Trouble.

Of these 11 games, just 7 were released in Brazil through Gradiente (All Star Tennis, Batman, Buck Bumble, Donald Duck, F1 Pole Position, F1 Racing Challenge (note: this is the only NTSC version of the ROM and the only release outside of Europe), and Rayman 2: The Great Escape).

Three of the remaining four - Monaco, Rocket, and SCARS, though unreleased in Brazil, did get Spanish variants as mentioned above, but Tonic Trouble remains notably absent from this line-up, so does that theoretically mean that there is a Spanish-backed variant of Tonic Trouble out there waiting to be catalogued?

Interestingly, most of the Spanish-backed variants located appear to be stickers applied to the back of the NTSC USA release boxes in order to make them regionally appealing to people who speak Spanish (including Argentina, Colombia, and Mexico). Due to high lexical similarities, Spanish is often easily understood by Portuguese-speaking countries as well, which means that Nintendo 64 fans in Brazil could quite easily adopt these versions to add to their collections because they could also read the boxes.

(Tonic Trouble released in Europe, USA and Canada as a variant which had a bonus collectible phone card inside. Monaco Grand Prix was also released in Canada with a similar bonus collectible phone card offer).

Other Oddities

One last thing I wanted to touch on are other regional variants that had similar Spanish backs or are regionally exclusive within Brazil/MSA regions. Fighters Destiny, published by Imagineer, oddly, has a variant that is USA coded but has a Spanish-language version box back. The front cover and cartridge are USA versions, but the back is quite unique.

As mentioned above, the Brazilian Gradiente version of F1 Racing Championship is another unusual example, as it is the only NTSC release of the otherwise PAL-exclusive F1 Racing Championship game. Why this game was released in Brazil must have been due to licensing deals between Nintendo/Ubisoft and Gradiente, their Brazilian distribution partners for the Nintendo 64's games and consoles.

The final oddity I wish to present is that of the MSA variant of WCW Backstage Assault, which comes with a Spanish back and a regionally exclusive grey cartridge. The NTSC USA release came on a blue cartridge; no grey variant was ever released for it in the USA region, and the game never made it to European shores, so no PAL versions, which would be typically grey, exist either.
