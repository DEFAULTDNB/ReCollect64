Two New JPN Translations WIP

Native Tongues

European and American players have always had one glaring issue when it comes to the 86 Japanese-exclusive titles on the Nintendo 64. The majority of these Japan-only titles contain just one language, Japanese, so it stands to reason that without going to extreme lengths to learn Japanese, photograph and translate each screen, or in more recent years, setting up a mobile device to watch the screen and provide live AI translation, there has been a bit of an impasse to enjoying these games in their entirety.

Enter Roboverse Translations, a brand new account on Bluesky who have already posted about translating not one, but two titles for the Nintend 64. Banpresto's Super Robot Spirits (1998) and Treasure's Bakuretsu Muteki Bangaioh (1999) translations are almost complete according to their latest posts, which is exciting news for players such as myself, who aim to play every N64 game in existence but have been holding out for such projects to come to completion on these foreign language titles.

Translation Team

Having recently reviewed Dezaemon 3D thanks to the wonderful English language patch by Zoinkity and Whowasphone404, I fully appreciate the benefits of these projects and the depths these teams need to go to to make these translations both make sense and flow within the existing game layout.

I have been closely monitoring the progress of the Yakochu II Satsujin Koro translation project by Whowasphone404 (DarkJapan404) for the past 5 years, and with new titles getting full translations, this excites me to see renewed interest in getting these gems translated for all to enjoy!

If you can help contribute or want to support Roboverse Translations, then please take a look at their Patreon link, sub to their channels, or contact them directly on their social media page or Discord server.
