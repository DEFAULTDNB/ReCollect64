BMHero Recomp Released

Da Bomb

Back in March, RevoSucks released Bomberman 64: Recompiled, which Bomberman fans adored. Today, RevoSucks has dropped another huge bombshell, because Bomberman Hero: Recompiled is now also ready to play on your PC/Mac/Linux/Steam Deck!

Based on the Bombhackers decompilation, this source port also utilises the N64: Recompiled codebase to deliver high framerate support, ultrawide screen settings, modern controller mapping, mod and texture support, near-instant load times, and more!

Divisive Dynamite

To enjoy the first release of recompilation, all you need to do is supply a legally obtained Bomberman Hero (U) *.Z64 ROM, with the MD5: EF2453BFF7AD0C4BFA9AB0BD6324EBF3, drag and drop it into the folder and run the executable (the current release, 0.6.3, oddly has the Banjo-Kazooie icon).

Bomberman Hero famously diverged from its top-down strategy gaming with multiplayer to focus on single-player platforming. The game released in 1998 across all three regions and met a mixed reception with critics, who outlined the lack of multiplayer and the uninspired storyline as the main issues, though praise came for its banging high-quality soundtrack and experimental gameplay. Notably, regardless of its 66% aggregated review totals, the game did go on to re-release as a Players' Choice/Million Seller title within its lifetime on the Nintendo 64.

As mentioned previously, RevoSucks has a range of forked decompilation projects within their own GitHub repositories, including Pokemon Stadium 1 and 2, so we could see some more recompilation goodness as and when the relative decompilation projects bare fruit.

Please check the front page of the site for all the latest links to the decompilation and recompilation projects, as well as any console ports that are publicly available! Enjoy!
