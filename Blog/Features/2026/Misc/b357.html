Rare Rhythms

With the release of the eagerly awaited Rare edition Super Pocket console just the other day, some people have noticed that the version of Banjo-Kazooie that's included is a little more than expected.

Having recently confirmed that the included game was indeed a port based on Rare's own 29-year-old source code and has nothing to do with the recent decompilations, the game includes improvements and additions such as D-pad control as opposed to analogue stick, 60FPS, and a new dynamic camera system.

The revitalised game also includes a secret jukebox found in the Stop 'n' Swap area, yet another cut feature of the N64 original. Within that selection of music comes not one but 21 tracks that were either used in early builds, such as the E3 demo, or went entirely unused. These were apparently scrubbed from the retail N64 ROM to save space, hence why we haven't found them until now.

These 21 tracks are stunning remnants of developmental ideas lost and the perfect example of preservation at its finest. Some tracks hark back to BK's prototype roots, Project Dream, and others have that Grant Kirkhope quality and charm that only he could produce.

If you would like to check out all of the tracks found on the Evercade Music player, which are all currently being uploaded, then please check out TSR Stormed's socials and their YouTube channel for heaps more glorious Banjo-Kazooie content from the Banjo-Kazooie series speedrunner and glitch hunter.

The Banjo-Kazooie/Banjo-Tooie double pack comes out soon, so assuming that will share parity in features, I can't wait to see what additional content and features may have been added to the Tooie port!
