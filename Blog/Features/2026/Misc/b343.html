Pokemon Stadium Recomp

Stadium Events

Another day, another recompilation project coming to life. This time enterprise IoT data engineer Matt Stanley, with the assistance of an AI agent, has got Pokemon Stadium working as an executable for PC under the nautical pseudonym "SS Anne". It has to be stated that this project has had no involvement from the Harbour Masters.

With the current release standing at 0.4.2 beta, the N64Recomp toolchain-powered recompilation features high frame rates and modern controller binding, as per usual, but astonishingly it also brings 4-player multiplayer and Transfer Pak support for importing your own Game Boy ROMs so that you can battle on the big screen and trade your monsters.

To run SS Anne, you will need to supply your own legally obtained Pokemon Stadium US v1.0 *.Z64 ROR with the MD5: ED1378BC12115F71209A77844965BA50. Rev A (V1.1) and the European or Japanese ROMs will not work.

(Just as in 1999, you can import Red, Blue, or Yellow ROMs into this recomp to battle all 151 original Pokemon with up to 4 players)

AI-Aided

Once again, Claude is listed as a collaborator on an N64 recompilation. With this becoming a regular occurence we might begin to assume that there is perhaps some merit to using AI to at least assist in the process. Time will tell if it's a worthwhile venture as there is considerable general disdain for the use of AI in any media.

Just how much work Claude did on the project is debatable, but MStan has at least been immediately transparent in stating that one was used during the process. To date Castlevania: Legacy of Darkness, Paper Mario: Recut, and now Pokemon Stadium ("SS Anne") have all used AI and have all been released within the space of two weeks.

With regular updates coming for this recomp, I hope that the issues with jittery sound can be worked out, as well as the codebase advancing enough to support mods and perhaps netplay, if it can be worked with after AI has had its grubby fingers running through the code.
