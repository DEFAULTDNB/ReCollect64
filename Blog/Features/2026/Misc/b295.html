Ocarina of Sublime

I saw something over at The Dreamcast Junk Yard this evening that absolutely knocked my socks off, so I had to echo the news here on ReCollect64.

Essentially, an anonymous developer, no, not the usual suspects, has allowed The Sega Guru to release a tantalising preview video of The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time Master Quest port for the Sega Dreamcast!

With the build running extremely well already bar a few notable slowdowns and some graphical glitches here and there, it's worth noting that this is an extremely early private version, but it will be improved over time. Check out the details below:

 

Notes:

KOS2.0 SDL[DREAMHAL]+GLDC]FAST Now using GLDC https://gitlab.com/simulant/GLdc 3D hardware via OpenGL and 2D video framebuffer PVR DMA FLIP Full KallistiOS and SDL integration UPGRADED blitspeed using moops dreamhal sh4 asm memcpy and memset

About Time

The Ocarina of Time decompilation was completed to a usable level in 2021, and since then we have seen a plethora of ports, including Rinnegatamante's Vita port and HM64's rather magnificent Shipwrite, AKA, Ship of Harkinian, for PC, Mac, Wii U and Nintendo Switch.

While a Dreamcast port was inevitable, it's only now that the tools for porting have matured to a level that it is feasible. But it begs the question: why isn't it being handled by the renowned current DC porting dream team? Well, we don't currently know who is behind the port, so it could either be a new challenger willing to bolster the scene with some new, wonderful DC ports of our decompiled 64-bit classics, or it could in fact be smoke and mirrors, with no one willing to claim credit for it just yet, waiting to see if Nintendo throws a DCMA at it or not.

Regardless, Dreamcast ports are more than welcome, and I am very excited to see this one progress. I will keep this updated when it hopefully releases.
