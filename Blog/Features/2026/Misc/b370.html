Sega Rally N64 Port WIP

Easy Right into Hard Port!

Snake, lead developer of the ongoing Tomb Raider N64 port, has recently previewed some early footage of his next project, a Sega Rally Championship port for the Nintendo 64. The footage appears to demonstrate the PC version of the game running natively on the Nintendo 64 hardware. Even in this early state, it is only missing a few effects, though it is running considerably slower than it should.

Not much more is known, but the 1997 PC release of the 1995 arcade racer has been used; elements of the UI are being reworked for the N64 iteration to remove the "Press Enter" buttons, for example, and Snake is aiming for 30 FPS performance and increased draw distance before any form of public release sees the light of day.

Rings of Saturn

Sega Rally Championship famously released on the Sega Saturn in 1995, but Sega's fifth-generation and 32-bit system hasn't seen as much love in recent years as the Nintendo 64 scene is currently seeing. That said, there are several Sega Saturn games being decompiled, including Panzer Dragoon, and several N64 developers are eager to flex their skills in porting these titles to the N64.

Another user, Superdisk, showcased an early recompiler for Sega Saturn games to run on Nintendo 64 last month, though I did not write an article for it, as there wasn't much meat on the bones to share. The footage shown was merely a 2D-based conversion; however, it demonstrates serious promise should it progress.
