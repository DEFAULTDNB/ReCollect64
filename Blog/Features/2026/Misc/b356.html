Perfect Dark VR Released

Perfect Reality

As many of you may or may not know, as well as being a huge N64 nut, I'm also a massive fan of light gun shooting games and VR gaming. When these combine, I'm in my element, and with today's release of the Perfect Dark VR mod for PCVR and Meta Quest devices, I have to admit that I'm thoroughly impressed with just how brilliantly Joanna Dark's sci-fi alien invasion adventure has translated into a virtual reality experience.

Built upon the Perfect Dark decompilation effort, this work-in-progress PCVR and standalone Android headset port is something quite spectacular, bringing full 6DOF (Six Degrees of Freedom) controls and intuitive 1:1 gun handling to the 1.0 NTSC-U version of the Y2K 64-bit title.

The Perfect Dark VR mod is now released! You can download it and play it now on PCVR or Meta Quest. This is the first beta release, which got a lot of improvements since I streamed this earlier last month. Enjoy! youtu.be/wkQiyeYn7QU

How to play:

You must already have a Perfect Dark ROM to run the game. Only the NTSC version works with the VR build.

On PCVR

Put your Perfect Dark NTSC ROM named pd.ntsc-final.z64 into the "data" folder.

On Meta Quest Standalone

Put your Perfect Dark NTSC ROM named pd.ntsc-final.z64 into "Android/data/com.perfectdark.port/files/data".

Virtual Sanity

As a beta, this is expected to contain bugs here and there, but overall the experience should be more than serviceable. The mod has been in active development for at least several months, and three weeks ago, Graslu00 got his hands on an early build and streamed an epic 5-hour-long session of the game in action. The stream notably played through the entire campaign with only a handful of interesting bugs, gave us a weapons showcase, and then smashed some bot-fuelled challenges.

I shall be setting this up and exploring a full play through this week, and hopefully giving it an official ReCollect64 review as soon as possible. If you want to give it a go, follow the links below and supply a legally obtained NTSC U V1.1 ROM with the MD5: E03B088B6AC9E0080440EFED07C1E40F (1.1 is recommended, though 1.0 will work with an MD5 of 7F4171B0C8D17815BE37913F535E4E93). Don't forget that you can even simply back up your own cartridges with N64-SwapDumper and a flash cart.
