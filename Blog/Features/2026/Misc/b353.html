SBK2 Recomp Released

Cold as Ice

Following the recent news that Snowboard Kids 2 had been 100% decompiled by CDLewis, we now have news that the first releases of the recompilation have been made public. Version 1.05, at the time of writing, is only fully compatible with the North American ROM and features all the things you have come to know and love of modern Nintendo 64 recompilation projects that utilise N64: Recompiled and the RT64 renderer as a base.

Unlike his decompilations, which controversially use Claude AI as an aide, this recompilation only lists CDLewis and Dario (of RT64 fame) as contributors, so you can rest assured that this is a high-quality human-only production.

Recomp Moguls

Notably, the original Snowboard Kids game is also listed as being decompiled by CDLewis, JamesLewis, and Claude. Though the progress sits at around 91% for data and just 16% for code, the progress made is always welcomed, as the more games we have verifiably 1:1 decompiled, the more recompilations we will eventually get our grubby mits on. There is also a little movement on the Gex 3D decompilation, though that curently stands at extremely low levels of completion.

The use of AI for decompilation appears to be better accepted than the usage for recompilations because the output is either complete or incomplete, 1:1 matching or not. When it comes to recompilations and AI, sure, there are barriers to entry that can be quickly overcome, but fine-tuning and adjusting the code to cooperate is far too nuanced for AI to fully understand currently, and the resulting code is usually far less than perfect and virtually impossible to maintain. Thankfully, this recomp does not use AI.
