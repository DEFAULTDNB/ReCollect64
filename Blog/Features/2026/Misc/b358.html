Retro Shelf 64 Released

Covered in Class

When it comes to flash carts, they're quite utilitarian. Primarily, they house and play your ROMs with the ability to apply patches, cheats, and even emulators for other systems, but rarely do they look like anything more than a simple list of games and a wall of text. They work, and that's what matters most. Retro Shelf 64 aims to remix this ethos by adding a vibrant cover art selection to your collection, and as a visual person myself, I'm thoroughly impressed with what I've seen so far.

Imagine you have your physical media all lined up on a shelf. Box-by-box or cartridge-by-cartridge, your collection would look superb. What Retro Shelf 64 has done is digitised the way you display your media and brought us a SummerCart64-compatible loader that emulates this display style and looks and sounds absolutely incredible, with sound clips and a graphical user interface that makes every ROM pop with its individual box, spine or cart art.

What is Retro Shelf 64?

Retro Shelf 64 is a native Nintendo 64 box art launcher for the SummerCart64. Instead of scrolling through folders full of filenames, you browse a shelf of 3D game boxes, cartridge spines, and carts on your console. Every supported game includes its own music, uses real cartridge saves, and can launch 64DD titles. Point Retro Shelf at your ROM library, and it builds the shelf automatically.

What do I need?

You'll need:

+ A SummerCart64

+ A microSD card

+ Your own Nintendo 64 ROMs

It runs on original Nintendo 64 hardware and the Analogue 3D, with the experience tuned specifically for the Analogue 3D.

Does it work on other flash carts?

Probably not. Retro Shelf 64 was built specifically for the SummerCart64 and relies on its hardware for fast loading, save handling, and 64DD support. EverDrive and other flash carts don't provide the features it depends on.

Do you provide games or ROMs?

No. Retro Shelf 64 includes only the launcher. You supply your own ROMs from cartridges you own. I don't distribute games, and Patreon never hosts or delivers ROMs.

How does it find my games?

Copy your ROMs anywhere on your SD card—/N64, /Nintendo 64, region folders, alphabet folders, or any other layout. Retro Shelf scans the card and matches games by filename. Standard No-Intro filenames produce the best results. The installation guide covers the process in detail.

Which games appear on the shelf?

The shelf currently includes 58 hand-picked Nintendo 64 games, from major releases to cult favorites, and that number grows over time. Every title appears on the shelf, but only games you own launch.

Can I request games?

Yes. Special Agent and 00 Agent members can vote on new games and request additions. Those votes help decide which titles I add next.

How do saves work?

Retro Shelf uses the same EEPROM, SRAM, and FlashRAM save files as the standard SummerCart64 menu. Saves remain completely compatible in both directions. Start a game in the stock menu, switch to Retro Shelf later, and pick up exactly where you left off.

What about 64DD and SimCity 64?

Retro Shelf launches real 64DD disk images. It also supports the English fan translation of SimCity 64. You provide the required 64DD IPL ROM and your own disk image, and Retro Shelf handles the launch.

Do PAL and Japanese ROMs work?

Usually. Retro Shelf matches games by filename, so ROMs from other regions still appear on the correct shelf tile. The artwork uses North American box art, and a few games expect a specific version, such as English fan translations.

Does it replace the SummerCart64 menu?

No. Retro Shelf boots automatically when you power on your console, but the standard N64FlashcartMenu stays one tile away for settings, save management, and 64DD tools unless you choose the optional replacement install. You always keep access to both.

Is it safe?

Yes. Retro Shelf scans your SD card but doesn't move or modify your ROMs. It doesn't change your SummerCart64 firmware, and it saves games through the same system as the stock menu. If a game isn't supported, it simply appears on the shelf without launching.

How much does it cost?

+ Agent ($3/month): Retro Shelf 64 launcher and access to the patron Discord.

+ Special Agent ($7/month): Everything in Agent, plus day-one builds and voting on games and features.

+ 00 Agent ($11/month): Everything above, plus a private Discord channel and extra voting weight.

You can also follow the Patreon for free to read public development updates. The launcher itself is available only to paid members.

Pay-Walled Pleasure

Now, there's a slight kicker. In order to enjoy this lusious creation, you currently need to be a member of Retro Shelf 64's Patreon. For a minimum of £3.50 a month you can gain access to the otherwise inaccessible front-end and the accompanying Discord server, for updates and technical assistance. There are higher tiers available that give you a little input into the dev's next iterations and enhance game compatibility for fringe-case ROMs/ROM hacks that won't load properly, as well as a "top secret" area to access at the £8.50-a-month top level.

While £3.50 a month isn't a huge amount, and supporting developers is certainly the noble thing to do, I can't help but feel that a lot of people will miss out on using this, simply because they do not want to pay for it. There's a fine line, but perhaps there should be a basic free tier or demo release at very least for users to try out and experiment with. Personally I will be watching this project mature and probably buying into at least one month in order to check it out and enjoy it for myself. If you don't want to pay for this glitzy upgrade, you can always use the more basic built-in SC64 box art feature, using the Flashcart menu metadata pack.

EverDrive 64 users also might not be able to enjoy this launcher because it's been strictly built around SummerCart64's open-source infrastructure. Having said that, ED64 users (up to V3.0) and ED64 clones (V2.5) previously had access to ALT64 and ALTRA64 for adding box art to their flash carts. X series owners currently have no way of adding box art, but the N64FlashcartMenu is still in the process of being worked on for ED64 hardware compatibility too.
