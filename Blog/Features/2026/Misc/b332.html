DK64 Joins NSO

So They're Finally Here

You wouldn't believe that the last two N64 games released on NSO were back in December of 2025, but finally, six months later, Nintendo of America has dropped the ultimate bombshell (read: banana) in announcing that Donkey Kong 64 is at last landing on the Nintendo Switch Online Expansion Pack service!

As the 45th game released, it's taken four and a half years for Rare's three-dimensional jungle jaunt to make it to the system, and on June 4th, players young and old will get to replay the game that has been otherwise unplayable on modern platforms, at least since it last launched on Wii U in 2015. The drip-feed continues.

Absolutely Bananas

Back in July last year, internet sleuths deduced that Nintendo had several undisclosed titles ready to go for the NSO service, including Forsaken, Glover, Donkey Kong 64, and most interestingly Super Smash Bros. With the release of DK64, all that is left to be released from that bout of data mining is SSB, because Glover has since been released digitally in February of 2025 through QUbyte Classics and physically via Limited Run Games.

Will we see Super Smash Bros. on the Switch any time soon? Who knows. Nintendo seems content to spread out the release of their games across the years and has so far launched 45 games in just under 5 years (55 months), making for an average of 0.81 games per month since the Expansion Pack service began in October 2021.

The question remains: why not just steadily release one game every 6 weeks instead of this seemingly sporadic and random release schedule? Perhaps underlying licensing and IP-related issues need time to resolve or dissipate.
