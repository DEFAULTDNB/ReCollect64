N64 Video Player Released

Movies, Games & Videos

Did you know that the Nintendo 64 recently had a video player application released? Nor did I, until I stumbled upon the GitHub repository of Lewilou22, a developer who has put together a clever method of playing movies and TV shows from the comfort of their Nintendo 64 thanks to the incredible functionality of the open source Libdragon SDK.

The process generates an M1V file, an MPEG-1 video file that exclusively contains video data using lossy compression, and a WAV64 file, which is essentially the audio track. Modern container formats such as MP4 and MKV contain both the video and audio tracks, sometimes with multiple audio tracks for different languages, etc. Sadly the N64 video player app doesn't, and likely won't, support these modern formats.

Conversion to Cartridge

With the latest release's GUI tools (which requires FFMPEG installed) the possibilities are utterly brilliant, though ultimately extremely limited. From modern blockbusters to entire seasons of your favourite shows, you could potentially cram as much as your blank flashable clone carts can fit, or you can simply bang the files onto the root of your EverDrive 64, SummerCart64 or similar flash carts.

According to reports, this player works well on Everdrive 2.5, has some sound issues on the Everdrive X7 (use the D-pad to toggle audio on), can occasionally black screen on the SummerCart64, runs well with either 20 FPS or 30 FPS conversion, but has a slightly laggy menu. It's nice to see alternative homebrew still coming to the system, regardless of the technical limitations regarding output resolution and video compression.
