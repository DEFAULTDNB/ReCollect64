Presto! Coming 2028

Indulging Our Nostalgia

Circus Cat Games might not (yet) be a name you're familiar with because they only formed in January, 2026, but Banjo-Kazooie ROM hacker extraordinaire Markus Kurko is certainly a celebrity within the N64 scene. This evening, he demonstrated a 12-minute video of the upcoming Nintendo 64-inspired, Unity-based, modern-platform-destined title in action over on his YouTube channel.

While the game is sadly not set for release on the Nintendo 64 itself, the gameplay plainly borrows from the best of the N64's iconic platformers, including definite notes of Hasbro/Interactive Studio's Glover (1998) blended with Rare's banana-fuelled 1999 smash-hit Donkey Kong 64. It will be interesting to watch this title develop over the next 2 years, as I think it already has an abundance of potential and absolutely nails the N64 aesthetic.

 

Presto! is an N64-style collectathon made by Circus Cat Studios that combines classic 3D platforming gameplay with the physics-based Rolling Globe that allows Presto to roll around, bounce, and take-off on ramps at high speeds!

On a Roll

Presto! places a heavy emphasis on explorative locomotion and collecting boatloads of items, as well as solving increasingly taxing puzzles. My first impressions of this were that the character almost perfectly emulates Lanky Kong's style of movement with the two arms bounding around the floor, while the ball mechanics definitely remind me of Glover's physics-based interactivity and ingenuity.

With a release date slated for 2028, Presto! will be available on Steam and modern console platforms (hopefully physically), though there has been no suggestion of an N64 release. While this is to be expected given the current scope of the game, it is still somewhat disappointing given that the N64 would be the perfect platform for this circus-themed platformer.

Regardless, there is a playable demo available right now for anyone who utilises Steam!
