Pokemon Snap 3DS WIP

Creature Comforts

What if I told you that the Nintendo 64's original on-rails point-and-snap simulator, Pokemon Snap, was being diligently recreated on the Nintendo 3DS? Well, having watched the progress unfold on Manuel Rodríguez Matesanz's X/Twitter account (linked below), I can tell you that this Unity-based recreation is already looking incredible, with assets, animations and paths taken directly from the 1999 N64 masterpiece.

Since mid-to-early April, Manuel has been hard at work posting updates on his work and cleverly solving any issues he's encountering along the way. The most recent updates suggest that basic in-game mechanics are working (including snapshot evaluation); the menu/UI transitions, sounds and bottom screen functions are also implemented; paths and models are being imported; and, most importantly, that image capture is now working within the confines of storing 64 images within the 3DS's meagre 1 MB save capacity.

Portable Photographers

The developer still has a long way to go before the first playable betas hit our handhelds, but the plans are to recreate the first beach course in its entirety, followed by the rest of the game, providing there is interest and support. Having seen the project running on emulators as well as real hardware, I can say for certain that there will be a great deal of interest!

Manuel stated that "The playable betas will be downloadable from Whack a Hack, Eevee Expo and PokeCommunity forums as always. :)" which are all great forums to showcase this work once it's in a playable state. I will be avidly watching this progress and will share updates here once they're ready.
