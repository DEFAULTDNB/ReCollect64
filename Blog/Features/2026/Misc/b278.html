Project Reality

Building on the cumulative Perfect Dark decompilation efforts, Flat2VR has today shared that developer and modder Alex LeTux has managed to recompile Rare's stealth action shooter into a full-on, living, breathing virtual reality experience which appears to work on Android-powered headsets such as the Meta Quest 3.

Perfect Potential

With this immersive concept proving to be so tantalising for fans of the game, it begs the question: "Can more PC-portable decomps be built into VR games?" This gets me thinking that games such as Mario Kart 64, Super Mario 64, Star Fox 64, and the Zelda PC ports, could at the very least take advantage of stereoscopic 3D rather than immersive 3D.

First-person shooters such as GoldenEye 007, Duke Nukem 64, Doom 64, Shadowgate, Turok 3, and even Virtual Pool (all of which have in-progress decompilations in the works) could hugely benefit from being given an additional dimension and become truly transformative experiences.
