SM64 DS Port With Multiplayer

Released by Game Of Tobi ~ Jun 16, 2026
SM64 DS Port + Multiplayer

Super Mario Makers

Having already wowed us with N64 game source ports to devices such as the Apple Watch, Game of Tobi has once again broken new ground by improving existing Nintendo DS ports of the Super Mario 64 decompilation to not only run better, but also now include some new and entertaining features.

Using the work of Hydr8gon's SM64 DSi port as a base, Game of Tobi began by optimising the code to load assets dynamically into memory and allowing the game to run on just 4 MB as opposed to the 16 MB of memory required by the previous DSi port. The result is much faster loading, mitigated crashes, and serviceable music and sound effects that don't sound incredibly stuttery.

King of the Kingdom

In addition to optimisation, Game of Tobi has also added another option to the start menu and has brought local multiplayer into the mix. This means that two DS's with two flashcarts and two copies of this homebrew can play together, running around the Mushroom Kingdom in perfect sync.

With options to recolour your character or change the model altogether, you can either team up to complete tasks and collect stars, or, for fun, play a massive game of hide and seek around any of the levels, including the hub world, around the entire game, with no virtually limits!

It's fantastic to see the 64-bit platformer being ported to more platforms that were often thought impossible, or would require a complete demake. It's even more impressive to see these ports gaining modern features and modes too. Who knows where we will see SM64 pop up again next, as it turns 30 this month?
