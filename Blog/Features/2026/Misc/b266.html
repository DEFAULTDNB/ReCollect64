SM64 DC Port Updated

It's Me, Again!

Almost 16 months after the initial news that Jnmartin84 and Co's Super Mario 64 Dreamcast port was underway, the team are back with a nice new update that addresses all the outstanding issues thanks to the incredible progress made and features added to their ports of Mario Kart 64 and, most recently, Star Fox 64.

Teased back in December, Falco Girgis explained that the team have harnessed a lot of gains to bring across the most cohesive and feature-rich port possible. They're also utilising an update to the original 2020 Dreamcast port of SM64 by mrneo240, which addresses the audio-visual issues that were present and adds VMU saving as well as 480p support. Dreamcast afficionados will finally get to experience a virtually flawless SM64 native port for their beloved Sega console, thanks to the tireless work of this astonishing team.

Sega Style

If you would like to play any of these ports on your original Sega Dreamcast hardware, then please check out the CDI building information that have been provided to make these ports easily buildable with just a correctly MD5 hashed ROM and the relevant link for the game you wish to build.

The Doom 64 instructions are HERE, the Star Fox 64 build guide is HERE, the Mario Kart 64 instructions are HERE, and the Super Mario 64 instructions are located HERE. Please follow the instructions to the letter, and also note that these are not made for use on emulators; they're designed to be enjoyed on real hardware, with or without an ODE, etc.

While checking Jnmartin84's GitHub repo, it's interesting to see that Harvest Moon 64 has been added alongside the already known Duke Nukem: Zero Hour decompilation, which was used in SonicDcer's WIP recompilation for PC.
