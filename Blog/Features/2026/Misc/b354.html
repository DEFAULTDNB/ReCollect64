64 Operator Incoming

Back Up, BACK UP!

Over the last few days, it has been incredible to see so many people celebrate the N64's 30th anniversary. Epilogue, the company behind their operator hardware, recently dropped the bombshell that their next endeavour is in fact Nintendo 64-related, and it could be quite a nice tool for N64 collectors to get to grips with.

Notably, the GB operator uses mGBA and the SNES operator uses bSNES for playback when connected. What's more, both offer in-game achievements and can facilitate an entry point to homebrew development too. It will be interesting to see what Epilogue uses to emulate the Nintendo 64 on PC, because the stakes are much higher when it comes to the hardware needed for decent N64 emulation on PC. I wonder if they will partner with Gopher 64 or Simple64, rather than ARES, because while the latter is the best out there, it's a multi-machine emulator and not dedicated to just N64 gaming.

Happy 30th anniversary, Nintendo 64! And since it's not nice to show up to a party empty-handed, we brought you a gift.

Computer Cart Slot

Plugging your carts into your PC is not something everyone knows about. I purchased BennVenn's awesome JoeyN64 a while back with the sole intention of verifying my purchased carts so that I know I've got what I've paid for. Costing £29 roughly, I was able to plug in a cart and drag and drop the ROM and save data to my PC with ease. Nothing needed to be installed, and it takes seconds to verify the ROM. The cheaper RPi-based dumpers also do a similar job at just £12. With these you can also rewrite clone carts too, which is a nice bonus.

The Epilogue 64 Operator is tracking to cost around the $60-$70 mark, which is around the £45-55 mark, currency conversion and tax/duty forgiving, which makes it considerably more, but will it be worth it? If you're not interested in the accompanying software, then probably not. I for one look forward to seeing what it brings to the N64 scene because integrity in preservation is king, especially when backing up your personal collection and verifying your purchases.
