Pandemonium WIP

Unsheath Thy Sword

Almost a year ago, I wrote about an upcoming game that was in development called Pickle 64. BoxingBruin was originally responsible for that tantalising demo, and during the recent repair-themed Game Jam 6 brew contest, he and his Game Jam partner HelloNewman had embarked on a new project dubbed Pandemonium, which is best described as "a souls-like boss encounter with its own unique story and cinematic".

The best news is that as of four days ago, Pandemonium is available to try out in a far more refined and stable form, with the latest 0.65 release available on the creator's Github repository. As far as I know, Pickle 64 remains in active development alongside this project, and I'm very much looking forward to seeing what happens with that too.

The idea was to create a polished boss fight in the style of Elden Ring, a game that puts epic boss battles centre stage. Pandemonium is a love letter to this genre and an absolutely stunning technical demonstration of what is possible on the Nintendo 64 regardless of the technical constraints.

Playable Pandemonium

Thanks to Tiny 3D, Libdragon and Pyrite 64, the "FROMSCRATCH" team (originally known as "Team Zero Cool") has crafted something special. With amazing texture work, animations, cutscenes, and special effects, the game has an incredible weight to it and an undeniably well observed gothic style that oozes FromSoftware's direction and aesthetic, albeit with a gorgeously retro 64-bit twist.

You can play it right now on the Ares emulator or on real hardware, including the Analogue 3D via flashcart. PAL and NTSC systems are supported, and it features blood particle effects, stamina bars, Z-targeting and even more cinematic dialogue at the end.

What are you waiting for? Head over there now and get enjoying it. I highly receommend it!
