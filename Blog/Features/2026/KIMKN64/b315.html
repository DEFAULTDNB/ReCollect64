Killer Kombat

Did you know that the original Killer Instinct (1994) which was released on the SNES was also meant to be ported and improved as a showcase for the then "Ultra 64"-named hardware? Sadly, that never happened; instead Rare opted to port a cut-down version of Killer Instinct 2 (1996) to the system under the guise of Killer Instinct Gold (1996).

A developer known as N64GameBuilder has been busy ripping, porting and developing with the Libdragon SDK to bring us not only a POC recreation of the original arcade Killer Instinct, but also, as a bonus and another proof of concept, he has also begun pivoting this idea into making a recreation of the Mortal Kombat arcade game ready for the 64-bit hardware.

While the Killer Instinct 64 project was seemingly halted and attention refocused on the Mortal Kombat project, it's still incredible to see and experience in this early state. With the ongoing updates and tooling advances, I hope that they pick it back up and bring KI64 to completion one day; the recent "KI64: Hidden Talent in the Shadows" video on Patreon suggests that this might be the case.

Below is the mocked-up boxart, a brief gameplay video and a quick explanation of each of the ports. Please bear in mind that these are essentially very early yet incredibly impressive demonstrations, which the author is still sporadically working on, according to his YouTube videos, but you can download and play these demos today.

 

DISCLAIMER: This is an abandoned prototype and is NOT a full game. It is highly incomplete and was released as a historical curiosity.

This build was the initial test for a Killer Instinct port on the N64 before the project was pivoted to Mortal Kombat.

Features:

FMV viewer for 7 characters.

Unlockable Eyedol.

A functional 2-player versus mode with a static stage.

Limited animation sets for Thunder (~15), Combo (7), and Orchid (7).

High-quality audio.

 

What's up, team! The day has arrived. Today I present to you Mortal Kombat for the Nintendo 64.

After countless hours of work, I finally have something playable... more or less.

Just a heads-up: this ROM is run with the Ares emulator. It hasn't been tested with any flashcards, so if you try it, it's at your own risk. It's also likely not worth the effort to transfer the file to a flashcard.

Download at your own risk (and enjoy the glitches)!

Artistic Instinct

Since these concepts were last shown, N64GameBuilder has been busy in the background beavering away at crafting tools that enable better output and even preservation towards these titles. "Nabila 64", which is an HTML-based 4-step preservation tool for the Killer Instinct coin-op hard drive image, extracts, sorts and visualises data ready for tooling and should be released around May 6th, 2026.

Another such tool named "N64 ATLAS GENERATOR PRO" is designed to take the effort out of sprite manipulation and converts assets directly into memory-optimised "atlases" which can then be imported ready for use by Libdragons libraries.

The latest updates to each game are available through the Patreon link below, and slightly a updated ROM for the Killer Instinct 64 demo, including the lava bridge level is also available there exclusively.

The YouTube channel linked below also offers a fantastic insight as to the development of various features for each game and the supporting toolset that has been crafted, as well as optimisation and special effects tricks and anecdotes as to how tricky-to-code obstacles and restrictions have been overcome. I highly recommend checking it all out in full!
