N64-era Zelda Teased for 2026 ~ Jun 9, 2026

Ocarina of Sublime

For the longest time, fans of the Zelda series have been clamouring for a remake of Nintendo's 1998 The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time for modern consoles. Sure, the 3DS was the last console to see a remake with The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time 3D, but that was back in 2011, and, astonishingly, the 15-year period of time between 2011 and today (2026) is longer than the 13-year period of time that went between the original release (1998) and the 2011 remake.

Today, Nintendo held their latest Nintendo Direct and finally gave the fans something to think about by displaying a few glorious seconds of what looks to be an absolutely dazzling remake of the classic 3D, hylian-adventuring, dungeon-exploring, action RPG. Though the clip is barely 97 seconds in length, the quality of textures, models and animation already gives us some clues as to the aesthetics of the game, and the first look at child Link, which pulls the design more in line with that of Breath of the Wild (2017) and Tears of the Kingdom (2023), albeit far more realistic.

Remaking History

Ocarina of Time is widely regarded as the best game ever made, having scored the highest score possible (an incredible 99/100) over the most number of reviews since its initial release in 1998. Notably, the 3DS remake's score only dipped slightly lower at 94%, so it leads me to wonder: will the latest iteration score lower still?

Having seen the remake of Star Fox 64 that is coming out on 25th June (with a free demo available too), I would have to say that Nintendo aren't going for a straight 1:1 remake; rather, they are probably going to polish the game up, possibly adding in additional content, extended or alternate cutscenes, and refreshing dialogue for modern tendencies. Like the 3DS remake, I hope we see a boss-rush mode, enhanced menus and a master quest mode, but I also hope that we might see some fresh content by way of official randomisers or even additional content, such as restored or reimagined beta content.

I can always dream, but with a 2026 release slated for this title, I'm very much looking forward to replaying the adventure that really cemented my love for the Zelda series. I suspect it will be winter 2026 that we will see a release, possibly November or December, to resonate with the original's release window.

The question on everyone's lips is, "Will it come on a gold cartridge like the original collectors editions did?" We can only hope, but seeing as Donkey Kong Bananza didn't come on a yellow cartridge, I wouldn't hold my breath.

(Ocarina of Time was the highest-selling game of 1998 and was critically acclaimed thanks to its revolutionary targeting system, horse riding, and time travel mechanics)
