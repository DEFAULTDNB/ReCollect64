Pokemon Snap 3DS Released!

Portably Photogenic ~ May 12, 2026

Mobile Monsters

A couple of weeks ago I reported that Pokemon Snap was being impressively recreated in Unity on the 3DS. As of Friday, that port has gone live and is available to download and play on 3DS and 2DS consoles, as well as via popular 3DS emulation software, if you don't still own the stereoscopic 3D handheld.

Version 0.2.7 is considered the "first" stable released beta ready to test out, but V0.2.8 is now also available in .CIA and .3DS format. It features English, French, German, Italian, Portuguese (PT), Spanish and Japanese languages, a tutorial, the full beach level, 12 Pokemon to capture and 64 shots on your film reel with a fully working evaluation system!

Features:

Simple gameplay inspired by the original Nintendo 64 game: Equipped with your trusty camera, you must take the best possible photographs while automatically traveling through each stage aboard the ZERO-ONE.

Support for Nintendo 3DS exclusive features: Touch controls, stereoscopic 3D effect, Amiibo support, gyroscope controls, and more.

New routes and Pokémon not present in the original game. Pokémon Snap 3DS will feature completely new areas and many more Pokémon than the original.

Fully localized in Castilian Spanish, English, French, German, Italian, Portuguese, and Japanese. The language can be changed directly from the title screen.

Credits scene featuring all resources and contributors used during development.

Although designed for Nintendo 3DS hardware (especially New Nintendo 3DS), the game is also playable on other platforms including Windows, macOS, Linux, Android, iOS, Nintendo Wii U, PlayStation Vita, and Nintendo Switch. It is compatible with both real hardware and emulators (Citra, Azahar, etc.).

Snap up a Download

The ROM is available for download over at Whack a Hack, Eevee Expo and PokeCommunity, and I highly recommend you check out the forums and posts on each of those sites, because there is a wealth of information and enjoyable ROM hacks there to pore over.

As a beta, there are a few teething issues to be ironed out yet, so if you're running it on an old 3DS, please expect a few crashes, and the apple and pester items are not enabled in this release. There are two versions of the ROM too, one with extended memory and one without It's recommended to use the extended memory version where possible to enjoy greater stability but slightly increased load times.

This remake is as imopressively smooth and attentive to detail as you could possibly want. I can't wait to see if more levels (even custom levels) and features come to this version because 3DS players will be eating extremely well if they do!
