Rumble in the Jungle

It's 2026, the thirtieth year of knowing and loving the Nintendo 64, and yet again there are new titles in the pipeline. This one in particular is a little different because the game was originally developed by Conceptual Realities Inc. (CRI) and slated within the actual console's lifetime, only to be unceremoniously cancelled in 1997. Jungle Bots, now under the NEO Entertainment Group (founded by Ron Teeman - one of the original creators of Jungle Bots!), describes itself as an "N64 first-person shooter (FPS) that redefines the genre with a thrilling narrative, vibrant visuals & robotic chaos."

The WIP game is currently on Kickstarter and sits just shy of £2,000 of its £22,705 goal, which began in early July; it offers some gloriously in-depth information regarding the game's inception and design process as well as including some of the original concept art, the proposed toy line, and a complete step-by-step breakdown of the planned levels and works completed so far.

 

In 1997, a hidden tropical paradise became the battlefield for one of the most ambitious Nintendo 64 titles never released.

Jungle Bots is a full 3D action-shooter where you are shipwrecked on a volcanic island guarded by an army of chrome-and-steel mechanical beasts created by a long-lost genius. You start as a fragile human scavenger. You end as Max – a metallic super-soldier fused with the same living alloy that powers the Jungle Bots themselves.

Nostalgic Redemption

Jungle Bots was ultimately cancelled in 1998 due to disagreements over creative control and production terms. The game had a neat concept, fantastic vision, and Titus interactive was to publish the game. They had plans for a fully fleshed-out toyline as well as a cartoon series based on the success of the IP however, none of this was to come to pass. Thankfully Ron Teeman retained his ownership over the name and core code when it all fell through.

Picking up where CRI and Titus left off, the Neo Entertainment Group plans to bring the concept back to life using the exact same development hardware that would have been used in studios back in the late 1990s. Partener-N64 hardware is being deployed for the N64 version, which is already 50% complete and will release on cartridge, and a native PC version will also ensue to allow more people to experience the action from day one.

Notably, there are several reward tiers for backing the project, and most come with digital bonuses and OSTs, but the "Island Intruder" comes with a physical black cartridge, the "Jungle Bot Legend" a silver one, and the "Executive Producer Tier" packs a golden cartridge.
