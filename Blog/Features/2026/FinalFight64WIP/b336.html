Final Fight 64 WIP

N64GameBuilder's Latest Project ~ Jun 3, 2026

89 Vs 64

Originally built as a sequel to Capcom's Street Fighter (1987), Final Fight (1989) took a different approach, switching development and becoming side-scrolling beat 'em up instead of a pure fighting title. All of a sudden in 2026, we have N64GameBuilder back in action, and porting the nearly 40-year-old title to the Nintendo 64 thanks to some swish new tools.

Using software they have created called Felipe64, N64GameBuilder notes that you can easily export data, create hitboxes, and configure state machines using a visual interface within this toolset. At the end of the showcase of Felipe64's plethora of user-friendly options, they have put together a short clip of Final Fight 64 in motion that shows imported assets, scrolling, character traversal and actions, energy bars, and enemy interactions across two environments.

Imagine if the Nintendo 64 had properly bridged the gap between the 16-bit era and the 64-bit era by championing fresh 2D games as well as paradigm-shifting 3D titles. Two-dimensional titles account for less than 10% of the 388 games in the N64's arsenal. Of those, only four were true 2D fighting games, and absolutely zilch were 2D side-scrolling beat 'em ups (Batman Beyond and Fighting Force 64 cornered the 3D-scrolling beat 'em up genre on the N64).


Proof of Concept

With the goal of enabling other developers to port 2D games to the system, N64GameBuilder states that "the engine is being developed to make character animation design as easy and intuitive as possible, so anyone can bring their projects to life on the N64". These tools give potential developers power and scope, but will anyone pick up the reins and take them beyond the proof of concept stage?

It's worth noting that N64GameBuilder has previously also developed another brilliant 2D-based engine, one that is powerful enough to bring Mortal Kombat and Killer Instinct ports to the console, but as yet, no completed ports for either of these projects exist, only short demos, which is better than nothing, but it would be great to see something a full port completed and ready to play.
