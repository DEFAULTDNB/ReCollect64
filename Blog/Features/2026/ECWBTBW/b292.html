Extreme Modding

Retro Randy Price is back with yet another mod of THQ and AKI's 2000-era wrestling title WWF No Mercy. This time it's ECW (Extreme Championship Wrestling): Born to be Wired. What's more, there is currently a public beta test which means that you can try it out today for free!

The previous beta, version 7.1, was released in December and required you to pay a nominal subscription fee to unlock the demo on his Patreon. This, the latest version, requires zero subscription fee and works with real hardware (N64 and FPGA) via SummerCart64 (SC64: 2.18.0 is required) as well as emulators. 7.1 Patch Notes:

New Content & Gameplay Updates

Move Animations: Updated animations for various finishers, including 911's front and back finishers.

New Spots: Added interactive environment spots, such as a top-rope piledriver through a table in a barbed wire match.

Roster Additions: Included or refined characters like Rob Van Dam and Balls Mahoney in earlier versions, with further polishing in 7.1.

Carryover Features from Preview 6/6.1

Many of the core technical improvements in the 7.0/7.1 cycle were built on the foundation of the October 2025 updates:

Cage Match Overhaul: Lowered cage height for easier interaction, added the ability to perform moves off the cage while an opponent is escaping, and expanded cage match support to all match types (Tag, 3-Way, 4-Way).

Stability: Vastly improved RAM overspill issues to prevent game crashes and fixed white screen freezes in customization menus.

Hardware Compatibility: Switched to a Homebrew header to allow flashcarts to automatically detect save types.

Music & Audio: Removed "Trons" to free up space for more entrance music and fixed "static music" issues on certain emulators by better managing data beyond 64MB.
