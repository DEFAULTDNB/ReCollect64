Tomb Raider Port WIP

Ancient Artifacts

The original PlayStation vs Nintendo 64 era was a wild time; I recall the playgrounds being constantly a buzz with rivalry. While the Sony fans had billions of hours of FMV, expansive and fully licensed music tracks as well as multiple 700 MB discs to swap, the Nintendo 64 kept it simple with legendary titles such as the Banjo-Kazooie games and GoldenEye 007, and cartridges that only maxed out at 64 megabytes for games like Conker's Bad Fur Day and Resident Evil 2, the latter still being often referred to as the miracle port.

During this competitive time, some games appeared on one console but not the other. Games like Square's Final Fantasy VII famously ditched the Nintendo 64 due to its severe storage limitations and high production costs, but nowadays, can this be overcome through homebrew and decompilation efforts? Developer Snake has taken on the task of porting Tomb Raider to the Nintendo 64 through the Libdragon SDK, TRX, and HailToDondongo's Tiny 3D API.

Adventurous Adventures

Lara Croft's first adventures began on the original PlayStation console, and Nintendo fans were definitely irked to find out that Eidos had no intention of porting the game to Nintendo's 64-bit system. Through TRX, an open-source engine that aims to enhance the classic through decompilation and re-implementation of various pipelines, the game has already started running well (even on the Analogue 3D + overclocking), with full FMV cut scenes and the original soundtrack squeezed into just 64 MB!

The developer is fully aware of the notable bugs, rendering and performance issues, but the full game has been completely implemented, and this is just the beginning of an incredible project, with very little optimisation implemented.

There is no download available yet, Snake is keeping it under wraps until it's more stable, but rest asured I will update this article with all these details and more when the time comes!
