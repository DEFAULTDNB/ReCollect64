LEGO/OOT Temple Spotted

Astounding Architecture

As you know, I'm a sucker for LEGO MOCs (my own creations), especially if they fall into the Nintendo 64 genre of builds. We have previously seen Hyrule by SuperBrickDude, the N64 console and carts by IBrickedItUp, Fiddle and Wahooie by Pino_Creations and even some official offerings from Nintendo itself.

This week, I spotted a rather lovely diorama of the Forest Temple from The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time built by Matticus Bricks, which was exhibited at the recent 2026 Brick Con in Atlanta.

The piece is huge and comprises the instantly recognisable steps, pillars and frontage, including a nicely decorated stained-glass door, as well as the exterior's stone symbol flourishes, and the vines and branches that shroud it in mystery.

 

I built The Forest Temple from “The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time.” 🌳 One of my favorite Zelda dungeons for its near perfect puzzle solving, structure and just the right amount of creepy atmosphere, I was excited to build out this beautiful location in LEGO bricks.

I not only built the ornate entrance to the dungeon but also the epic boss battle with Phantom Ganon. Getting this technic mechanism to work was one of the biggest challenges I’ve ever faced as a LEGO builder. Swipe for work-in-progress pics and a look at how the mechanism works.

This MOC made its debut last month at @atlantabrickcon alongside an incredible Fire Temple build by my friend @antbandit

Incredible Innards

Not only does the project encompass the exterior with its fully greebled features, this build contains an additional fully animated battle scene between Link and Ganondorf, using a clever bit of LEGO Technic wizardry to bring it to life. The two can be seen here batting an energy ball back and forth just as in the battle within the game!

I have to say that the Forest Temple build is exceptionally eye-catching, and I can only hope that Matticus brings it across to either Nintendo for consideration of their future sets or to Rebrickable for anyone to build. The latter would be preferable, because it could be in the hands of LEGO and OOT fans a heck of a lot quicker than going through the formalities and stages of the LEGO Ideas submission system.

If that happens, rest assured that you, my avid followers, be the first to know!
