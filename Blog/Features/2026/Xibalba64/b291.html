Xibalba 64 Coming Soon

Beautiful Blasters

A fresh first-person shooter with visuals akin to Doom is destined for the 64-bit system thanks to its developer, Phoboslab, aka Dominic Szablewski.

Xibalba, a WebGL FPS, was built using JavaScript on top of the author's own HTML 5 game engine, named Impact, back in 2014, and after more than a decade, it is being natively ported to the Nintendo 64 for even more players to enjoy.

Mayan Mayhem

What features or improvements the 64-bit port might see, if any, are yet to be revealed, but the current versions available are fully featured with excellently animated sprite-based enemies, stylish cubic worlds, six levels, two difficulty settings, and bosses to defeat.

 

Fight your way through the Mayan Underworld in this retro First Person Shooter. The path leads you through lush jungles, eerie catacombs and high tech bases filled with dangerous creatures, traps, vital powerups and weapons.

The intuitive and precise controls provide an amazing gaming experience while the dark ambient soundtrack and high quality sound effects build a dense atmosphere.

The game features 6 different levels, 3 weapons, 6 types of enemies and an epic boss battle. And if that isn't enough, you can test your skill with the even more challenging "hard mode".

If you can't wait for the N64 edition, then please check it out on your PCs and web enabled portables right now via this link. The game looks gorgeous, runs buttery-smooth and the touch screen controls are superbly implemented to make the game very playable on mobile devices too!

With the gorgeous pixel art and mysterious themes, I'm certainly looking forward to giving this a full spin on my flash cart some time soon!
