BK Nostalgia 64 Coming May

64-bit Sentimentality

Prolific N64 ROM hacker and retro enthusiast, Markus Kurko, has recently announced that his latest Banjo-Kazooie overhaul project is due to arrive in May!

 

After a tough weekend fixing more bugs than I expected, I can finally announce that development on BK Nostalgia 64 is complete!

There are still a couple of weeks of extra testing left to look for minor bugs and polish things here and there, but if nothing major pops up again, I think I'll have the trailer ready with a definitive release date by the end of April.

I also still need to record the video guide so everything is ready for release day.

I just want to thank you all for your patience and support; without you, I'm not sure this project would have been possible.

Big hug!

Kurko has previously dropped several teasers showcasing environments and gameplay ideas for this mod from as far back as 2020. He then put out a tantalising five-minute teaser specifically for the "BK Beta" content of the Nostalgia mod on the 8th of March 2024. The gameplay within gives a fascinating look at how the Banjo-Kazooie beta footage differentiated itself from the final game and some super-cool customised secrets!

Patreon Powered Artistry

The BK Nostalgia mod looks to be something incredible for N64 fans to pore over when the final product lands. Thankfully, it will work on real hardware as well as emulators, so I will be playing it on everything possible to absorb as much of the nostalgia as I can!

 

The project started as a series of levels for people to play during the coronavirus lockdown.

The first level was released in April 2020, and I added the other levels little by little.

By early 2021, I already had all the levels, so I thought about making a small Hub World to bring them all together and renamed it BK Nostalgia 64.

There wasn't much more ambition than adding Yoshi's Story level and little else. However, the project grew, and a programmer joined, so we expanded everything.

At the end of 2021, I put the project on standby because many of the programming things were unfinished, and I had to wait.

In 2023, seeing that the programmer had completely abandoned the project, I took it back, modifying and adapting everything so I could finish it myself without outside help. I've been working on it since January 2024 until now.

By the way, the original Pokemon Snap world of Stay At Home was created by Bynine (creator of Cave of Dreams).

For Nostalgia 64, I've reworked the models to update them a bit, but the ideas and level design are his. Btw, for the music Riposte helped me with tons of songs, arranging new Midis and fixing some old of mine.
