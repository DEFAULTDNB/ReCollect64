UPDATED Brawler64 Pro Announced!

Kickstarter by Retro Fighters ~ Sep 16, 2025
Brawler64 Pro Announced!

Big Bad Brawler

Retro Fighters has today announced a fresh Kickstarter project for their new Pro series of Brawler64 controllers. Initially available in 6 "funtastic" colours, including Classic Grey, Watermelon, Extreme Green, Turquoise, Atomic Purple, and Smoke Black, the team are also offering stretch goals at $50k for more coloured faceplates, including those from the original solid-coloured OEM controller selection.

 

Introducing the Brawler64 Pro

We've listened to years of feedback and are now ready to deliver once more! The incredibly nostalgic charm of primary colored face buttons now meets the precision and comfort expected of a modern wireless gamepad.

Features:

+ 2.4GHz Wireless

+ Hall Effects Stick (no wear, no drift)

+ Back Grip Buttons (programmable)

+ Improved Accuracy (0.95 standard deviation validated with MIMI)

+ 8-way Notched Gate

+ Magnetic Faceplate System

+ Immersive Adjustable Vibration (3 levels)

+ Ultra Compatible (N64, Windows PC, NS/NS2, Steam/Deck & MiSTer FPGA)

+ 30 Ft Wireless Range

+ Back Button Locks

+ Precision Tactile D-Pad (feat. Kailh switches)

+ 1000Hz Polling Rate (wired mode)

Pad Proficiency

Retro Fighters has always provided quality controllers across their entire range. From the Brawler64, the BrawlerGEN, the Hunter, the StrikerDC, the BattlerGC, the Duellist and the Defender, there are some absolutely stunning controller creations available.

If you want to be notified regarding the launch, please head on over to their Kickstarter page link below and join the waiting list to be the first in line to bag this truly next-gen N64 controller when it lands. I will also update this post, to keep everyone in the loop!

I would imagine that this will also be fully compatible with the Analogue 3D and the Mod Retro M64, so Nintendo 64 fans certainly have a lot of great things to look forward to in the coming weeks and months!

Having only recently reviewed the Brawler64 V1, I am definitely chomping at the bit to get my hands on this one so that I can do a bit of a comparison and give it a thorough test across a range of hardware, including the new FPGA consoles when they arrive!
