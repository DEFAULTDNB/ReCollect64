2 NSO N64 Games Incoming

The Great Escape

The Nintendo 64 NSO drip-feed continues to surprise and amaze, as today, Rayman 2: The Great Escape and Tonic Trouble have been revealed as coming to the Nintendo Switch Online service on December the 17th.

The addition of these two games will bring the total number of Nintendo 64 games available on the Expansion Pack service to 44, out of the 388 available games for the system.

Tonic Trouble

Ubisoft were premuim 3D platform game designers in the late 90s, though nowadays they tend to focus on 2D platforming. Tonic Trouble was originally developed as a test for the engine that powered Rayman 2 and was released after heavy delays in August of 1999.

Rayman 2: The Great Escape was highly regarded on all platforms, and released just two months later in October 1999. The quality difference between the two games meant that Rayman scored an incredible 90% average score, whereas Tonic Trouble scored just 72% on average. The latter is often overlooked as a game, but it's actually not terrible!
