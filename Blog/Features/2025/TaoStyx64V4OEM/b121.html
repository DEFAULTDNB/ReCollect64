A Speedrunners Dream

The sticks feature a unique adjustment system to allow players to customise the feel and output for their specific needs. It also features OEM gate and bowl compatibility, as well as a tongue-and-groove tapered cap to ensure stability during play and a simple method to swap them over as you wish. All you need to provide is the spring, encoder wheels, encoder PCB, lower housing and gears from an OEM controller so that you can build your own module with any combination of these redesigned (stick/bowl/gate) components, and drop it all into your controller.

As part of Project Renaissance, the components have been tested by speedrunners and competition-level players with incredible results. Using the portable N64 test rig or any controller testing app as a guide, you can see that any variances are extremely small in comparison with parts built by Nintendo, meaning you get rock-solid performance with parts that are all guaranteed to work in conjunction with the OEM modules built into any first-party controller.

The steel bowls are multi-material with a hardened insert for durability, while the gates are expertly notched and made from injection-moulded high-quality polycarbonate that features a set-screw design to fit with existing OEM assembly.

The level of technology and stringent micro-measurements has led the project to become as good, if not better, than OEM in terms of tolerances. The bowl housing is also now being injection moulded, as opposed to 3D printed, making it more consistent with benchmarking across the board.

Production Perfection

The smoothness and guaranteed performance from these sticks are incalculable. This is, after all, the fourth version of an already highly regarded product. Steel sticks are revered in the speedrunning scene as the best of the best, and though they aren't strictly aimed at the average Joe, these products are capable of extending and improving the lifespan of your favourite controllers, regardless of your skill level.
