GE Recomp Update!

Firing on all Cylinders

Following on from my initial article on March the 12th, yesterday, KholdFuzion shared a small update regarding SonicDcer's input towards the GoldenEye 007 recompilation project. Whereas yesterday the game booted to the initial file select menus, today that progress had accelerated into fast and fluid gameplay!

SonicDcer is a name you will recognise from the previous Star Fox 64 recomp project, and he is also part of the community-driven group who are affectionately known as the Harbour Masters of The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask and Ocarina of Time PC port fame.

Got it working right, there's still a lot to be done though. pic.twitter.com/F7IAVnnHU1

Logical Progressions

To the untrained eye, this already looks extremely promising, with the controls seemingly mapped to a modern controller and a staggering 240 FPS according to the statistical overlay. This is no mean feat, but if anyone can do it, you had better believe that it will be the Masters of the Harbour!

While no release date has been given, the progress looks to be coming on leaps and bounds now, so hopefully we will see pro-level GoldenEye 007 players, such as Graslu00, getting to test it out sooner rather than later.

Notably, while checking in on SonicDcer's Github repositories, I noticed a little movement around the Wave Race 64 codebase, with some assembly added and the decompilation progress jumping from 17.78% to a healthy 29.01%. It's still too early to tell, but I guarantee that Wave Race 64: Recompiled would go down an absolute treat with a certain unhinged account on the old bird app.
