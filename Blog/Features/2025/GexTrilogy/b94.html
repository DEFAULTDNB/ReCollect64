GEX Remasters Coming 2025

Lounge Lizard

Powered by the Carbon Engine, Gex Trilogy Remastered is about to cling to consoles come Summer 2025. Featuring GEX, GEX: Enter the Gecko, and GEX 3: Deep Cover Gecko, Limited Run Games and Crystal Dynamics are releasing the 2D and 3D titles physically for Nintendo Switch, PS5, & Xbox Series systems. However, it will also release digitally those platforms as well as Steam.

The first GEX title, a 2D platformer, was first released in 1994 for Sega Saturn, PlayStation, 3DO, and the like, but it wasn't until GEX: Enter the Gecko (1998) and GEX 3: Deep Cover Gecko (1999) that the N64 got to play the 3D platformers.

The remasters promise native widescreen support for the two most recent games and additional save state and rewind support for all three. You can view the latest trailer here:

Cunning Linguist

Gex is renowned for slinging quips and quotes, but even in 1999, I had no clue who or what he was referring to half the time because the jokes were so pop-culture-specific. Starring US comedian Dana Gould, this package will include a bonus behind-the-scenes interview with the voice behind GEX, a jukebox, and a media viewer too.

The games featured in the trailer indicate that the PlayStation version of the 3D games are being remastered, not the N64 ones; you can tell by the triangular textures and wobbly pixels.

I'm interested in replaying these games today, even if I still have no clue who Joe Piscopo or Skatman Crothers are, to relive some of the nostalgia of the time before we all freaked out about the Y2K bug.
