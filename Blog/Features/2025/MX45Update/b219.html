New MagicX Retro 45 Info

Handheld Hopes

Following on from the initial tease of the MagicX Retro 45, today MagicX has released fresh images of their portable Nintendo 64-focused console, as well as giving a little info on the technical specifications.

The device is set to use emulation rather than FPGA, which means that the performance will be restricted by the emulator's ability to replicate how an N64 works, but with the following overheads, it should do just fine:

 

Hi everyone, we are preparing for the next equipment.

MagicX One Retro45

MTK G85

4+64GB

4.5-inch screen 1440X1080

5G wifi + 5.0 Bluetooth

Price: $95

We would love to hear your thoughts or suggestions.

Specific Specifications

By all accounts, the 4.5" 4:3 display is not the strongest feature shown off. It would have been nice to have seen something a little bigger, perhaps more akin to the 5" display of an OLED Sony Vita, or even nearer a 6.2" display that the OG Nintendo Switch had, though it would cause the size of the device to become all the more burly. For $95 I can pretty much guarantee that it will not be an OLED panel; it will probably get the same treatment as the Retro 40's IPS screen, but time will tell, because not everyone was satisfied with the screen's performance on the Retro 40.

With an emulated core at its heart, it will be interesting to see how MagicX implements a UI for the console and how it will map the 6 front buttons for other emulators, though with the release of the M64 and the Analogue 3D looming, I will probably not be able to stretch my funds in order to purchase one of these for review. (MagicX, please hook me up if you're reading this!).

The 4GB RAM and MediaTek Helio G85 have been a notably solid combination for emulation up to PSP and Dreamcast, with a cap around the Dolphin-level emulation (Wii/GC), though there is very little chance of PS2/3DS or Switch emulation on this build. I'm looking forward to seeing just what it's capable of and how flawless the N64 emulation turns out, to give buyers an authentic-looking and feeling N64 experience on the go, without N64 fans just resorting to using their phones and a Bluetooth controller.
