BK Themed Console Spotted!

Honey Dipped

A couple of days ago, I came across this stunning console on the N64 Italia Facebook group and made the decision to reach out to its creator. The customised console is the work of Alessandro Filippone and features some of the most brilliantly nostalgic modifications I have ever seen given to a console.

To begin with, the shell is half yellow and half red with matching red slot doors and expansion port cover with a Rare "R" logo adorning it. Even the Nintendo logo has been inset with the same colour red, all of which contrast perfectly with the honey-coloured golden top half! The feet, buttons and controller ports are the same blue as Banjo's backpack, and not only do they pick out the small details, such as the player number being inlaid yellow, but they all work together wonderfully within the gorgeously vibrant frame.

The system's front jewel is also bespoke, with an iconic pose of the bear and bird duo on the cubic "N" logo, and there is a blingy golden embossed "B-K 64" honeycomb logo attached just to the right of the cartridge slot.

Even the controllers have had the tailored treatment, with a red and yellow pad being cut-and-shut together (or perhaps Stop-N-Swopped) to form a Jiggy-yellow-topped, red-backed Banjo-Kazooie version and a Breegull-red-fronted, yellow-backed Banjo-Tooie variant. Each one has a logo emblazoned onto its front, which really elevates the quality of this build.

Augmented Awesomeness

On the top left of the console sits an ominous pair of Rareware's trademark googly eyes, a persistent feature within all their games for the 64-bit system. This time, the eyes sit within a circular groove which can be used to slot in the official Banjo-Kazooie Amiibo!

Internally the console has also been fitted with an HDMI socket, so you can enjoy your games at HD quality while you gawp at this beauty!

As a display piece, this is superb, and as a functioning console, it's breathtaking. I can't imagine any BK fans not wanting to take this home with them; it really is oozing with 90s nostalgia!
