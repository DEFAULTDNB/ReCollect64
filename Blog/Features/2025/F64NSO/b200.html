Mature Marauding

This morning, Nintendo of America posted that a 42nd N64 game would be joining to the 64-bit selection on their Nintendo Switch consoles by way of the paid Nintendo Switch Online + Expansion Pack services "Mature 17+" app:

The future is… forsaken!

Forsaken was initially released in 1998 for Windows, PlayStation and Nintendo 64 and offered 360° shooting, exploration and hunting in the year 2113, with Descent-style gameplay and a frantic four-player multiplayer mode built in.

With four difficulty modes, 25 weapons, 22 missions, 6 multiplayer modes, and up to 3 AI bots, Probe Entertainment were praised for delivering smooth gameplay, advanced lighting and explosion effects, a banging EDM soundtrack, and a split-screen experience that was proclaimed as one of the best on the N64 at the time.

In 2018, Nightdive Studios remastered Forsaken, with fans on modern platforms getting to experience the game once again, with upscaled graphics, tighter controls and powered by the KEX engine. The N64 version notably led with a vigilante-style goal of killing looters and marauders while hunting down treasure, whereas the PS and Windows release focuses less on violence and more on you hunting for the planet's lost treasure.

Pre-Identified Inclusions

With Forsaken's entry into the N64 Mature app as of tomorrow (4th of September), this addition lines up with the recent speculation that other games had been identified as potentially coming soon to the Nintendo Switch's add-on emulation service.

We will have to keep holding on hope for Super Smash Bros., Glover and Donkey Kong 64 to make their way to the service at somepoint through Nintendo's drip-feeding system of completely random and sporadic releases.
