GC N64 Emu Decompiled

Games Cubed

In 2003, Nintendo brought out The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time Master Quest and The Legend of Zelda: Collector's Edition for the GameCube. These discs contained the classic Ocarina of Time, a version with tougher dungeons, more enemies, and beefed-up bosses, and the latter contained a couple of NES Zelda games but also included The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask. These games were not ports; they were ROMs, and the discs contained emulators.

Today, some 22 years later, the first GameCube decompilation effort by ZeldaRET (Zelda Reverse Engineering Team) has successfully produced the 100% complete source code for the N64 emulator that powered those games on the cubic system.

The emulator is attributed to Stephen Lee, a software engineer at Nintendo of America in the early 2000s, who built a functional emulator specifically for the two N64 Zelda titles. His work would also be used for N64 emulation on Wii Virtual Console, where the 21 released titles each had a bespoke, tweaked emulator bundled with them.

The information was shared by Twitter user RobloxVoiceChat, who is focused on security research, reverse engineering, and Roblox:

 

This emulator was used in the releases of Ocarina of Time and Majora's Mask on the GameCube. Of course, the zeldaret team was behind this decomp project.

The original emulator had full dwarf information non some versions, which really allowed this one to be ahead of everyone.

The thing that really pushed this one ahead was MetroTRK.

On older versions of the Metrowerks PPC compiler, there is a bug fix which in short would cause MetroTRK to become unmatchable. Someone from the oot-gc team patched the compiler to make it matchable again.

Achievement Unlocked

With Nintendo 64 emulation being carried by Cube64 and Not64, both ports of Mupen64 by team Wii64 and Extrems, there aren't many options for playing N64 ROMs on the GameCube system.

The decompilation of this emulator may yield some interesting information that might prove useful in bolstering existing emulators, but by and large, this is more of a technical achievement, rather than something that will immediately benefit the system when it comes to N64 emulation.

That being said, this also marks the first GameCube title to be fully decompiled, which is a notable achievement for both scenes nonetheless.
