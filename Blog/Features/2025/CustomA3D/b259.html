Candy Coloured Creation

Barely one month after the launch of the Analogue 3D 4K FPGA Nintendo 64 console, and just days after the bittersweet announcement of the Funtastic and gold variants being released in time for Christmas, people have already begun taking it into their own hands to modify their console from the stock black or white that us early adopters purchased.

Jordan Handwerker recently wowed the Analogue 3D Facebook groups (linked below) by posting what is possibly the very first customised Analogue 3D console, in a gorgeous candy orange and white colourway. He also provided some stunning images and a tutorial video for those who wish to customise theirs too!

Patient Painting

In essence, to respray your console, you need to disassemble the unit by peeling off the rubber sole, then using a T15 Torx screwdriver and disconnecting two ribbon cables. Then the process of priming the shell begins by rubbing it down with fine sandpaper until it's lightly scuffed all over, giving it a surface to adhere to, before using rubbing alcohol to ensure a clean surface ready for painting.

As you can see, Jordan's paint process requires four stages: firstly, two coats of a plastic adhesion promoter, then a coating of metallic base (if you're spraying it a candy colour), and then roughly four layers of the candy colouring over the top of that, followed by a final layer of plastic clear coat. Ensuring the layers are extremely even and giving each one 15-20 minutes of drying time in between is key to getting perfect coverage and solid colouring throughout. If you're going to undertake this, please take your time and keep your layers thin and even.

It will be interesting to see what other custom colour creations people come up with, as well as effects, themes and possibly even hardware mods such as cutting into the shell or adding RGB LEDs or something whacky. I'm here for all of it!
