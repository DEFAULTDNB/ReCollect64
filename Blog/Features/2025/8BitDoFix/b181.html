Fixing the 8BitDo 64 Pad

Diagonal Diagnosis

As confirmed in my recent review, the 8BitDo 64 controller has one major caveat: those pesky analogue stick diagonals. But in recent days, new information has come to light, and I have tested and verified the solution to the problem!

Having contacted 8BitDo support, I was met with a dead end and a request to upload video proof of my lacklustre inputs. Rather than needing to do this, which I found to be a frankly ridiculous ask, you can simply update using the beta firmware funtion of the updated software!

First things first, download the Ultimate Software 2 for PC, then when installed, click settings and press "Get Beta Firmware". Lastly, connect your controller in D-Mode using the provided USB-C cable, select the version 1.03 update, and click update.

Once this is done, and it only takes a minute or two, you will find that the range on your controller has been miraculously recalibrated, and shows diagonal values consistent with an official controller!

The UL/UR/DL/DR values should be within the range of +-67~69, whereas the main directions should still be set at around +-83~85. This is a huge improvement over the previous tests, which reported decent values U/D/L/R but rather flat values of just +-60 each way on the diagonals.

The resulting stick values feel a heck of a lot more responsive, and far less sluggish when pressing into those angles, meaning that precision aiming and flying are now a reality!

Marvelous Modifications

I also spotted this image recently, which was floating around one of the Facebook groups. The mod involves taking apart your 64 Controller and swapping out the buttons; spoiler alert, they fit perfectly ad they look insanely good!

I'm probably going to modify mine, if I have a knackered-out controller to hand, because it just elevates the controller even further by having the real coloured buttons as well as a modern-feeling pad with OEM-accurate inputs.
