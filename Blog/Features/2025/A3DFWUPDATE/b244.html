TROUBLESHOOTING TIPS


Remember to update your 8BitDo 64 controller using the 8BitDo Ultimate Updater V2 software on a PC when you update your A3D's firmware. Use THIS LINK as a guide to help you through it.

If you get the corrupted icons issue on your Analogue 3D console, the best solution is to remove the SD card. Try a new SD card (FAT32 or exFAT up to 1TB), and reinstall the latest firmware and the labels.db of your choice.

Connect your console to a PC using a data-friendly USB-C to USB-A cable. Hold RESET and then hold the POWER button until the LED turns green. Then drag the firmware update onto the root of the SD card.

Remove the console using "remove drive" on your PC. Plug the console into your TV and power cable, and turn it on. The LED should turn yellow. Allow it to update over the next 5-6 minutes.

Connect your console to your PC again to put the labels.db in "root/Library/N64/Images" once the firmware has been successfully updated.

Performing a global reset will not fix this issue. It is caused by a corrupted SD card and is likely the result of a cheap or faulty SD card being supplied by the manufacturer.
