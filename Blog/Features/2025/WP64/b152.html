Wrestle Planet 64 WIP

New WIP Modification by MFillaz ~ Jun 11, 2025

No Limits Action

Following on from his WWF No Mercy 97-99 Mod Pack, MFillaz is back with Wrestle Planet 64, an upcoming modification of WWF No Mercy that aims to let you wrestle anyone from any time period or company, without any restrictions!

The endgame is to have the ultimate AKI-style mod that features around 75% classic wrestlers to 25% current faces, with moveset codes credited to WldFb, the author of the WWF No Mercy Moveset Editor V3.

Since the 21st of May, the project has been publicly underway, and with the help of various community tricks, hacks, mods, and even Game Shark codes, it has made significant progress in transforming clever ideas into a massive modification.

While this selection is exhaustive, there are already plans to add yet more names to the jam-packed lineup. Firstly, some names have been left off that list to preserve a little mystery, and secondly, MFillaz will be utilising a "DLC"-like function. By leaving just one full slot with four attires open for DLC swapping from the Memory Pak, theoretically, there could be another 72 wrestlers added. Leaving two or three slots available for DLC could see a tremendous amount more added over time, thanks to MFillaz looking for creative ways to circumvent attire limitations.

 

The plan is to have a few alt variations using actual slots (Attire 'types'), as well as having textures loaded and ready to be altered in SD Mall.

For example, if you change HBK from 98 to black and green, you get the DX attire.

Continual Content

Speaking to MFillaz, he stated that the goal is (possibly) to open a community that hosts packs of wrestlers which can be imported and exported with ease, creating an ongoing AKI-style universe of characters, movesets and attires that pulls together the entire community of 64-bit wrestling game modders' work into one cumulative effort.

It's commendable to see such passion and dedication with mods like this for a game that's teetering on 25 years of age. The project looks fresh and exciting and will no doubt ignite a buzz with wrestling fans and N64 fans alike. Even games such as NFL Blitz get updated rosters each season thanks to modders and enthusiastic N64 communities.

With no set release date for this massive overhaul modification, the title could yet change to Wrestle Planet 2026, or even AKI Planet, or something similar due to the creator's ambition of crafting an AKI-based equivalent of the Fire Pro Wrestling World game.

Even though this will not be playable on real hardware, I'm very much looking forward to getting to play this in the future and giving my opinion of the mod via review. Keep your eyes peeled for this one, it's going to be epic!
