M64 Variants Revealed

Baring All

As promised, ModRetro has finally shown off their 4K FPGA console for the world to drool over. With a small selection of three colours, the M64 will be available to preorder in translucent purple, translucent green and a rather natty translucent white, which looks most like the original Smoke Black variant Funtastic console.

The power and reset buttons look rather comical in my opinion; they didn't need to be so oversized, but I suppose from an ease of use and accessibility standpoint, bigger is always better!

Palmer Luckey has since mentioned that twelve colours are in production, and that the shells are easily interchangeable with a very snappy one-minute fitting time. He also went on to show a previously unannounce variant, the AMD red shell, which signifies the collaborative partnership between ModRetro and AMD on the M64.

Trident Tested

Let's talk about the controller. Sure, it's a trident shape, and rather than reinventing the wheel, ModRetro have kept it retro, with the fork design that only slightly deviates from the OEM controller's contours, the same way a typical third-party pad would back in the day, however, the part that throws me off immediately is the angular and sharp-edged-looking d-pad. Is that really going to be comfy for extended usage? We shall see.

As long as the stick reads the same ranges as the OEM stick in a benchmark and has an identical gate, it should feel relatively similar. Let's hope that Palmer Luckey's team has gone for gold with their 64-bit effort. It will be an interesting shootout, comparing ModRetro's version to the Analogue 3D and the 8BitDo 64 controller.
