Superlative Mario

Today Kaze Emanuar has released the second demo for his highly anticipated Super Mario 64 ROM hack dubbed "Return to Yoshi's Island".

The hack is capable of producing 60 FPS on real hardware, thanks to years of extensive and well-documented research by Kaze, reversing the game's source code, and even entirely rewriting it in places to produce the most amazing-looking ROM hack to date!

The first demo patch, which was released in 2021, showed off all manner of hacks and patches that improved the engine that SM64 runs within. Demo 2 is another opportunity to check out the project's progress, with the focus being on testing out the camera system and movement mechanics.

The C-Buttons control the camera as expected, but there are a slew of new camera controls, including first-person mode on the L button, you can recentre the camera behind you with the R button, zoom in and out with the D-Pad up and down buttons, and slowly rotate the camera with D-Pad left and right too.

New mechanics include wall jumping, a new jump when you hit Z to ground pound and then press A to jump again, and you can hold Z to spin/land faster when you bounce off a launcher, just like in Super Mario Bros Wii. The game also adopts lots of features akin to those you would find in Super Mario Sunshine, such as collectable red coins, coin-holding birds, new animations, and enhanced NPC interactivity.

For the best experience, Kaze recommends you install https://parallel-launcher.ca/, an emulator that effectively uses the Parallel core from Retroarch to play Nintendo 64 games on your chosen platform.

Demonstrative Delights

The ROM hack, which has been in active development since at least 2020, was due to release in 2023; however, Kaze has been perfecting his miraculous mods in order to provide an experience that almost seems impossible to have achieved running on unmodified hardware.

With the optimisations made throughout the past several years, Kaze has managed to eke out performance gains, improve draw distance, number of objects on screen, fonts, textures, and models, and add a vast array of new features that show just what the game and console are truly capable of. Included in the updated patch are four stars in the Bunny Brushwoods area, as well as a bunch of secrets including a hidden playable "stress test" area of Delphino Plaza from the GameCube title Super Mario Sunshine, which I have yet to find, and an orange Yoshi who exchanges coins for clothes and hints at future in-game shop mechanics.

To play the game, you can either patch the 8MB Super Mario 64 U.Z64 ROM (MD5: 20B854B239203BAF6C961B850A4A51A2), or apply the BPS file to the ROM in Parallel Launcher. The hack runs on most flashcarts, and if you patched the ROM directly, you will have a 21.2MB *.Z64 file that has the MD5: A391CA7238F4411712C3EF9E8E23F514.
