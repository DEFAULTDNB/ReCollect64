WCW vs nWo: 9 Underground Released

A Hack by RagDas ~ Jun 27, 2025

Data Recovery

Recently, wrestling ROM hacks have gained popularity, and RagDas has now released Underground 9, his modification of AKI's 1997 wrestle-fest WCW Vs nWo: World Tour.

This hack is only the second ever made by RagDas, and this one aims to add nine playable characters to the roster and tap into the unused remnants of the Virtual Pro Wrestling 64 code within the ROM itself.

As it turns out, the moveset and parameter data for the entire playable roster of VPW64 is contained within World Tour, so what RagDas has done is to reimplement and configure characters and their repertoires from varied and incomplete code.

The characters that have been added along with their stables, plus the replaced faces, are as follows:

 

New Characters:

1) Andy the Gigant (Andre the Giant) - WCW

2) Chono (Masahiro Chono) - nWo

3) Bad Blood (Great Muta) - nWo

4) Danny Fang Jr. (Dory Funk Jr.) - DoA

5) Major Tom (Terry Funk) - DoA

6) Abispa (Jushin Thunder Liger) - IU

7) Le Masquerade (Mil Mascaras) - IU

8) Dosquerade (Dos Caras) - IU

9) The Discloser (The Destroyer) - WWW

Replacements:

Shawn Michaels replaces Eric Bischoff

Stone Cold replaces Buff Bagwell

Undertaker replaces Scott Norton

Bret Hart replaces Tiger Jeet Singh

Ken Shamrock replaces Chris Benoit

Andre the Giant replaces Fake Sting

Rickson Gracie replaces Shunji Takano

Dr. Death 'replaces' Koji Kitao (this is a reskin; Kitao already used Dr. Death's moveset)

The Final Count

In order to fit the new players and accommodate the 62 animations that were imported from VPW64, a few sacrifices had to be made. Namely, text and titles have been cut down to make space, as well as consolidating existing character attire setups to free up two costume slots, and the newly added faces don't have portraits yet. This is because adding those images simply would not fit within the strict confines of the 12 MB ROM.

To try out your own ROM, all you need to do is take the REV1 USA *.Z64 ROM with an MD5 of B7A220B59303D47f3BEAE233CA868CFD and use the xDelta patch linked below to create the final ROM with an MD5 of BE66CE406E5C07633C3CDF4B8CA43152.

According to the author, this ROM hack is incompatible with real hardware for now, and in testing I found that only the M64py front end ran the ROM hack on my aging laptop, whereas PJ64 (latest) and RMG (latest) wouldn't run it at all. I'm not entirely sure why this is; perhaps some settings need adjusting, but the rest of the library works fine.

The game is certainly fun, but it's a real shame it doesn't work on consoles yet. Here's hoping RagDas might choose to get it working on flashcarts in the not-too-distant future.
