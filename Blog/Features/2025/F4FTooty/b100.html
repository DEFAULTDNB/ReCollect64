F4F Tooty Announced

Rooty Tooty Booty

First 4 Figures has unveiled a fresh range of Banjo Kazooie-themed figurines, along with Tooty, with a projected release date of Q2 2025, which could mean as late as the end of June.

The model makers Instagram blew up this week with new images of the super cutie Tooty figurine. Preorders for Banjo's little sister will begin on the 18th of March 2025.

 

The statue draws inspiration from the Grunty's Furnace Fun minigame, where Banjo and Kazooie must play to rescue Tooty. It faithfully reproduces Tooty's appearance, including her expressive eyes, high pigtails, and signature red shirt with a yellow star. The base design reflects the platform from which Tooty is "won" as the star prize in Grunty's Furnace Fun.

Nostalgic Nintendo

Though Rare is owned by Microsoft, it remains strange to see a lack of Nintendo branding. When Rare was acquired by the Windows company in 2002, I think it's fair to say that our collective Nintendo-adoring hearts sank. There hasn't been any further platform games featuring Banjo-Kazooie since 2000, when Banjo-Tooie, the titular sequel to Banjo-Kazooie, stormed onto the N64, raising the bar for platform gaming around the globe.

Banjo-Kazooie: Nuts & Bolts released on Xbox 360 in 2008, and while most people were hoping for Banjo-Threeie, they were met with a confusing fusion of building and driving, with a very strange, caricature-looking, version of the bear and bird duet.

First4Figures has gone back to the N64 roots to capture a selection of characters, transformations, and poses from the animal duo. I particularly like the Mumbo Jumbo figure, but I'm not sure I can stretch to $451.00 for the privilege.

Mumbo Jumbo, Gruntilda (Standard and Exclusive), Jinjos (Combo Edition), and several of Banjo's transformations (Walrus, Termite, Crocodile, Pumpkin, and Bee) are available to buy now, ranging between $299.99 and $599.99, if you can stomach the cost.

Mumbo Jumbo (Crocodile bundle), Banjo-Kazooie (Flying: Standard and Exclusive), Banjo-Kazooie (Duet: Standard and Definitive Edition), five colours of Jinjo (sold separately), and the standard, definitive, and exclusive editions of Tooty will all be available Q2 2025, with prices ranging from $129.99 for a singular Jinjo up to $699.99 for the Mumbo Jumbo Crocodile Transformation bundle.

Bearing in mind that most of these figurines are 18" in stature and numbered for rarity, the eye-watering prices make a little more sense, but I still think that these will only be snapped up by the die-hard BK collectors and fans. I'm not sure many can justify the expenditure with the current cost of living.

Back in the day, I owned the The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time Sheikh and The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask Skull Kid F4F statues. Luckily I was gifted them, so they didn't cost me a thing. Of course, I still own the Skull Kid one!
