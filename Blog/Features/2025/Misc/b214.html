N64 Games For Halloween

Spooky Season is Here! ~ Oct 7, 2025
N64 Games For Halloween

Killer Cuts

With October firmly upon us and the evenings drawing in, I thought it only apt to put together a list of games that you should be playing this month if you really want to get encapsulated by the Halloween spirit!

Here are some of the top horror and supernatural-themed games available on the Nintendo 64 that are sure to evoke a sense of fear and excitement. If you don't already own these games, I have presented them on this page as if you have just picked up their boxes in the store and are reading the descriptions from cover to cover.

Imagine you are at Blockbuster Video on October the 31st, in the late 90s, and take your time considering these for your entertainment, ready to play on All Hallows' Eve!

Castlevania - Konami - January 26th, 1999

Castlevania (N64), also known as Demon Castle Dracula: Apocalypse, was the first time that a Castlevania game had been allowed to transform itself into 3D. The 19 prior games in the series had all been 2D side-scrollers, but this one was set to pit you against the undead in a new dimension of realism.

Set in 1852, you play as Reinhardt Schneider, of the Belmont clan of vampire hunters, or Carrie Fernandez, who is gifted with magical powers. Tasked with exploring the Wallachia province, you must focus on banishing all evil entities and ultimately facing off with Count Dracula himself.

Set across ten locations, you battle against skeletons, werewolves, weretigers, lizard men, knights, and vampires as well as 10 larger-than-life bosses that will frighten you from start to finish!

Castlevania Legacy of Darkness - Konami - December 25th, 1999

Castlevania: Legacy of Darkness is essentially a remastered and expanded "prequel" special edition version of the original Castlevania (N64) release that contains two additional characters, Cornell and Henry Oldrey, as well as some extended story elements.

Starting out in 1844, you follow the journey of Cornell, a lycanthrope on a mission to protect his adopted sister from becoming a blood offering to resurrect Dracula. With the strength of a werewolf, hack and slash your enemies to shreds and vanquish evil at every juncture to save your sister from a morbid fate.

Henry's mission begins in 1852, being sent by the church to rescue children from the horrors of Dracula's fortified castle. He has 7 days to rescue 6 children and is clad in knight's armour and brandishing a gun with which to slay evil. Completing his missions unlocks bonuses for other characters!

Doom 64 - id Software - April 4th, 1997

Doom 64 is a brutally violent first-person shooter that pits you against 28 levels of pain and suffering, battling demons, collecting keys and surviving wave after wave of ambushes and deadly traps.

Playing as a marine, you blast through thousands of enemies to reach the Mother Demon. You need to destroy her and the resurrector before she can create even more super strong, vicious monsters to drag you back to hell.

With four secret bonus levels, piles of corpses, blood and guts everywhere, and chainsaws to slice up the spectres, what's not to love?!

Hexen - id Software - June 23rd, 1997

Hexen: Beyond Heretic uses its dark fantasy horror themes and unsettling music to draw you into this spooky title. Set in Cronos, you assume the role of one hero in a band of three in a quest to destroy Korax, the evil serpent rider.

Choosing between Baratus the brute, Parias the cleric, or Daedolon the mage, your weaponry and characteristics differ depending on the class you pick, affording you three playthroughs before you've experienced it all!

Multiplayer for up to four players also gives this game a brutal and replayable reason to huddle around a sofa together sharing ghost stories or trash-talking about your proficiency for killing your friends in the game.

Mortal Kombat 4 - Midway - June 23rd, 1998

The only fighting game to feature on this list is Mortal Kombat 4. The cast of 18 characters, each with four fatalities, offer hand-to-hand as well as weapon-based combat, gallons of blood-soaked gore, bone breaking, spine ripping, acid melting, skin tearing, decapitation and impalement-lead action that is bound to captivate and make you wince.

As fighting games go, this one has in-depth stories for each character, interesting endings, and creepy secrets to uncover. Another great thing about this arcade fighting game is that it's quick and action-packed, so playing with a group of friends is massively entertaining as well as shockingly violent.

Using a GameShark you can also unlock Kitana as a fourth secret character with the code "800FE293 0010". Though her portrait is broken and the game freezes in single player modes, she was only cut from the final build of MK4 at the last minute due to time constraints.

Nightmare Creatures - Kalisto Entertainment - November 30th, 1998

Nightmare Creatures is a survival horror game set in the gritty grimy gothic streets of 1834's London and proceeds to incorporate the game's narrative into the root cause of the fire of London.

The story goes that back in 1666 the brotherhood of Hecate endeavoured to create a superhuman serum in order to rise up and take over London; however, they accidentally produced grotesque mutated monsters and now intend to use these creatures as an army of conquest.

Playing as either Ignatius or Nadia, your mission is to slay the ungodly creatures and the army of the undead within the spooky, foggy atmospheric locations during a time of panic and vulnerability in London due to the engulfing flames of the great fire.

Resident Evil 2 - Capcom - January 21st, 1998

Resident Evil 2, considered "the impossible port", squeezed 2 CDs worth of data (around 1200 MB) into one 64 MB cartridge to bring one of the best survival horror games ever made to the 64-bit console.

Playing as Leon Kennedy or Claire Redfield, the game continues where the original left off, with Racoon City overrun with zombies caused by the T-Virus outbreak, and you get to run and gun around far bigger and more immersive environments.

Biological weapons, zombies, mutants, and jump scares fuel this heart-pounding spectacle, but always keep an eye on your ammunition count, because it can get scarce out there!

Shadow Man - Acclaim - August 31st, 1999

"Come on, slow coach!" Voodoo warrior Shadow Man Michael LeRoi enters Deadside, meeting with his long dead brother Luke, to stop an immortal army from crossing into Liveside in an attempt to prevent a world-ending apocalypse.

The game is based on the comic book series by Valiant Comics and sees you battling five bosses and consuming their souls before confronting the final boss, Legion.

Brutal executions and a story that features demonic rituals, Jack the Ripper and Michael's dead brother's teddy bear; Shadow Man has a compelling, gritty narrative that will keep you on edge throughout.

HONORABLE MENTION: Yakochu II Satsujin Koro - Athena - October 22, 1999

Set at sea, you, a student, survive an explosion on the maiden voyage of the luxury liner "The Dynasty". Once rescued, you witness strange murders aboard your saviour vessel, "The Pandora", and have to solve the conundrum as to who is committing them and why you are there.

With a choose-your-own-adventure visual novel structure, you get to pick through branching options to reach the end, and as a result, there are over 30 endings, and each subsequent playthrough yields new scenes and mysterious elements to discover and explore. There are 2330 screens of text in this game and reportedly 60,000 images!

Sadly, the fan translation project of Yakochu II Satsujin Koro, or Mareel II The Murder Line, is unfinished, but with the help of online translated documents and Google Translate's camera feature, you can adequately enjoy this tense and intriguing murder mystery thriller.

Haunting Highlights

So there we have it. A fine selection of gory/horror/murder/mystery games to give you goosebumps during the spooky season, with some genuinely cracking titles available to play.

Games like Scooby-Doo: Classic Creep Capers and Mortal Kombat Mythologies: Sub-Zero didn't make the cut because they're genuinely terrible, and offer no fun or genuine thrill for accomplished players.
