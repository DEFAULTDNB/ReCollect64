Dr. Mario 64 Recomp+ Released

Pill Poppin'

Following strongly behind the announcement just yesterday that the USA ROM of Harvest Moon 64 has now been 100% successfully decompiled comes news that Dr. Mario 64 is following suit, and already a recompilation effort called Dr. Mario 64 Recomp Plus has surfaced on theboy181's GitHub.

Utilising the decompilation efforts of AngheloAlf's repository, which publicly sits at 98.58% complete for the USA ROM as of the time of writing, Theboy181 has released the first implementation of a static recompilation, meaning you only require the linked executable below and the USA *.Z64 ROM (MD5: 1A7936367413E5D6874ABDA6D623AD32) to get playing.

 

Overview

Dr. Mario 64 Recomp is a modernized recompilation build focused on improved rendering, smoother motion, and quality-of-life enhancements while remaining faithful to the original game.

Highlights (v1.0.0)

Custom icon set (red and blue capsule)

Updated main menu with tuned default Recomp64 settings

Pills are now rendered on the GPU instead of the CPU

Interpolation support for smooth 60+ FPS gameplay

4-player controller support

CRT effects support

Requirements

Dr. Mario 64 (US) ROM

(A clean, vanilla US ROM is required at runtime).

3 Competitive friends.

Known Issues

Very lightly tested overall

Daily Dosage

With Dr. Mario 64 joining the wonderfully ever-growing list of recompilation projects, it's interesting to note that this one in particular supports four players off the bat and has had a slew of improvements made to it that gifts us with CRT-style scanlines, a framework for mod support, silky smooth 60 FPS gameplay and GPU-rendered GFX where it best improves the gameplay.

Dr. Mario 64 was only released in the USA on the Nintendo 64 towards the end of the console's lifespan in 2001, though notably the game also saw a release in Brazil via Gradiente and eventually landed again on the iQue in China in 2003. The game never reviewed very favourably, with critics citing difficulty and dullness as reasons to get the titular Dr. struck off.

The project is already off to a great start, but let's hope that a community of N64 fans and tech-savvy hobbyists can inject some life into the physician puzzle game after 24 years!
