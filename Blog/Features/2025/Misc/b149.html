MK64 Dreamcast Port WIP

DreamKarts

Making use of the Mario Kart 64 decompilation efforts, jnmartin84, of Doom 64 DC port fame, has begun porting the game to Sega's Dreamcast console. The news, shared this morning by KallistiOS contributor Falco Girgis, was accompanied by a brief video which demonstrated the state of the port as it currently stands after just three weeks of working on it.

Though understandably the graphics are a little garbled at the moment and there is no audio, this is a monumental achievement to see the 64-bit game running natively on the homebrew-friendly 128-bit console. Prior to this, the only way to play MK64 on a Dreamcast was through emulation, though STRMNNRMN's Daedalus N64 emulator was ported to Dreamcast by Simpson474 in 2008; it was far from perfect, so it was over 17 years ago that this project existed!

There is currently no ETA as to when this port might be released in a playable state, so stay tuned for further updates as they arrive!

UPDATE: 05/07/2025 - Sonic has taken the place of Toad, with jnmartin84 demonstrating modding and character support for the DC incarnation.
