WIP Progress by Rinnegatamante ~ May 22, 2025
Perfect Dark PS Vita Port

Perfectly Portable

Sony Vita homebrew developer Rinnegatamante has today shared an updated WIP of his Perfect Dark port for the 2011 PlayStation Vita handheld console.

The port, which is a fork of fgsfdsfgs' repository, demonstrates shader cache implementation and improved audio codepath.

 

This is running on an almost stock perfect-dark repository with vitaGL's GLSL->CG transpiler and shader cache implemented.

vitaGL having a computational cost of 28ms over 30 frames (less than 1ms per frame).

Really satisfied of vitaGL state nowadays :P.

Work in Progress

The video shows a fluctuating 9-60 frames per second depending on the amount of action on screen however, most importantly, the game already looks incredible with transparencies, light flares, particle effects, explosions, reflections and textures looking spot on.

The demonstration of the first level also shows that the controller scheme is mapped in nicely and the game, though unoptimised, is playable with menus, weapon select, dialogue and enemies present and correct. Imagine what it could be like with some tweaking!

Though there is no announced release date yet, Rinnegatamante is usually quite reliable for releasing homebrew and ports on the Vita, so hopefully we might see something we can publicly play with in the coming months.
