MK Mythologies: Konfirmed

A Frosty Reception

When Mortal Kombat Mythologies: Sub-Zero first debuted, I think it's fair to say that critics and fans alike were distinctly divided. While some saw this as an interesting and puzzle-fuelled break from the traditional format, others saw it as a poorly made yet punishing "Kash-in" of a beloved franchise which ultimately tainted the whole idea of creating more "Mythologies" games as its own spin-off series.

Ultimately, no more "Mythologies" games were made, but today, with the Mortal Kombat Legacy Kollection due to release before the year is out, Ed Boon and co. are still teasing more upcoming "Kontent" for the game, and today, eagle-eyed fans and modders @TheThiny and @TheCl0wn85 have provided possible proof of the Marmite-like game "Koming" soon to the "Kollection", along with Mortal Kombat: Special Forces.

Killer Kollective

The leaks, though unconfirmed, are interesting nonetheless, as Mortal Kombat: Mythologies Sub-Zero was only released for the PlayStation and N64 in 1997, so this iteration is possibly an HD port of either one or the other version, with the PlayStation outing providing a superior experience thanks to the included live-action cutscenes on its spacious disc, though the N64 version's loading times were significantly faster due to the cartridge format.

The Mortal Kombat Legacy Kollection is already set to feature MK1,MK2, MK3, Ultimate MK3, Mortal Kombat Trilogy (PS version) as well as the arcade edition of Mortal Kombat 4, and the GBA versions of MK: Advance, Deadly Alliance, and Mortal Kombat: Tournament Edition. These new additions mean that the tally of games and versions included sits at around the 22 mark, with versions spanning SNES, Genesis, Game Gear, Arcade and Game Boy.
