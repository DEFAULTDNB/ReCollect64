WIP: Metroid 64

Project by Luto Akino ~ Apr 29, 2025

Phase One (Phazon)

When considering the Metroid games, one notable omission is the absence of an official Metroid title for the Nintendo 64. Well, game developers Luto Akino and their team aim to (partially) rectify that gap in the market with their own retro-inspired Unity 3D effigy of Metroid 64. The project, which will work on PC or any platform that supports Unity 3D, is a work in progress that recently appeared on my social media timeline, so I had to dig in and take a look!

To give you a little history, following on from 1994's Super Metroid on the SNES, N64 fans were eager to see their favourite intergalactic bounty hunter come to the 64-bit console. First mentioned in Next Gen Magazine in their May 1997 issue, Metroid 64, as it was tentatively named, would take the cinematic quality of Super Metroid's 16-bit worlds and expand it into a superior 64-bit 3D experience. Sadly, that never happened due to a reported inability to pull together a cohesive structure for the game and Metroid series director Yoshio Sakamoto stating that he was unable to envisage how to control Samus with the then brand-new controller.

Starting out around 2021, the Metroid 64 project began to take shape with Luto having built a similar run-and-gun 2D space platforming game, Killy from Mars. Wanting to see Samus in 3D, the footage of Luto's third-person perspective 3D test came to light on the 30th of March, 2022. The video contained a brilliantly animated Samus exploring a blue test environment, with the ability to jump, strafe, dash, flip, aim, fire, and use the bomb and morph ball (Maru Mari) power-ups.

Just three years later, the project has come on leaps and bounds, with Samus's model being reworked by Adrian Garcia and a full, beautifully eerie soundtrack provided by composer Kassil. Take a listen to the Metroid 64 opening theme here:

Tanked Up

The game now has a full HUD with improved aiming and lock-on reticles, segments for weaponry and special attacks, and Samus has a few new moves up her sleeve. Manoeuvres such as a ledge grip, the ability to roll up cliffs and bomb-jump in her morph ball, and even some close-quarters melee kicks duplicated directly from Super Smash Bros. You can check out the latest footage on Luto's YouTube channel or check out developer notes on his Instagram, but one of my favourite demonstrations was uploaded recently in March, 2025.

From the footage below, you can see there are patrolling enemy insects to target and blast, such as Mellows, Zebs, Dessgeegas, Zoomers, and Skrees, with individual attacking behaviours, a fully working minimap, a textured environment, lighting effects and an overall colour palette of dusky blues and neon oranges and greens that gives the visuals a far more authentic Metroid feel. Check it out:

A Prime Fusion Returns

The project looks somewhere between a demake of Metroid Prime for the GameCube and a 3D version of Metroid: Zero Mission for the GBA, but with its very own story being fleshed out through storyboarding and the imagination and creativity of the developer.

Having contacted Luto, it seems that the current progress of Metroid 64 (which is unreleased) is incompatible with the N64; however, the assets and ideas are so compelling that perhaps someone with N64 coding skills might one day make it a reality. As Luto pointed out during the 2022 Game Jam, there is definitely space to expand the N64's catalogue, and a universal engine with which to develop new games for the system would be beneficial to players and developers alike.

Let's pray to the N64 gods!
