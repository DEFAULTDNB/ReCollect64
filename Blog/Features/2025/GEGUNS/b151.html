3D Printable GE007 Guns!

Weapons of Mass Delight

GoldenEye 007 had some of Bond's most iconic weaponry ever seen, so it only makes sense to celebrate them in 3D-printable form, right?

In May 2025, Daniel Lyell, known for his Peaches Castle Diorama from Super Mario 64 made that a reality by designing and printing a couple of the iconic guns from GoldenEye 007 in exquisite style.

The PP7 Special Issue, Bond's signature weapon, has been lovingly recreated in black filament, with the silver and gold editions alongside it. Together with the removable clip and silencer, these three guns look incredible on their presentation stands.

 

This is Bond’s iconic sidearm and the very first weapon you equip in the game. Whether you're sneaking through the Dam or lining up clean headshots, the PP7 is as classic as it gets.

This model was created using the in-game design as a base and working my way through making it easily printable. I've kept it true to the original polygonal shape only adding a few small details but retaining that blocky N64 charm.

It’s roughly life-size (based off the Walther PPK) and fits comfortably in-hand. The game never shows the magazine, I've included 2 variants, One flat and One angled (nicer to grip) Only the flat will fit if using the Stand.

All of the guns are printed to scale, and as you can see, they fit perfectly in hand to replicate that suave MI6 operative look when aimed at anything.

The RC-P90 is a rapid-firing submachine gun that made its appearance in the Train, Jungle, and Caverns levels. It's one of the most powerful guns in the game and this rendition has even been printed with a detachable magazine.

 

This [RC-P90] model was built from the ground up, starting with the original in-game geometry. I did my best to keep it game-accurate, meaning it may not make logical sense as a real firearm, but it looks the part.

The polygonal, blocky look of the original model has been preserved, with a few small added details to help it stand out as a shelf piece. It’s close to life-size and works great as a costume prop.

Whereas the PPKs take 3 hours per print on a single plate each, the RC-P90 takes a whopping 15 hours across four plates due to the multiple coloured filaments and intricate details like the magazine, foregrip, and buttstock.

Reaching out to Daniel, he stated that he's probably going to do more guns from the 64-bit armoury, so keep your eyes peeled for more of his fantastic prints!

All the 3MF/STL/CAD files are available, free of charge, on Daniel's MakerWorld profile (Arniel86) below, so if you want some slick props to adorn your gaming space, get on this because they're incredible!
