SuperBrawl64 in Development

Heavyweight Heat

Following the release of Virtual Pro Wrestling 64 Mod, Sh1t We Wanted to Add Vol 1, Wrestling Modders, Inc. recently shared some information on their latest project, SuperBrawl64.

This modification of THQ's wrestling game WWF No Mercy (2000) has no set release schedule because it is in the very early stages of development. It is also noted that it will only work on emulators, not real hardware, due to the sheer mass of textures and information being added in. The recommended emulator will be Project 64 to guarantee compatibility and provide end users with a smooth ride.

To span the first five SuperBrawl events broadcast between 1991 and 1995, this mod will feature an action-packed roster, each with attire consistent with their appearances within those events. Some "creative liberty" will be used to fill in gaps for those who, canonically, did not appear in one or more of the five extravaganzas.

Pinning Your Hopes

The lead developer of SuperBrawl64 (or possibly "SuperBrawl LXIV") is currently in the process of tracking down colour portraits, updating character faces and hair, and crafting bespoke outfits for every individual. There is a possibility that they will also be adding custom arenas, belts, moves, and more!

With multiple other projects in development from Wrestling Modders, inc., it does mean a lengthy wait might be inevitable before this sees any form of release.
