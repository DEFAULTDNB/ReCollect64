Analogue 3D Q4 Confirmation

Assured Shipping

With just 77 days remaining in 2025, Analogue has dropped a nice little update on their social sites that affirms preorder customers' hopes that the product will ship sooner rather than later. Having already suffered several setbacks, the company is doubling down on their latest promise and giving people a glimmer of hope that no more delays are to be expected.

The timing of this update is suspiciously relative to ModRetro's recent update that their M64 64-bit FGPA system will be playable at this weekend's Portland Gaming Expo. The rivalry, though unspoken, is clearly deep within the atmosphere, and it's interesting to see what the two companies will bring to the table.

Analogue 3D October Update:

1. Analogue 3D remains on target to ship in Q4 of this year.

2. There is no change in price or added cost for all open preorders.

Previous Promises

With preorder customers, such as myself, anticipating an email one week prior to shipping, it is nearly time to lock in and get hands-on with the console after exactly one year to the day that it was first announced.

I personally cannot wait to see the thing in real life and check out its features and benefits. Having never experienced FPGA gaming, this will be a first for me, so I am very much looking forward to putting it through its paces whenever it may eventually arrive on my doorstep.

Let us hope that there will be no more delays and no issues with the final units from here on out, as potential customers have already been put off by delays and lack of communication from the company over the last 365 days. Analogue has yet to post a single N64 hype gif, despite the console purportedly releasing in the near future.
