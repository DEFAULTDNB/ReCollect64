Mario Builder 64 1.1 Released

Making Marios

Today, Arthurtilly and Rovertronic have updated their Super Mario 64 level-editing ROM hack Mario Builder 64 to V1.1. The latest version features quality-of-life improvements as well as a massive changelog of features, which you can check out here:

 

Changelog for V1.1:

- Increased vertex limit from 40,000 to 50,000

- Increased tile limit from 10,000 to 20,000

- Optimized vertex generation by up to 25%

- New boundary type: Interior

- New object: On-Off Conveyor

- New object: Inverted Timed Box

- New object: Star Trigger

- New Sample Tile building shortcut by pressing L & R

- Ability to place multiple objects/tiles in the same spot

- Dialog to exit a level after collecting every star

- Added a speedrun timer option in the pause menu

- Added a Star Radar option in the pause menu

- Added 5 new level templates

Conveyors

- Added thin and sloped conveyors

- Added red/blue on-off conveyors

- The underside of conveyors are now hangable

- Improved conveyor physics

Imbuing System

- All enemies can be given objects to drop on death

- Objects can be placed inside ! Boxes

- Can now place Red Coins and Green Coins inside objects

- Can now place Badges and Powerups inside objects

- Dropped objects appear at the object's last safe location

- Boxes with yellow coins drop 3 coins instead of 1

- Boos and Moneybags show the item they will drop

- New Star Trigger can only be placed inside objects - when all Star Triggers are activated, a star will spawn, Star Triggers can also be placed inside coins and badges

Badge Changes

- Removed Star Radar badge in favor of a pause menu option

- Star Radar consistently always shows the closest star

- Star Radar shows red coins if all stars are collected

- Buffed Heal Plus badge to apply to all coins

- Buffed/renamed Double Damage badge to One Hit badge

- Buffed Defense badge to spend 1 mana per hit instead of 4

- Buffed Burn badge to not consume mana

Editor Tweaks

- Ability to hide help text in screenshot mode

- Screenshot mode has been moved to the System menu

- Custom Theme menu shows slipperiness of current tile

- Display the current number of placed objects in the editor when placing Stars or Red Coins

- Objects now have shadows in the editor

- Improved vertex/tile/object limit display

- Better warnings for vertex limit and having FBE disabled

- Improved cursor wrapping around the sides of the level

- Faster navigation between level pages using left/right

- Muting music no longer requires a level reset

- Added the Bowling Ball to the BTCM gamemode

Gameplay Tweaks

- Objects can trigger Activated Moving Platforms

- Wooden Platforms form a stack when placed vertically

- Bowling Balls can destroy boxes and kill enemies

- Hammer Bro's hammers can destroy boxes and kill enemies

- Throwable and Crazy Boxes respawn after being broken

- Using a Noteblock no longer forces you to drop objects or stop riding a shell

- Koopa the Quick respawns after losing the race

- Nerfed the Showrunner

- Collecting a Power Star fully heals Mario

- Bowser no longer instantly dies to lava

- BTCM Powerups can be used simultaneously

- The crowbar can hit multiple objects at once

- The crowbar can be used to pick up coins

- Heavily improved Thwomp physics and interactions

- Physics given to Mr. Blizzard and Piranha Plants

- AI and physics improvements for all enemies

Improved Assets

- Better texture mapping for slopes

- Better object models for Wooden Platform, On-Off Block, On-Off Switch, Reinforced Box, Fire Spinner

- Improved textures: Quicksand, Cosmic Void, Rocky Dirt, Green Rock, Ocean Floor, Scorched Pillar, Hazard Stripes

- Improved certain music ports (thanks to Pixelissimo)

Bug Fixes

- BTCM void respawn mechanic not working

- Bottomless badge having no effect

- Incorrect star IDs with 8 or more stars

- Infinite coin exploit from Blue Coin previews

- Trajectory data not resetting between levels

- Crash when climbing poles with Mario cam

- Wooden platforms not squishing Mario

- Softlock on top of fences at the level boundary

- Lava bubbles appearing far below the level

- Glitchy bully physics in midair and on snow/ice

- Bug allowing unlimited stars to be placed

- Failure to load levels on some flashcarts

- String overflow glitches in level and author names

- Periods not being allowed in level names

- Bad handling of invalid level files

- Level files corrupting if the vertex limit is exceeded

- Broken menus with widescreen viewport hack enabled

- BTCM templates selecting the wrong music tracks

- Infinite flight time bug with the Bullet Bill Mask

- Fire spinners having the incorrect rotation ingame

- Broken boss music with multiple bosses

- Exiting on a moving platform not resetting inertia

- HUD issues with the air bubble and rocket fuel meters

- Spinning around when flying after sliding down a pole

- Broken camera when collecting a badge while flying

- Cosmic Phantasms copying model state from Mario

- Countless minor physics and AI fixes

Competitive Creations

To celebrate this release, the crew over at RHDC has put together a prize-laden contest! The rules are simple: build a level in Mario Builder 64 and submit it by JULY 15th, 11:59 PM (Pacific Time Zone).

With the editor doing the heavy lifting, the barrier to entry is significantly reduced in terms of entering this contest; you literally need no ROM hacking knowledge whatsoever, just a creative mind and the ability to put together something cohesive!

 

Create a level within Mario Builder 64 for the competition. This competition has no theme, so you may make whatever type of level you like.

Your level MUST be made on Mario Builder 64 v1.1 or later.

You should aim to have all the stars in your level be collectable in 10-15 minutes if you want to guarantee that the audience will see all of it.

Your level MUST have genuine effort applied (poor quality levels made in a few minutes will not be streamed).

Your level MUST be created solely for the sake of this competition, and you may only enter once.

Your level MUST have a thumbnail (captured ingame using the Set Level Thumbnail option).

Submit your levels at https://romhacking.com/submit/mb64#e16af634-bb68-43c0-b0ab-870a290705b8.

First place winners stand to get $500 prize money, second place $350, third place $250, fourth and fith spots can pick up $200, and sixth to tenth place winners will also win $100!
