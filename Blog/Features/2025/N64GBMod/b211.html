GBA/N64 Hybrid Spotted!

Handheld Hybrid Heaven

Imagine cramming an entire Game Boy Micro into an N64 controller and making it fully functional and portable to boot. That's precisely what Reddit user MaSaKee has done, and the pictures have to be seen to be believed!

The memory pak slot has been repurposed to allow you to plug in 32-bit Game Boy Advance cartridges (but sadly not OG Game Boy or Bame Boy Colour carts), and the entire workings have been completely strung out to work seemlessly with the N64 OEM controller layout. There is a speaker in the middle prong along with a 3.5mm headphone jack on the underside, a USB-C charge port and on/off switch where the cable would otherwise be tethered, and last but not least, there is a perfectly placed 2" screen planted squarely in the middle with LED indicator lights by its side to signal power and charge status.

Regarding the extraneous buttons in comparison to a GB Micro, the C-buttons niftily control volume and brightness, and the Z-trigger has been remapped to be the select button. It's all rather genius work from the hobbyist creator MaSaKee!

The net result is a gorgeous portable that has the comfortability of the N64's controller with the portability of the GB Micro, a stunning build which I think is incredibly gutsy and extremely clever!

Now You're Playing With Power

With visions of the ill-fated Hyperkin N64 portable as well as Project of the Week's brilliant DIY project in mind, this version does make me wonder what the possible theoretical limit is nowadays when it comes to creative minds packing existing consoles into tiny form factors. Probably not a miniaturised home console, but certainly portable consoles with similarly sized screens could be cannibalised and repurposed in a similar fashion.

Could we see something more versatile for emulation like the Miyoo Mini+, Mod Retro, Retroid, or perhaps an Anbernic device dissected and reconfigured into this form? The mind boggles, but the more powerful and compact the device, the more mileage you could possibly get out of it, especially with modern high-capacity batteries and storage solutions for playing ROMs!
