Don't Lego of the Past

Retro nostalgia reigns these days. More people want to zone out and relive the glory days rather than remain stuck in today's headspace. I enjoy surrounding myself with things that make me feel good inside, which is why I have a massive N64 collection and this site!

Lego enthusiast James, aka IBrickedItUp, has been building awesome miniature Lego creations since at least 2021, with a staggering 820 MOCs (My Own Creations) to his name. From vehicles to modular buildings, characters, diaramas and retro technology; James has built it all. The range of vehicles on the site is stunninga alone, with literally hundreds of cars, trucks and transporters from various movie franchises.

I spotted a couple of Nintendo 64-themed goodies that I wanted to share here on ReCollect64 because the way they are built is interesting. They're minimalist, almost impressionist, and though they all share the same scale, they are extremely simple yet cleverly combined. Every single one is iconic and easily recognisable as a good reflection of the original item.

Bargain Builds

The detail that emanates from these bricks is superb. The console has a controller built to scale, and there is a fine selection of instantly recognisable games, including Donkey Kong 64, Super Mario 64, Pilotwings 64, and The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time.

There is a nice collection of retro builds on James's Rebrickable link, with an OG Microsoft "Xblox", the Zoltar Machine from the 1988 movie 'Big', the infamous dangling scene from 1996's 'Mission: Impossible', plus a selection of action-figure wrestling characters that are built to be fully posable.

If you haven't given James a follow already, then I highly recommend that you do; his body of work is magnificent, and the inclusion of the Nintendo 64 hardware and software is outstanding.
