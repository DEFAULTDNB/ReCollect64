Shadow Man and Turok 2: Seeds of Evil have been released as part of Nintendo's "Mature 17+" NSO N64 service. The two games are today joining the current catalogue of N64 titles, bringing the total to 39 games to enjoy if you pay for the Expansion Pak!

I only recently replayed the Shadow Man remaster by Night Dive Studios (and I replayed Turok and Turok 2 a couple of years before that), and the experience was sublime because of all the quality of life upgrades made by the team. Nintendo's NSO service will not feature the remasters, and so will not feature QOL changes seen in the remasters; instead, the games will be emulated and will no doubt feature the same graphical oddities and audio quirks that the NSO service has become known for.

The YouTube trailer HERE demonstrates a few seconds of the four player splitscreen multiplayer modes in Turok 2: Seeds of Evil and the enhanced resolution offered by re-playing these games via the Nintendo Switch emulation service.

Awesomely, Graslu00 has already provided a brief analysis of what, if anything, has changed within the "Mature 17+" NSO app. He noted that the updated emulation of Perfect Dark in particular has improved input delay, blur, and UV effects, though the sound mastering is still quiet and it still has many frame drops throughout gameplay.

At least it feels like Nintendo is trying to fix things gradually, but is it too slow and too late after the initial impact of the games arriving feeling like lazily emulated efforts?

Seed Your Evil

Online multiplayer is bound to be a blast; however, I'm dubious about input lag with the NSO N64 controller paired with emulation quirks marring the experience.

With Nintendo pushing the "Mature 17+" variant of the NSO app, does this mean we will see more 18 rated N64 games coming to the hybrid handheld? It should be feasible to predict games such as Turok 3: Shadow of Oblivion, Turok Rage Wars, and Forsaken eventually hitting the service, but will we ever see titles like Resident Evil 2, Mace: the Dark Age, Duke Nukem: Zero Hour, Daikatana, or perhaps the three Mortal Kombat games? I guess it depends on the licenses and rights of the parent companies to figure out a way to achieve this with Nintendo somehow.

Rare's N64 swansong, Conker's Bad Fur Day, remains the one that everybody wishes to see return, but sadly that day too may never come. With Rare being owned by Microsoft, it remains to be seen if they can wrangle the rights to get the titular squirrel back on Nintendo hardware, albeit emulated, for one last booze-fuelled escapade, even though Banjo-Tooie just arrived on NSO (October 25th, 2024) to rapturous praise from long-time fans!
