N64History Timeline

Inspired by the wonderful PS3History by GregoryRasputin, aka PlaystationHax, I have attempted to collate as much information as possible into a timeline of events for my favourite console. The N64 saw several hardware revisions and had exclusive console and controller variants, as well as 388 games officially released during a seven-year lifespan from 1996 to 2002. In the surrounding years, there was plenty of 64-bit action, with announcements and partnerships as early as 1993 and brand new 4K FPGA versions of the hardware coming out in 2025.

Incorporating information about hardware, games, accessories, software, homebrew, flashcarts, emulation, services and more, this page aims to give an authentic look at the console over the years as well as plenty of additional fascinating statistics and facts to muse upon.

Jump to Year:

1993

August 23 - Nintendo and SGI form a partnership and "Project Reality" is announced. It was set to be as powerful as 100 PCs for the low price of $250.

The SGI Onyx would be used for the earliest N64 development, using NinGen software, a 3D modeling package from MultiGen.

1994

"Project Reality" was renamed to "Ultra 64" ahead of Shoshinkai in November 1996.

Cruis'n USA first released in arcades. Developed by TV Games Inc, published by Nintendo under the "Ultra 64" name on Midway's V-unit hardware.

1995

May - The consoles release date was pushed back from 1995 to April 1996, to allow studios more time to develop games.

May 15 - The N64 was unveiled at E3 in Los Angeles, USA, with several games showcased (Super Mario 64, WaveRace 64, PilotWings 64, Star Wars: Shadows of the Empire, Cruis’n USA, Mortal Kombat and Killer Instinct).

November - Super Mario 64 gameplay information was leaked ahead of the official reveal at Shoshinkai.

November 15 - The Ultra 64 is shown off at Shoshinkai, Japan, with 15 games demonstrated but only 2 playable. The Nintendo 64DD was announced with a release date anticipated for late 1996.

November 22 The 64DD was shown at Shoshinkai but not in the spotlight. The release date for the add-on was pushed back to late 1997 in Japan.

The "Ultra 64" was renamed to "Nintendo 64" (N64) to avoid issues with the Konami owned "Ultra Games" licence.

1996

February - Killer Instinct 2 debuted in arcades. Developed by Rare, published by Nintendo under the "Ultra 64" name on customised Midway hardware.

February 1 - Nintendo pushes the consoles release date back again, citing parts shortages.

June 23 - N64 launched in Japan 🇯🇵

June 23 - Super Mario 64, Pilotwings 64, and Saikyo Habu Shogi released in Japan.

September 29 - N64 launched in North America 🇺🇸

September 29 - Super Mario 64 and Pilotwings 64 released in North America.

November 22 - Super Mario DD, Earthbound 64, Yoshi's Island 64 and Zelda 64 footage shown at Shoshinkai.

November 22 - The Jolting Pak (Rumble Pak) was also demonstrated at Shoshinkai.

The N64 featured 64-bit NEC VR4300 MIPS, SGI 62.5 MHz 64-bit RCP, 4MB memory, 240i/480i, Cartridge ROM based media.

The Dr V64 was released by Bung Enterprises Ltd. This was the first backup device released, was based on the 6502 chipset, and was capable of playing CD/VCD as well as ROMs from the built-in CD-ROM drive (US$450). It was used as a cheaper alternative to Nintendo's development tools by Acclaim/Iguana for the Turok series of games.

GameShark released by InterAct in North America.

1997

March 1 - N64 launched in Europe 🇪🇺

March 1 - Super Mario 64, Pilotwings 64, Star Wars: Shadows of the Empire, Turok: Dinosaur Hunter, FIFA Soccer 64, and Wayne Gretzky's 3D Hockey released in Europe.

April 27 - Rumble Pak launched with Star Fox 64.

June 22 - Star Fox Competition N64 Controller given away at E3. It has a gold Nintendo 64 logo on the top, and roughly 100 were given out to winners who competed in a Star Fox 64 themed event.

July 19 - Hyundai Comboy 64 released in Korea 🇰🇷

September - Nintendo Power 100 Gold OEM controller purchasable in North America and Canada via the Super Power Supplies catalog that came with issue #100.

December 10 - Nintendo 64 released in Brazil with distribution through Gradiente/Playtronic 🇧🇷

Mr Backup Z64 released by Harrison Electronics, Inc. The device was able to dump ROMs to a Zip Disk drive, and could also play backups. There was a second revision of the hardware released to handle the reading and dumping of larger games, but no final revision was ever produced to handle 512 Mbit cartridges.

Action Replay 64 released by Datel in Europe.

Gold OEM controller launches internationally.

Hello Mac OEM controller launches in Japan as a Hello Mac store exclusive.

Geoffrey OEM controller launches in Japan as a Toys R Us exclusive.

1998

February 27 - Project Unreality, the first N64 emulator released by BlackBag.

March Project 64 N64 emulator for Windows was first devised by Zilmar.

March 22 - CD64 add-on released by UFO to play backups from CD-ROM drive with 128Mb-256Mb memory (HK$1150-HK$1400).

April 22 - GB Hunter released to play Game Boy games on N64.

May 25 - Project Unreality emulator's final release V0.4a-1.

August 1 - Transfer Pak released with Pokemon Stadium.

September 11 - NINCEST 64 emulator for Windows is released by Marius Dumitrean.

November 13 - Bio Sensor released for use with Tetris 64 by Seta.

December 12 - Voice Recognition Unit released in Japan with Pikachu Genki Dechu.

December 15 - 4MB Expansion Pak released at $29.95

The Dr V64 Jr was released by Bung Enterprises Ltd. It used battery backup and parallel port loading intead of CD to keep costs down.

Pro Action Replay released by Karat in Japan

Gold N64 console released in Japan as a Toys R Us exclusive.

Seta and Nintendo produce Aleck64 arcade hardware (5101 CIC).

Extreme Green OEM controller launches as a North American exclusive.

Atomic Purple OEM controller launched in North America and Japan (known as Clear Purple).

1999

June - ANA edition console given away to lucky All Nippon Airways customers in conjunction with Pokmon Jet Part 2.

July 30 - Densha De Go Train controller released in Japan.

December 1 - Randnet service goes live.

December 1 - 64DD released in Japan.

Jusco edition console released in Japan to celebrate Jusco's 30th anniversary.

Daiei Hawks edition console released in Japan to celebrate the baseball team winning the Japan Series in 1999.

Nintendo Power DK Banana OEM controller launches as part of the DK64 promotion. Less than 5000 made, NA exclusive.

Nintendo Power Silver Millenium 2000 OEM controller to celebrate Y2K. Only 1000 made, and has black coloured buttons, NA exclusive.

2000

February 23 - 64DD Randnet Disk released.

March 20 - Funtastic wave 1 released in Europe with six colours (Watermelon Red, Ice Blue, Fire Orange, Jungle Green, Grape Purple and Smoke Grey).

April 21 - F-Zero X Expansion kit released for the 64DD add-on, allowing players to create their own tracks.

June - Chinese law prohibiting the sale of home video games introduced.

June 22 - Tristar64 released by Future Laboratory Hong Kong. It allows you to play NES and SNES carrtidges on an N64 console.

July 21 - Orange Pikachu edition console released in Japan as a Toys R Us exclusive.

July 21 - Blue Pikachu edition console released internationally.

August 29 - Mario Artist Polygon Studio DD released for the 64DD add-on. It was the last game released for the system.

September 15 - Blue Pikachu edition console released in Japan.

September - LodgeNet service rolled out to 1000 hotels, offering 38 games on a pay-per-view service. The hardware incorporated an Expansion Pak (for MM & DK), a flashable CIC, and a tool to wipe any residual save information. The controller featured buttons that facilitated ordering room service.

November 6 - Voice Recognition Unit released in USA with Hey You, Pikachu!.

Pokemon Stadium Battle Set console released in Australia, UK and Scandinavia.

2001

Feb 28 - 64DD's online service Randnet was shutdown.

May 26 - Project64 initially released by Zilmar, Shygoo and Azimer.

June 5 - Daedalus N64 emulator for Windows first released by .

December 10 - Mupen64 initially released by Hacktarux.

2002

April 30 - The N64 was discontinued, it had life time sales of 32.93 million units (5.54M JPN, 20.63M USA, 6.75M Other).

August 5 - Dream Leopard OEM controller painted by manga artist “Ashura” Benimaru Itoh for Nintendo Dream Magazine in Japan.

August 20 - Tony Hawk's Pro Skater 3 released exclusively to North America on a black cartridge. This was the last official game released for the system.

2003

Aleck64 hardware was retired from the arcades.

November 17 - iQue player released in China with Dr. Mario, The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time, Star Fox 64, Super Mario 64 and Wave Race 64. The games would be distributed by a network and written to memory card for use at home.

November 17 - iQue player Swim Box released that enabled local multiplayer between units.

December 25 - Mario Kart 64 released for iQue.

2004

February 25 - F-Zero X released for iQue.

March 25 - Yoshi's Story released for iQue.

May 21 - 1964 emulator 0.9.9 released by Rice and Schibo.

June 8 - Paper Mario released for iQue.

August 2 - GPF releases DCNin64 (alpha), an N64 emulator for Dreamcast. It is based on NINCEST 64 by Marius Dumitrean.

September 25 - Sin & Punishment released for iQue.

October 14 - Ares Emulator begins development.

November 21 - Super Mario 64 DS released for Nintendo DS. It was a remake of the 1996 original that featured four playable characters, Mario, Luigi, Yoshi, and Wario, as well as touchscreen controls.

December 7 - Ridge Racer DS released for Nintendo DS. This version was essentially a remake of Ridge Racer 64 that included touchscreen controls and wireless multiplayer.

2005

March 11 - Rayman released for DS. It was a remake of the N64 version Rayman 2: The Great Escape.

June 15 - Excitebike 64 released for iQue.

November 15 - Super Smash Bros. released for iQue.

2006

January 31 - Monkey 64 is announced by PSMonkey. It is based on NINCEST 64 by Marius Dumitrean.

March 10 - Monkey 64 V1.0, the first ever N64 emulator for PSP is released by PSMonkey.

May 1 - Custom Robo released for iQue.

May 10 - Daedalus N64 emulator begins being ported to PSP by STRMNNRMN.

May 26 - Neo N64 Myth Cart announced by NEOFlash Team. It would play backups from a NDS slot 2 cart 256 - 1024Mbit flash memory.

June 1 - Animal Crossing released for iQue.

November 19 - Wii Virtual Console began adding N64 games with Super Mario 64.

2007

February 5 - Diddy Kong Racing DS released for the Nintendo DS and was developed by Rare. This is a remake of Diddy Kong Racing that features Tiny Kong and Dixie Kong instead of Banjo and Conker. Taj and Wizpig are also unlockable playable characters, and there are stylus-based drawing mini games.

November - Information about Project Bean aka a Rare led GoldenEye 007 remaster for XBLA was leaked online.

2008

January - Gameplay footage from Project Bean, the GoldenEye 007 remaster for XBLA leaked. It showed three minutes of gameplay and confirmed the prior leaks credibility.

June - The official LodgeNet website is discontinued .

2009

June 7 - The final version (V1.1) of the N64 emulator 1964 is released. Active development ceases.

December 30 - Neo N64 Myth Cart released by NEOFlash Team for $199.00.

2010

June 14 - Nintendo announced a 3DS remake of Star Fox 64 at E3 2010

2011

March 4 - Wii Virtual Consoles last N64 game, Bomberman Hero, was released.

March 27 - Rayman 3D released for nintendo 3DS. It is a remake of the N64 game Rayman 2: The Great Escape, but based on the superior Dreamcast version.

July - MarshallH beta tests the 64Drive flashcart. It featured a Compact Flash slot for storing ROMs.

June 16 - The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time 3D was released for Nintendo 3DS. This remake includes a redesigned "shard of agony", green blood for Ganon and a mirrored Master Quest mode.

July 14 - Star Fox 64 3D was released for Nintendo 3DS. This version featured new voice lines and a 3DS difficulty setting however, it removed the Landmaster and secret on-foot mode from multiplayer.

Everdrive64 first released

2012

June - XRGB-Mini Framemeister released by Micomsoft. It is regarded as the best method for upscaling RGB to modern TVs.

2013

July - 64Drive released by Marshall H.

September 12 - ED64 V3.00 Released

The LodgeNet service officially ends.

2014

June 26 - The 64DD version of Super Mario 64 was discovered. Assumed to be the 1996 Shoshinkai tech demo disk, it has longer loading times than the cartridge version and very few notable differences other than some sound effects found in the 1997 Shindou edition of Super Mario 64.

2015

February 13 - The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask 3D was released on 3DS. This remake featured a redesigned inventory, gyro controls and a new side quest for a 7th empty bottle.

April 2 - Donkey Kong 64 was released on Wii U Virtual Console. To date, DK64 has not been released for Nintendo Switch Online's Expansion Pak.

June 21 - Ultra CIC released by Mike Ryan to allow region free copy protection on flash carts.

September 23 - Nemu64 by Lemmy and LaC, the successor to Daedalus, released by Purplemarshmallow.

December 5 - UltraHDMI hardware mod released by Marshall H, it allows resolutions of 480p, 720p, and 1080p once soldered into the console and an HDMI port cut into the shell.

2016

February 11 - GameShark codes to disable antialiasing (blur) started appearing on Assemblergames.

October 31 - iQue announces that iQue@Home service will be discontinued in December.

A prototype cartridge containing a 1999 build of Rev Limit (Seta/Aleck64/64DD) was sold on Yahoo! Auctions in Japan.

2017

March 9 - Bomberman 64 was the last game to be released on Wii U Virtual Console.

2018

March - The Nintendo Gigaleak occurred, exposing files, games and internal documents.

May 31 - Gigaleak files began filtering onto 4chan.

iQue content distribution servers finally went offline.

2019

April 15 - Eurocom and GT Interactive's 40 Winks was released on physical cartridge by Piko Interactive, following a Kick Starter campaign that exceeded the stretch goals.

July 22 EON released the plug-and-play Super64 HDMI adaptor. It features a button to toggle smoothing.

August 25 - Super Mario 64 (U) and (J) was successfully decompiled to C Source code with documentation.

November 30 - DaedalusX64 initially ported to PS2 by Belek666.

2020

April 4 - Saturnu releases an automatic antialiasing patcher for N64 ROMs.

May 3 - Shogihax remote code execution released by cTurt.

June 16 - ParaLLEl low-level RDP rendered with upscaling released for emulators.

July 24 - The second half of the Gigaleak, with 3 gigabytes of data.

July 24 - Super Mario 64 PC Port was first released.

July 25 - iQue ROMs found in Gigaleak documents.

November 6 - SummerCart 64 software was first uploaded to Polprzewodnikowy's GitHub.

December 25 - Project Bean, the GoldenEye 007 remaster for XBLA leaked in full. It was playable on Xenia emulator.

2021

February 20 - A Dinosaur Planet Prototype from December 1st, 2000, was shared. It was cracked by LuigiBlood to run on flashcarts and emulators.

February 22 - Everdrive 64 X5 released by Krikzz. The X series features Ultra CIC III.

June 8 - ParaLLEl RDP ported to Project64 and Mupen64Plus.

July 27 - Rosalie241 Mupen GUI first released.

September 9 - Everdrive 64 X7 released by Krikzz. This version features RTC clock, save without pressing reset and a USB socket for developers.

September 23 - Nintendo Switch Online + Expansion Pack announcement at the Nintendo Direct, which would add N64 games to the roster.

October 26 - Nintendo Switch Online's Expansion Pak is released with Super Mario 64, Mario Kart 64, Lylat Wars, Yoshi's Story, Sin & Punishment, The legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time, Dr. Mario 64, Mario Tennis, and Operation Winback.

2022

October 16 - DaedalusX64-3DS V1.0 port for Nintendo 3DS released by Masterfeizz.

2023

31 of the 38 LodgeNet games were dumped, decrypted and released by an anonymous hacker. The ROMs feature no ability to save.

September 4 - First version of N64FlashcartMenu uploaded to Polprzewodnikowy's GitHub.

2024

May 10 - N64Recomp officially begins on Mr-Wiseguy's N64: Recompiled Github repository. The tool aims to statically recompile any N64 ROM into a native executable, which is usable on PC with minimal effort. The Legend of Zelda Majora's Mask was released as a demonstration.

May 29 - ReCollect64 Blog first opens to the public, featuring news, reviews, Q&A chats and more!

October 1 - SummerCart64 officially releases and is purchasable on Amazon (Kaiko) and PhenomMod's store.

October 2 - Nintendo opened the Nintendo Museum with exhibits including a giant N64 controller for people to play Super Mario 64 with.

October 30 - Nintendo released the Nintendo Music App for mobile phones. It features official soundtracks from various titles, including N64 games.

2025

March 30 - Nintendo adds the OST from F-Zero X to the Nintendo Music App, featuring music from the 64DD F-Zero X Expansion Kit. This is the first time Nintendo has put out content related to the 64DD add-on since the year 2000.

July - Analogue set to release the Analogue 3D with 4K, HDMI, FPGA, 3DOS and 100% compatibility.
