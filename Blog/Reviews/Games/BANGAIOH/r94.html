Bakuretsu Muteki Bangaioh ~ Feb 18, 2026

Translated Treasures

Now that Roboverse Translations has kindly released their Bakuretsu Muteki Bangaioh translation patch, I feel it only apt to get stuck into a game I have never played before due to the language barrier.

Released in 1999, Treasure had built a game that meshed bullet-hell destruction with a fruit-collecting adventure, though due to a Japanese-only release run of a reported 10,000 copies, the game has garnered a well-deserved reputation as a rare title on the 64-bit hardware.

Projectile Pandemonium

You play as siblings Riki and Mami, who plan to thwart the nefarious Tiger Gang's ploy to smuggle fruit to finance evil schemes. Destruction is the name of the game. You literally need to destroy everything in sight and collect all the fruit as you progress through the levels. The more fruit you collect without getting hit, the higher the points value per piece, up to 1000 points each.

The story isn't that deep, but the 44 levels are varied and punctuated with some nice comic strip cutscenes with trash talk between the characters and the bosses, which builds the tension and pushes the flow of the game along nicely. Thank goodness for the translation patch, because I would not stand a chance of enjoying this without it!

Frantic Firepower

In-game, you can swap between Riki and Mami to utilise their unique traits when it comes to their projectiles. Riki has lock-on ammunition, and Mami has bouncing bullets that ricochet off walls and surfaces. I found Riki's lock-on missiles useful for tricky moving targets but Mami's projectiles for quickly obliterating static ones.

As well as smashing buildings and walls, hitting switches and busting turrets, you need to destroy enemies who are jetting around the levels. With you being able to deploy hundreds of missiles and the enemies spewing loads of bullets, the action can get extremely frantic very quickly unless you fully engage the twin-stick mentality of this title. You have 8 ways to move and 8 ways to shoot, so coordinating your movements to your artillery spray is critical to succeeding through the levels.

I really enjoyed the gravity physics in the game too, because you can jet across to somewhere, aim in one direction, move in the opposite one, and then float down to land on a platform while watching the explosions unfold. It's extremely satisfying when you're winning and in control of the situation, but each level gets progressively tougher, presenting more of a challenge and testing your reflexes each time.

2D or not 2D?

This is a fully 2D title, which means no fancy 3D boss fights and no massive sprawling arenas to explore. This isn't a bad thing; 2D games have their merit, and this game is a prime example of a 2D shooter done right. It's engaging and fun to play. It's sad that it isn't multiplayer; that might have amped it up a little more, especially for replayability.

The game can take up to 6 hours to finish, though completionists will be pleased to hear that it can take around 20 hours to clock everything, including extras. It took me around 6 hours to play through all stages, and I absolutely enjoyed every second of it.

Playing on real hardware, the game looks great, but emulated, the game had issues with maintaining full speed and stitching the 2D tiles together. There are options in emulators to fix the tiles displaying black edges, but I couldn't get it quite right, even just for the screenshots.

The Pros:

 44 frantically explosive stages and 11 bosses.
 Increasingly compilcated levels.
 Combo system is engaging.
The Cons:

 Bosses can be cheesed at range with homing missiles.
 Music is a bit repetitive & no multiplayer.
 Small scale sprites, but it helps the game to run smoothly.
Conclusion:

Bakuretsu Muteki Bangaioh is about the only two-dimensional bullet-hell, side-scrolling action shooter on the Nintendo 64, and it should be commended for mastering its craft on the system.

In an era where 3D was the norm, this 2D shooter manages to feels well made and fresh throughout, keeping you coming back for more. My only real gripe is a lack of two-player mode, and the music, which I really didn't enjoy.
