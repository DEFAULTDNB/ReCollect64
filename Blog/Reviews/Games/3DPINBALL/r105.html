3D Pinball N64 Port ~ Jul 23, 2026

Pinball Wizard

Spooky Iluha has graced us with an N64 port of the PC game 3D Pinball Space Cadet, which is based on the decompilation project that was completed in 2021. This port was released in April 2026, but it's only now that I have found out that it exists, so I need to share it, so that you can enjoy it too.

Default Controls:
Left FlipperRight FLipper
Nudge Nothing
Nothing New Game
Toggle Music Launch Ball
Pause Game

Rumble 'N' Tilt

Pinball was invented in 1869 and became a mainstream game around the early 1930s. Since then, almost every arcade, public bar or themepark attraction has sported a pinball table of some description to reel in the customers thanks to their dazzling lights, captivating themes and iconic mechanical sounds and digitised sound effects.

3D Pinball was originally developed by Cinematronics and Maxis and was bundled with every Microsoft Windows installation from 95 to Me and XP. This meant that every PC owner and their kids, from 1995 to 2001 had this game at their fingertips. The 2021 decompilation of that game has been deployed here and lovingly ported to the Nintendo 64 with all the original features recreated to redeliver us those retro vibes but with an extra twist: Rumble Pak support!

Launching the game, you see the table on the left and the informative high score table/display on the right. This layout is a necessity given the OG game and the N64's native 4:3 display ratio. Graphically, there isn't any fancy 3D stuff going on; it's actually a fully 2D game that resembles a 3D table with various elements lighting up and systematically leading you around the table to launch your fleet.

3D Pinball has fun permeating its core. It's a simple matter of keeping the ball in play and hitting all the targets. It's skilful, taxing, and most importantly, heavily replayable thanks to the constant chase for the highest scores. I'm not the best at pinball, but I really liked the layout and interactivity of this game, starting up various missions and levelling my lowly cadet up through the ranks.

Flipping Out

Playing on a 65" OLED through the Analogue 3D with the latest upscaling feels insane, especially when you condier that originally I would have only had a 15" monitor to play this on. It also runs perfectly on the OG N64 hardware too, even without an Expansion Pak. The game runs brilliantly, never falters, and most importantly, it never crashes or hangs. You must enable the "force original hardware" option on FPGA platforms due to how the game was crafted with Libdragon, but once that's set, you're good to go!

I thoroughly enjoyed the nostalgia of this game. It's so simple yet so replayable. Experiencing this as an adult hits different, especially if you were there to appreciate the whole Y2K era growing up. Everything was simpler, less fast-paced, and, to some extent, possibly more enjoyable because of it. I have to admit that adding rumble to this port elevates the experience too; if anything, it feels more immersive than I ever remember it being on PC.

The only thing people might baulk at with this port is the lack of tables. But since this is the bundled Windows version, it only ever had one table. Pinball 95/Full Tilt! Pinball (1995) contained 3 tables: Space Cadet, Skullduggery, and Dragon's Keep, so perhaps it would be great to see that version decompiled and source ported to the Nintendo 64 too, for just that little bit more variety.

The Pros:

 A vivid and faithful port.
 Easy to control with just L, R, and A.
 No Expansion Pak required.
 Lights, sounds and features.
 Rumble Pak support.
The Cons:

 Only one table.
Conclusion:

3D Pinball Space Cadet takes me back to a simpler time. Sure, there isn't much content here technically, but the replayability is infinite if you lock in.
