Crystal in the Rough

Telocation Gemini is, at its heart, an astonishingly polished 3D platforming puzzle title that sets your adventure against the background of space with just two objectives: collect crystals and return safely to your ship.

The devious puzzling action ramps up each level, and the motions you must perform to complete your tasks become more varied and more tactical. After a couple of levels, you find your robot buddy, and this changes the dynamic of the game from a simplistic run, jump, and collect game to a truly genius-level, brain-scratching, character-swapping series of missions.

Graphically, the game looks a little Loony Tunes-like, with a semi-cell-shaded look and colourful environments that pop with vivid colours. I really like the look and feel of this game; it's a high-quality brew!

Team Work / Dream Work

The latest version of the game, version 1.2, houses the full 20 levels, which span the basics of navigation, avoiding hazards, destroying rocks with your robot, pressing switches, triggering doors, travelling on sliding platforms and gathering your green crystals. Each level, though short, has something interesting to do or find within its relatively small environments.

Using your robot to destroy rocks, traverse hazardous surfaces, push big switches or provide a platform for the main character, Smaldrin, to stand on, the game continually escalates into more complicated scenarios that require both of you to get from point A to point B safely and using each other's speciality traits. Where the robot is oversized and brute-like, Smaldrin is tiny and nimble, able to squeeze through small gaps, press small switches, and leap across platforms with ease.

Switching characters with Z allows you to experiment with each character quickly and solve those tricky sticky situations. Thankfully there is no timer stressing you out, so you can take your time and figure out what needs doing at your leisure. I cannot decide if the addition of a timer or life count would ramp up the jeopardy nicely or simply make the game annoying to play. As it stands, it's relatively easy-going and an exploratively chilled experience.

Twenty Tiny Tests

As a puzzle game fan, I found this title exquisitely made and incredibly fun to get to grips with. There are 3 crystals scattered per level, so 60 to collect in total, and even though every stage gets trickier to overcome in a slow but sure fashion, you never feel too taxed or that there is simply no solution.

Level creation tools have since been built for the game, and as such you can either modify existing levels or build from scratch. The levels can then be packed into a playable ROM with ease, or you can upload and share your levels for the world to play!

The addition of a level editor is a stroke of genius too. The creators have given you the tools to extend the game, making it more complex, bigger, or more frustrating. When I blitzed the first 20 levels, I was genuinely disappointed that it was all over and done so quickly, well under an hour in fact. But adding new levels gives you infinite possibilities if you put in the effort.

Perhaps a level generator would have been a neater solution, and that way you wouldn't need to rebuild the ROM each time you wanted to play new levels? Regardless, Telocation Gemini is a brilliant game, and well worth checking out!

Conclusion:

Telocation Gemini is one of those homebrew games I never got around to playing, much to my detriment. The game is well made, fantastically engaging and expandable beyond the initial 20 levels.

I implore you to grab it from Team Ultra Rare's Itch.io and play it today!!!!
