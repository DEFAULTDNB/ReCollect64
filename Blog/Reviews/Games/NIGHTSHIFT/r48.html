Nightshift at the Beta Museum ~ Mar 27, 2025
Nightshift at the Beta Museum

Blue Collar Worker

I'm not going to lie; I left this alone for a week because I assumed it was an April Fool's, but it turns out I'm the fool for not clocking in to play this jumpy little mini-game of sorts sooner!

Kaze, Rovertronic and Cheesepin are back with a spooky scary SM64 ROM hack: Nightshift at the Beta Museum. This one has major Five Nights at Freddy's vibes, with you overseeing museum security from the comfort of your desk, using CCTV and a breaker to ensure no burglars get in and start wreaking havoc. Survive through the shift until 6am and you've won!

The premise is simple: just keep watch. If you see something, close the nearest door to prevent them coming towards you, flush the vents, or play a sound to distract them. The real challenge comes from managing your available assets in the breaker cupboard. You have to manage and replenish doors, audio and utilities as well as keep an eye out for crazy danger.

L is Real!

The game sees you protecting the assets of the Beta Museum, and those assets include some fun references to cut or misremembered content from Super Mario 64. I particularly liked how the console room's cartridge is actually the server that powers your surveillance system. Protecting assets, and yourself is the name of the game, so pay attention and don't get distracted.

Across the five shifts, you will encounter several different intruders. Now, I don't want to give everything away, but you will come up against some smaller enemies as well as some bigger "celebrity" cameos in an attempt to spook you out. I specifically enjoyed the "L is real" mythology and the creepy possessed version of Mario without any eyes. A lot of small details have gone into this hack; it's extremely well put together.

Tech Savvy

The game uses clever cues in an attempt to freak you out. For example, lights flicker, the telephone rings, you hear shouting, you hear a creak, and them boom, a character is right up in your grill screeching at you at full volume. It's quite exhilarating, but these sorts of games have never really been my go-to, so the novelty wore off quite quickly for me. Avid fans of the other games of a similar ilk might appreciate this genre of ROM hack far more than I do.

I like the fact it has a desk-job feel, but in its simplicity comes a restricted palette of abilities. The resource management aspect starts off interesting but ultimately becomes limited and repetitious very quickly. Panic-stricken plate-spinning is not exactly my idea of fun, but I could see the appeal for others who might enjoy it.

Exhibiting Issues

Though this title is technically very well made, it's a little lacklustre in the variety department. Sure, you can affect the path of the encroaching enemies by blocking doors and playing sounds, but ultimately it's a simplistic defence strategy to whittle away the hours. Worst case scanario, you can hold Z and hide under your desk too, I thought that was quite good and very Alien Isolation-esque. The five nights have five different stories and five different cameo characters, one per night, but ultimately, replaying a single night plays out remarkably similarly every single time.

If the game had slightly more randomness, I think it would have better replayability, and I would have had far more impetus to get stuck in again and again. As it was, I replayed the five levels around 3 times each, and that was more than enough for me.

The Pros:

 Good jump scares.
 Nice beta references.
 Very well produced.
 Audio clips are brilliant.
The Cons:

 Only five nights.
 Not very difficult to survive.
 No RNG per level, it's the same each replay.
Conclusion:

Five Nights at wait.. I mean.. Nightshift at the Beta Museum is a brilliant proof of concept and a fantastic overhaul of the 3D platformer into something completely different. The biggest issue is that though there are only 5 levels, and there is little variation or reason to go back once each one has been survived.
