ReCollect64 Reviews No. 56

Driving Strikers 64 ~ May 22, 2025

Revved Limit

Driving Strikers 64 is a port of the PC and Dreamcast indie sports action game, that was originally made by Reality Jump and LD2K, aka Luke Benstead and David Reichelt. Built with Libdragon, an open-source SDK for Nintendo 64, the game is an exciting new addition to the nearly 30-year-old console's line-up, and what's more, it shows off a lot of cool tricks!

The port is provided by Spooky Iluha, an N64 homebrew wizard with some extremely impressive ports and original games under their belt. Spooky most recently stole second place in the N64Brew Game Jam #5 contest with the absolutely stunning SpaceWaves, which was one of my personal favourites because it demonstrates simplistic 3D gameplay mixed with wonderfully vibrant graphics and overlays.

Have you ever heard of Rocket League? How could you not have heard of Rocket League? It was huge when it came out in 2015, but some 10 years later, it's not a name I hear very often.

In principle, Driving Strikers 64 bears an uncanny resemblance to the decade-old automotive soccer experience, and yet it continues to feel fresh. The idea is simple: drive your car into an oversized football (soccer, for the Americans out there) and knock it into your opposition's goal. The most goals scored when time's up wins!

You can choose from eight teams, including Frozen Thunder, Flaming Wheels, Steel Rebels, Lucky Vixens, Electric Squad, Cruel Fuel, Venomous Rhinos or Skilled All-Stars as your team, and you have a selection of four stadiums to play in. The arenas range from a sunny beach to a volcano, a neon nightscape city to a castle, and there is also one more secret arena to unlock!

As best as I can tell, the teams are purely livery-based, and none have any advantages over the other, so pick your favourite colour and get competing!

Controlling the game is an odd one. It uses a sort of tank controls, whereby you push the stick, and you move in that direction relative to the camera. It's like R/C racing POV with R/C racing-style controls. I found it quite difficult to control, and my ability to score was shockingly poor, unless it was in my own goal, but eventually I managed to win a few games in a row, and the feeling of progression really lifts you up.

The arcade-y replayability of this game is through the roof. In quick match you can select two teams, pick an arena, and you're all set to battle it out for goals. In league mode you literally play through the entire league of all 8 teams in a bid for ultimate glory and a peek at that unlockable last level!

Motoring Along

Graphically, the game looks fantastic with a wonderful design language used throughout, and the cars and the environments in particular look stunning.

With 480i resolution and Truecolour graphics that have HDR applied, the game has that Dreamcast look to it, that slick, arcadey Sega AM2 look of a quality game, albeit scaled back to work within the confines of a 16 MB N64 ROM.

The music in this game is "fire", as the cool kids would say. The EDM energy is large, and while the modern-style drum and bass and dubstep rhythms seem out of place on the late 90s hardware, it works so well!

The whole package is brilliant, from the visuals to the grooves, and the fun factor is definitely there. One small issue I found was that the game needs to be set to either nicest-looking, or fastest-running modes, so there is instantly a trade-off you must wrestle with for graphics over framerates or vice versa. When the objects initially load in, there is also quite a bit of stutter and slowdown too.

Lastly, it doesn't play nice with emulators, at least on my old daily laptop. Whereas every commercial game and 99% of ROM hacks work for me using M64P/RMG or Project 64, Driving Strikers 64 would not load on anything I threw it at. I assume the Libdragon requirements and snazzy enhancements used to make this game special are completely unsupported by most emulators that aren't cycle accurate. Ares and a machine that supports Vulkan was the solution that worked for me!

UPDATE: Version 1.0.3 released with fixes, horn honking, intro demo, translation adjustments, expansion pak crash fix, and most importantly, Spooky Iluha has optimised the game to run up to 60 FPS at 480i.

Conclusion:

Driving Strikers 64 proves that there is life in the console yet. Though it chugs a little in places and the controls are tricky, it's a homebrew port that brings Rocket League-esque E-Sports gameplay to the 64-bit system and demonstrates a shedload of new tricks to make it look modern.

I would highly recommend getting four of you together to give this a smash, because playing single-player doesn't have quite the same impact.
