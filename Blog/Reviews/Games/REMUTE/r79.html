ReCollect64 Reviews No. 79

A Cartridge Concert ~ Sep 22, 2025
Remute R64

Musical Magic

The humble Nintendo 64 cartridge was never classed as or proposed to become a storage medium for movies or music, and yet in March 2022, Denis Karimani, aka Remute, squeezed an impressive 15-track LP into just an 8 MB game cartridge, in conjunction with some clever N64 programming wizardry from Rasky, in a way that generates the music in-engine!

For some reason, this package puts me in mind of the cracktro intros you would find embedded in old PC games, with edgy synth-wave beats and a quirky visualiser to show off which group did it first. Let's find out whether Remute's "R64" is all for show, or if it has more substance.

Niche N64 Noises

As bizarre as it seems, there seems to have been a mini-rush to be the first to release an album on an N64 cart. "R64" however, does this natively, without packaging an MP3-capable N64 app into ROM form, and simply auto-playing a playlist. Only two albums were ever released in N64 cart format, Remute's "R64" and Belushi Speedball's thrash hardcore "What, Us Worry", which was released just two months after, in May 2022.

 

No tricks - this is not an embedded MP3-player, terribly compressed WAV-files on a microSD-card or other cheating: as with all previous Remute cartridge albums the sound on this cartridge gets generated and played back in realtime and it's all happening within meager 8 Megabytes! With 'R64' the Nintendo 64 console is your very own synthesizer and happy to serve you every time you turn it on and press play! 93,75 MHz, baby!

For this wizardry Remute cooperated with genius Nintendo 64 dev Rasky, who is not only responsible for the sound engine and player-GUI, but also managed to put together an amazingly trippy 3D-experience accompanying Remute's music - you will...uhmmm... fly.

The cartridges are region-free and will fit and play flawlessly on all models of the Nintendo 64 console - no matter if NTSC-U, PAL or NTSC-J.

Rhythmical Revelations

Of the 15 tracks available, there is a nice mixture which spans a variety of tempos, mainly with gritty electro-pop synths and thumping techno beats. The genre is extremely niche and reminds me a little of Aphex Twin, whose music, though strange, was also just as catchy and earworm-like.

The full line-up includes "The Mirror Reflects", "Superposition", "When A Thought Becomes A Weapon", "I Want To Go With U", "Your Church", "Business Coaching", "Stupid Fun", "Tradition Und Moderne", "On The Rocks", "Out Of Time", "What's Your Quote?", "Remains Of A Cult", "Jimmy Boy (Far Out)", "They Are Dangerous", and lastly, "A Message From The Creator".

Within those, I found that "Your Church" was a personal fave of mine, with a breakbeat sound and crisp drums, and I have to give a special mention to "Out of Time", which is another banger with punchy WipeOut 64 vibes. Lots of people seem to big-up "Super Position", but I honestly much preferred those two above everything else on offer.

The music is generated in-engine, which is extremely ingenious, and pressing Z brings up a QR code that can be scanned to listen to a bonus track on Remute.org.

The visualiser displays a kind of graphical landscape of the audible soundscape, but there isn't a great deal of variation in what you see. The B button flashes a colour on the screen and the A button makes it shake for a couple of seconds, but that's it. It would have been cool to have trippy colour options or a bolder assortment of shapes jutting out and flying around you; instead, it's a bit underwhelming and gimmicky in this respect.

The Pros:

 Very clever use of N64 technology.
 15 varied tracks: there's something for everyone.
 Visualiser and simple controls.
The Cons:

  Underwhelming visualiser.
  €39.99 is an extremely big ask IMHO.
Conclusion:

As clever as this is, it's so niche that I don't think it would be on many people's "want" list. The 15 tracks are nice and varied, and the sound quality is excellent, but for €39.99 it would have been nice to have something more to it than just a basic interface and a visualiser.
