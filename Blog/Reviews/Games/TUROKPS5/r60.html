Turok: Dinosaur Hunter [PS5] ~ Jun 6, 2025

Hi-Res Hunting

To mix things up a little, I have begun replaying N64 games that have seen a re-release on modern consoles. Packing my trusty PS5 Pro console, I have recently picked up a bunch of titles that have been given a modern makeover, and today I'll begin with Nightdive Studios' remaster of Turok: Dinosaur Hunter.

Turok: Dinosaur Hunter released on Nintendo Switch in 2019 and PS4 in 2021, but most recently, in March 2025, it got a nice native PS5 upgrade that boosted the frame rate to 120 FPS as well as restoring cut content back into the game!

Dinosaur Delights

Turok: Dinosaur Hunter spans across eight varied levels, including Catacombs, Tree Top Village, The Ruins and The Lost Land. There are lava pits, swamps, fortresses and ancient tombs to explore on the hunt for shards, switches and keys. The levels are still brilliant to explore, and there are so many secrets and weapons scattered around that you can be searching for ages. Helpfully, there is an OSD map you can pull up with the Square button that gives you hints on how to get to those tricky places.

There are 15 weapons to use and four bosses to battle, including the Longhunter, Mantis, T-Rex, and the Cyborg Campaigner. The bosses are a huge difficulty spike in this game, where the majority of running and gunning is done with relative ease if you're good at strafing, but the bosses will kick your ass repeatedly unless you have a bulletproof strategy to take them down. The first boss, the Longhunter, for example, spawns Humvees to mow you down with, and circling and blasting is your only chance of survival until you face him on foot, at which point ranged accuracy is key.

The revamped modernised controls are superb. With the addition of gyroscope aiming, it has never been easier to adjust for pinpoint precision on the move. I was sceptical at first, but it worked perfectly and consistently across my entire playthrough. For a more authentic feel you can disable it altogether, but honestly it's a huge upgrade to the original's clunky, unconventional control scheme.

Gorgeous Gunning

Graphically, the game is a treat for the eyes. The mixture of shooting, swimming and platforming is brilliant, and the slick, silky framerates allow you to plough through the game at a rate of knots. The only downside is that because of the twitchy motion controls, fast-paced running around and camera sway, I felt quite motion sick in places. I found this odd because I'm usually pretty good with things like PSVR2 and Meta Quest 3 games, so to get motion sick on a flatscreen was strange.

The sense of scale and the atmospheric times of day are rendered extremely well thanks to Nightdive Studios' KEX engine, the software behind the port. The game looks incredible throughout and runs perfectly smoothly with zero slowdown, no lag and precision controls that make you realise that game preservation needs to be handled this way. Services like NSO and the recent GoldenEye 007 re-release could have massively benefitted from Nightdive's treatment, as this game sets the bar for what is possible with remasters and demonstrates the gold standard for how others should follow.

Textures, lens flares, weapon effects and, of course, the spurting squirting blood in the death animations have all made it across seamlessly, with additional details such as reflections and enhanced lighting and draw distance serving to increase the immersion. The cut content also shows that the game can be improved upon without altering the core mechanics or destroying the OG look and feel. Simply adding back a dinosaur seems like such a small addition, but seeing things you never got to see in the original, which have been tactfully engineered back into the game, is a wonder to behold.

I can't wait to sink my teeth into the two sequels, because if they look and play anything like this, I'm in for a treat!

The Pros:

 Gyro aiming elevates this above the stock N64 version.
 Smooth framerates and zero input lag.
 Upscaled, more colourful, and less foggy!
 Features sensible graphical upgrades and restored cut content.
 Remappable modern controls make this infinitely better to play.
The Cons:

 The bosses are very difficult to grind down.
 Runs so fast I felt motion sickness coming on.
Conclusion:

This is easily the best way to officially play Turok: Dinosaur Hunter in any shape or form. The 4K textures and 120 FPS framerates make it gorgeous to glide around, while the core gameplay manages to respectfully retain its original look and feel.

Highly recommended for a good four to five hours of classic dinosaur blasting, especially if you're a fan of the original.
