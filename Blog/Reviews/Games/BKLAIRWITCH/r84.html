ReCollect64 Reviews No. 84

BK: The Lair Witch Project ~ Oct 29, 2025

Horrific Happenings

October 26th marked the early access release of Markus Kurko's latest ROM hack, Banjo-Kazooie: The Lair Witch Project. This hack plants the bear and bird duo firmly within a horror-infused version of the OG platformer.

With 16 Jiggies, 200 notes, 16 Mumbo tokens, 4 honeycomb pieces, 1 Kurkoin, and a secret trophy for players who achieve the alternate ending, this mod intends to give fans of the game a petrifyingly good time on emulators or real hardware!

Default Controls:
NothingCenter camera (hold to lock)
Nothing Up/Down: Change zoom level, Left/Right: Rotate camera
Run, walk or tiptoe Perform attacks
Crouch Jump (Hold to jump higher/further)
Pause Game

An Instant Kurko Klassic

Beginning your latest creepy quest from your quaint little cottage, Tooty informs the titular pair that their friends have all gone missing. Banjo, the stoic adventurer he is, takes on the mission to find and rescue them if they're in trouble. Just seconds into the game, you see that Spiral Mountain has been overcome with evil. Rivers run red, the sky is raining blood, and there are bats, ghouls, demons, skeletons and freaky little collectable eyeballs littering the scenery.

I immediately found that Boggy's kids are hiding somewhere; I won't divulge exactly where, but they're performing some unhinged satanic rituals with various character's eyeless, decapitated noggins. Restoring the eyeballs to them is just one task you must undertake to complete this scare-filled mod! You will find eyeballs in weird places as well as sliced off blood-dripping faces strewn around the levels including Bubsy and Donkey Kong himself, amongst piles of Jinjo cadavers. The amount of imagery and atmosphere captured and packed into each area is unbelievable. From the gloomy foreboding darkness of the scantily torch-lit sections to the unnerving children's voices permeating the background of the grimy cemetery areas, this game is masterfully sinister throughout.

Collecting notes, you can unlock doors and access new areas to collect more goodies and figure out more of the overarching mystery at hand. I won't provide any spoilers or give away the twists, but it is a pretty good little story! With time-based and tricky platforming puzzles to solve, there is a big variety of things to do here. I'm (still) shockingly slow with the time-based puzzles, so it took me a few tries to get some of them dialled.

Taking tokens to Mumbo will transport you to a stunning-looking circus-themed level, which incorporates all the clown-based horror tropes you could possibly need. If you suffer from coulrophobia then good flippin' luck!

Blood Soaked Fur

In typical Kurko fashion, woven into the very fabric of the game, you'll discover a plethora of Easter eggs, cameos and much, much more. I particularly like all the spooky elements that have been added, but the South Park "Game Over" screen legitimately cracked me up; it was so randomly comical!

With just two areas to explore and one boss fight, this ROM hack is a little smaller than most of the previous efforts, but that doesn't mean that the quality isn't incredibly polished. Every surface, every character, every item is customised or expertly crafted to fit the eerie settings. I have to admit, the game has a cartoon version of the vibes I feel when playing Shadow Man. With the rivers of blood and piles and piles of bodies everywhere, it's a massive visual departure from the happy, vibrant Isle O' Hags we grew up with, but it sure does work!

The boss fight is big and bold, and the levels are superbly assembled and scattered with intricate things to find. You're guaranteed a thrill-ride, that's all I'll say. There are a variety of jumpscares built in too, so pack a spare pair of pants if you're playing at night!

The Lair Witch Project contains a handful of hours of gameplay, so overall it is a little bit short in terms of length if you're good at not dying frequently. I definitely had to keep remembering to save periodically, rather than losing my progress, because I kept falling off things or losing all my health.

The Pros:

 An excellent horror-themed hack.
 Plenty to do over 2 levels.
 Masses to collect & discover.
 Works on real hardware!
The Cons:

  It's a bit short; if I had to quible.
Conclusion:

Kurko never fails to disappoint with his stunning hacking skills. The Lair Witch Project is yet another golden feather in an already gilded nest full of incredible hacks which extend the life of the 1998 platforming collect-a-thon game!

I have yet to get the "better ending" or that illusive last trophy, so I have plenty of reason to keep chipping away at this in the lead-up to Halloween!
