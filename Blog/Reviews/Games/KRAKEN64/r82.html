ReCollect64 Reviews No. 82

Kraken64 ~ Oct 21, 2025

Boating Battles

Kraken64 is a game that is heavily inspired by The Legend of Zelda: The Wind Waker's nautical battles and was designed by Vapnet, programmed by Nomnom, and given audible presence by Inverted Silence in under two months!

This game only released last month, though it has somehow gone largely unnoticed; I only found out about it this week! In order to give it the exposure it deserves, I wanted to give it a thorough test sooner rather than later.

Mysteries of the Deep

First and foremost, this game IS playable on flashcarts, which is awesome, but to be able to play it on your PC, you will need to use the Ares emulator, because every other emulator I threw it at refused to boot or crashed out. This seems to be the trend with the latest LibDragon games and homebrew.

Once Kraken64 is up and running, you're tasked with defeating an evil kraken who is causing havoc in the lagoon. Trudging from the starting bay area to the lagoon via bright yellow portals, you scour the lagoon for signs of the beast, only to get sucked into a swirling, twisty, seafaring battle of cannons versus sea mines and kraken attacks. The kraken looks like a dragon, whereas I imagined it would be squid-like; it was actually serpentine-looking, and its attacks are either spewing out mines or slamming down on top of you.

Once you're engaged with the target, the controls shift from driving to battle mode, and your options are to jump over the spikey mines and skillfully dodge the other attacks all while firing your cannon to blast away at the weak spots on the titular enemy.

Thar She Blows

While the game is fun, it's extremely short-lived, with just one boss and two small arenas to admire. The graphics are stunning in these areas, with some nicely textured backgrounds, a cozy warmth to the bay environment, and a harsher, more foreboding atmosphere for the Lagoon area.

Sound-wise, the Island has a nice welcoming and familiar vibe, whereas the lagoon has eery sfx until you begin the encounter. The music shifts to a more upbeat melody which builds and thrums along with the action. It all makes for one beautiful audio/visual package!

You can totally feel the inspiration from The Wind Waker too; as you rotate around the creature and exploit its weaknesses, it can get really fast-paced and it can be quite tricky to coordinate your dodging and shooting at the same time. It's intriguing, frantic, and fun, but it's over way too fast, with mere minutes of gameplay at its disposal.

The Pros:

 An incredibly good looking game.
 Tense battling action.
 Fish jewels to collect.
The Cons:

  Just one boss and two arenas.
  Tough little battle!
Conclusion:

Kraken64 is a little sparse but packed with potential. This demo is just the very tip of an awesome iceberg that demonstrates what these devs can truly do. If this game gets expanded on and more fleshed out then this is certainly one to watch going forward!
