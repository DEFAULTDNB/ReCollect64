GE007: Disrupt Surveillance Equipment [V1.1] ~ Mar 26, 2026

Smile, You're On CCTV

Rare's GoldenEye 007 (1998) left such an indelible mark on the video games industry that even today, in Earth year 2026, we are still getting hacks and mods of our favourite movie-based spy shooter.

This recent mod, entitled "Disrupt Surveillance Equipment", is the sole work of CptLima, a competitive GoldenEye 007 player and streamer turned ROM hacker, who has added in his own brand of spice to reinvigorate and shake up the original game's formula.

The premise is simple; seek and destroy all cameras. Sounds simple, right? Well, whip out your emulators and patch your ROMs because I'm going in!

Slick Camera Work

With all of the 20 main levels re-rubbed to contain 15-25 CCTV cameras as an additional objective to the originals, as well as guard patrols being beefed up to make things trickier regardless of the difficulty, there is a lot to do here. This ROM hack is strictly for use on emulators if you want a good time playing it. The additional guards and increased number of objects sadly make this one unsuitable for N64 hardware to run properly.

Starting out on the iconic Dam level, you quickly discover parked cars, alternate alarm panels and even more objects that look strangely familiar yet were completely absent from the OG games' release. This is because CptLima has painstakingly restored a metric tonne of beta content to every level, such as background detail models, vehicles and textures the original game omitted due to time or memory constraints. I just wish that the island had been somehow added back in to the dam: that would have been legendary!

Scouting around any of the levels, regular players will feel at home and familiar with them. You might even recall small areas of salvation, somewhere to explore that was safe from prying eyes, somewhere to catch your breath. Well, not any more! CptLima has converted every liminal space into new expansive areas to hunt for the eyes in the sky. For example, I remembered an area in Control where you could walk through a grate and get some armour, and low and behold: a camera was above me watching the whole thing. It really keeps you on the edge of your seat!

It's quite literally impossible not to get seen by the cameras, so thankfully getting spotted does not render your diligent efforts useless, and you can continue to tick off those objectives with the ringing of the alarms in your ears. You'll begin hearing them in your dreams too; it happens that frequently!

(GE007: DSE takes advantage of the footsteps patch (by Carnivorous) a modification tool that is designed to enhance the original game's engine with features such as Expansion Pak detection and enhanced customization for more complex custom level setups. It's the same tech used to create GoldenEye X, the Multiplayer expansion for the classic FPS)

Memes and More

With difficulty options including Agent, Secret Agent and "Camera Agent", you get the gist of this game's humour. There are between 2 and 6 objectives per level, and every single one includes the disruption of the titular equipment. The cameras can be absolutely anywhere, from immediately in front of you as you open a door to way up in an otherwise innocuous ventilation system. I love the creativity that has gone into making this mod not just entertaining but explorative and taxing too.

Throughout the jaunt, which generally follows the OG games' story beats, you'll find a few surprises, some cameos, and a lot of references to other players within the competitive GoldenEye 007 scene. One in particular that tickled me was that of Natalya stating that the GoldenEye satellite was positioned to fire on AdzyIn3D's house, but there are heaps more references to all the big names, such as Graslu00 and this year's LTK Netplay Cup winner: THOneTwoSix.

Another amusing thing is the variety and scale of the cameras. On Surface, I was happily scouting around when, out of nowhere, the alarm sounded. Now, I wasn't near any buildings; I had taken out almost all guards, but still something somewhere had spotted me. Turning around and looking across the horizon, I spotted it: a gigantic camera sweeping the environment. It had to have been 20 feet tall!

The Pros:

 20 levels remixed to add more camera blasting.
 Beta & cut content restored, as well as memes & cameos added.
 Three difficulties, all of which are tough.
The Cons:

 Too complex to work on real hardware.
 Strictly single player, no multiplayer mods.
Conclusion:

GoldenEye 007: Disrupt Surveillance Equipment is a triumphant stealth-based remix of a much-loved nostalgic title. It will send anyone who plays it down a paranoid rabbit hole of checking every corner and searching every nook and cranny for those blasted cameras!

The addition of beta content also elevates this hack and keeps it feeling like a legitimate piece of Rare's own work. This is marvellous, and therefore an absolute must play!
