ReCollect64 Reviews No. 87

Simpsons Kart 64 ~ Nov 19, 2025

Cartoon Capers

What if Mario Kart 64 was re-skinned with characters from the hit cartoon series The Simpsons? Well, wonder no more, because ROM hacker snipertf22014 has done just that, and I'm about to take it for a spin around Springfield!

This review will merely take into account the quality of modding that has gone into the revamp, not the core pillars of gameplay, as this is only a re-skin and not a fully fledged brand new game built within the MK64 engine.

Default Controls:

Recognisable Racers

Snipertf22014 has produced a few reskins now, including GTA San Andreas Kart, Slasher Kart 64 and Pokemon Kart 64, and each one has incorporated a decent level of synchronising motifs and iconic characters and items that ties the hack together nicely.

Simpsons Kart 64 is no different, with the characters all swapped out for Homer, Bart, Marge, Lisa, Apu, Chief Wiggum, Krusty and Flanders, and each character has been carefully given their portraits, 2D sprites and relevant voice clips to make them feel coherently grounded within the game. Every aspect of each character has been modified to ensure that each one feels complete, and I have to say that hearing each character spouting off a variety of lines while razzing around the MK64 courses was pretty entertaining! In this version I noticed that Apu sounds like Yoshi, and Wiggum has an insane-sounding, yet amusingly warped voice.

Iconic Items

Instead of bananas, stars and shells, we have Duff Beer cans, donuts and catapults. They're a nice little nod to the series staples and serve to tie the characters nicely together with the type of comic mischief the series is synonymous with. The cups have notably also been renamed to now be called things like "Duff Cup", "Road Rage", "Spring 500" and "Rigel Cup".

Sadly, there are no actual Simpsons tracks in this hack. I would have loved to see 16 new tracks in here, even if they were stylised to look two-dimensional, more basic, and in keeping with the cartoon aesthetic. Instead we have the original 16 tracks that have been rather pointlessly renamed to "Satans Anvil" or "Mt. Embolism". Sure, each track has been retouched to add in textures that theme them towards their titular titles, but it left me feeling underwhelmed given the care and attention the characters had lavished upon them.

We all know that it's possible to create and import user-built tracks into a Mario Kart 64 ROM hack using tools such as Tarmac64, so it's a little disappointing not to see any effort having gone into that aspect of the game. It would have made the game feel complete and far more interesting.

The Pros:

 Brilliant Simpsons-themed reskin.
 Voice clips for all characters.
 Modified items and textures.
The Cons:

 No unique tracks.
Conclusion:

It's always a mixed bag when reviewing ROM hacks like this, because they can either impress you or leave you feeling like you wanted more out of it.

Though the included SFX are great, I would have liked to have seen a selection of new tracks as well as the characters, because though the tracks have been renamed and had a few textures swapped here and there, they're just the exact same old tracks we are used to.
