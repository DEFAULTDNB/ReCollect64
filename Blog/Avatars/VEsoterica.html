ReCollect64 Chats No. 40

Video Game Esoterica ~ Sep 30, 2025
@VEsoterica

Welcome to ReCollect64 chat Anthony! Let's kick-off this Q&A by describing your presence on social media for people:

I am Video Game Esoterica, AKA Anthony, a collector of retro games, rare hardware and obscure arcade boards with a focus on preservation, dumping and just showing people all the fun things they missed back in the day.

When did you get into gaming, any formative recollections?

As long as I can remember I played video games. My father loved NES so basically every new console would appear in our house not long after it released. So it's been a life long thing for me.

What does your retro gaming collection look like?

Massive and filled with weird items that take up WAY too much space. So many rare arcade boards stored for use later with a lot of "in progress" repairs or control mods waiting to be finished.

Do you have a favourite console?

Ask me on a Monday and I would say 3DO. Ask me on a Tuesday and I would say Dreamcast.

What games do you play regularly?

With the YouTube channel now I play like 500-600 games a YEAR just for footage so basically everything but at least once a year I will replay Grim Fandango and Symphony of the Night like clockwork.

What snacks/drinks keep you fuelled up for gaming?

Water haha. Honestly I don't snack and game. Weird right?

Do you have a prized gaming possession?

I love all the games as just holding a physical object is fun as a collector but if I had to pick one item it would be my Tobe! Polystars board and original art set.

Do you have any gaming unicorns left to obtain?

Not so much left to obtain but to repair and make work haha. Crazy Streets: Thrill Drive 4 I have yet to find the drive for. I have the security dongle for reverse engineering it when it does finally get found. I love to find items you "cant find" though. It's not about cost or impressiveness...its purely about "find it and share it".

Are you looking forward to any games in particular, regarding the recent N64 Recomp projects?

Bomberman Hero. The only correct answer! haha.

You’re ordering pizza, you get three toppings, what’s your combo?

Pepperoni, fresh garlic and basil.

What’s your most expensive/thrifty retro purchase to date?

Thrifty? A free 3DO M2. Most expensive? Honestly I dropped like $600 on a copy of Matrimelee on AES like fifteen years ago and back then that was a LOT.

Lastly: How many consoles do you own?

Oh god this will take a minute lol; 3DO, 3DO M2 FZ-21, 3DO M2 FZ-35, 13 3DO M2 arcade boards (if you count those), PS1, PS2, PS2 PSX DVD, PS3, PS5, PS5, OG Xbox, Xbox 360, Xbox One, Xbox One X, Xbox Series X, NES, Sharp Twin Famicom, SNES, Super Famicom, N64, GameCube, Wii, Wii U, Switch, Switch 2, Nuon, PC-Engine Duo R, PC Engine Briefcase Unit, PC-FX, Master System, Sega Genesis, Sega Saturn, Sega Dreamcast, Sega NAOMI, OUYA, FM Towns Marty x2, Neo Geo AES, Neo Geo MVS, Neo Geo CD top loader, Neo Geo CDZ, MiSTer FPGA x7, PSVR, PSVR 2, Oculus Quest 2 and 3.

If you count arcade boards ask me and I'll DM you THAT list haha.

Thank you very much for taking the time to be part of ReCollect64's Q&A chats!

Hailing from Chicago, Illinois, Anthony combined his love for filmmaking with his passion for gaming and tech in 2018 to produce one of the internet's most captivating and informative channels for modern information when it comes to the rare and obscure.

His impressive collection of retro and modern hardware and devices also makes him an invaluable source for all things MiSTer or anything arcade-related, and his videos never fail to entertain and educate!
