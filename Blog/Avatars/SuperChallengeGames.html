SuperChallengeGames ~ Oct 14, 2025

Welcome to ReCollect64 chat Ken! Let's kick-off this Q&A by describing a little about yourself.

I’m Ken Barnes, a web Developer, former game journalist, and owner of the online import game store SuperChallenge Ltd.

When did you get into gaming, any formative recollections?

My family got an Atari 2600 when I was 4, and we only had three games - Donkey Kong, Pole Position, and Kangaroo. Whenever the TV was free, that was my time. I’ve never looked back.

What does your retro gaming collection look like?

It’s less voluminous than it was previously, as I sold off a fair amount in order to start the store. I only keep things I really enjoy or am definitely going to play, so I’ve around 200 titles across tons of consoles.

Do you have a favourite console?

My heart says Dreamcast. My head says PlayStation 2. Nostalgia says Xbox 360.

What games do you play regularly?

Anything and everything, though my top replays are Burnout Revenge, Burnout Takedown, and anything by PlatinumGames.

What snacks/drinks keep you fuelled up for gaming?

Cherry Pepsi Max and Popchips.

Do you have a prized gaming possession?

Not one in particular. I love all my children equally.

Do you have any gaming unicorns left to obtain?

None that I can think of!

Are you looking forward to any games in particular, regarding the recent N64 Recomp projects?

WWF No Mercy, Wrestlemania 2000, and International Superstar Soccer 2000..

You’re ordering pizza, you get three toppings, what’s your combo?

pepperoni, Cumberland sausage, bacon. Now I’m hungry.

What’s your most expensive/thrifty retro purchase to date?

Most expensive is a copy of Outrun 2 SP on PS2, which I still got for a great deal. Most thrifty is a boxed Neo Geo CD from a boot sale over a decade ago. £10 because the seller thought it was faulty. Still working fine to this day!

Lastly: How many consoles do you own?

29
