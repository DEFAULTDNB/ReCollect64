ReCollect64 Chats No. 26

Tony From Hard4Games! ~ Feb 17, 2025

Welcome to ReCollect64 chat Hard4Games! Let's kick-off this Q&A by describing your presence on social media for people:

Hi! I'm Tony, a producer on a YouTube show called Hard4Games. We cover retro gaming preservation, Zelda mods, retro homebrews, etc.

When did you get into gaming, any formative recollections?

In the late 80s I got a bit into gaming as a young child, but dove really deep into it during the SNES era. Some of my favorite moments were playing 3-player Secret of Mana with friends. Mario 64 and Zelda Ocarina of Time were also formative, mind-blowing gaming events for me.

What does your retro gaming collection look like?

Skimpy! I sold most of my software and kept most of my hardware. I had SO many titles that I've just been consolidating to flash carts (sorry!). That said, I like having some select games around and like to make sure I still have access to the consoles. I also have a ton of development equipment for game preservation purposes.

Do you have a favourite console?

I'm the most nostalgic for the SNES or N64.

What games do you play regularly?

I rarely ever replay games, at least not start-to-finish. Honestly the only games I've ever replayed are a few Zelda titles and a few Mana titles. I play A LOT of Zelda Ocarina of Time mods, primarily for my YouTube channel.

What snacks/drinks keep you fuelled up for gaming?

Beer. A good bourbon.

Do you have a prized gaming possession?

A development Nintendo 64DD.

Do you have any gaming unicorns left to obtain?

I don't actively collect games anymore, mostly putting my money into gaming preservation so not really. At least not retail games. I'd LOVE for someone to find prototypes of Zelda 64, Earthbound 64, the SNES CD version of Secret of Mana, etc.

Are you looking forward to any games in particular, regarding the recent N64 Recomp projects?

YES any and all. It's awesome!

You’re ordering pizza, you get three toppings, what’s your combo?

Feta & anchovies. OR goat cheese, honey, & red pepper flakes.

What’s your most expensive/thrifty retro purchase to date?

I once bought a Master System, multiple games, and stacks of 1st party SNES games for about $40 at a thrift shop. However, that was years ago when such a thing was still possible! My most expensive has probably been the N64DD development drive that I mentioned earlier.

And Finally: How many consoles do you own?

NES, Famicom / Famicom Disk System, SNES, Game Boy, Game Boy Color, Game Boy Advance, Game Boy Advance SP, Virtual Boy, N64 / N64DD / Japanese variants, GameCube, Wii, Wii U, Switch, DS, 3DS, PSP, PS1, 2, 3, 4, Xbox, Xbox 360, Atari 2600, Atari Jaguar, Odyssey 2, Neo Geo Pocket Color, WonderSwan, WonderSwan Color, Swan Crystal, Master System, Sega Genesis, Sega CD, Sega 32X, Game Gear, Sega Saturn, Dreamcast, Game.Com, 3DO.

Development units: N64DD, Wii U (2 versions), PS3, Xbox 360 (46)

Thank you very much for your time Tony, and a massive thank you for being part of ReCollect64 chats!

Hailing from Detroit, Michigan, Tony delivers high-quality, factual information-laden content via his incredible YouTube channel. I have been a massive fan of his content for a long time, so this has been a fantastic Q&A session with some incredible answers and memories shared!

Tony has a staggering 46 consoles in his collection (without an exact count on the JPN variants), which allows him to enter the chat console contest directly into third place!
