ReCollect64 Chats No. 29

N64Magazine! ~ Apr 15, 2025
@N64Magazine

Welcome to ReCollect64 chat Lewis! Let's kick-off this Q&A by describing your presence on social media for people:

I was a writer for a couple of gaming websites several moons ago now, as well publishing bits on my own website. Lately I had the idea of @N64Magazine simply to make myself (and hopefully others) giggle, plus serve as a little tribute to the legendary British mag, and the Nintendo 64 itself. Even if you don't know what N64 Magazine was, the no-context nature of it means anyone should be able to enjoy its soothing doses of bygone gaming nostalgia.

When did you get into gaming, any formative recollections?

I received a Sega Master System for Christmas aged five, which I loved. But when this new game called 'Sonic the Hedgehog' appeared not long after, I became absolutely hooked. That spiky blue 'hog has much to answer for.

What does your retro gaming collection look like?

It's just the consoles that I enjoy, plus a sizeable gaming magazine collection. The biggest (and favourite) is for my Japanese Nintendo 64, which includes plenty of controllers and accessories too.

Do you have a favourite console?

My opinion changes on this. I suppose the correct answer is the Super Famicom – it's faultless in every way, really. But the Nintendo 64 is the one system which I always have plugged in and ready to go, so perhaps it's that?

What games do you play regularly?

These days I only get to play sporadically, but whenever I do I try to work through my backlog of games I haven't finished, or (to my shame) even opened. Mario Kart 8 or SM64 Randomizer are invariably where I return to for re-playing.

What snacks/drinks keep you fuelled up for gaming?

I've reached the age where I need to keep snacking to a minimum. But I confess I do still enjoy a Dairy Milk Button or three.

Do you have a prized gaming possession?

I was one of the blessed few who got hold of a SteelSticks64, erm, stick. I'm sorry to report that it's glorious.

Do you have any gaming unicorns left to obtain?

I want for nothing. Well... most that I do have my eye on are just too expensive to justify now anyway. Ah, well.

Are you looking forward to any games in particular, regarding the recent N64 Recomp projects?

I've zero interest in playing games on PCs, so not particularly. In my limited understanding of it, it should help those clever modders in making more/better ROM hacks for native consoles too, so I'll look forward to what that brings.

You’re ordering pizza, you get three toppings, what’s your combo?

Several slices of existential dread, a pinch of amnesia, topped off with a dollop of pure cynicism.

What’s your most expensive/thrifty retro purchase to date?

I handed over £250 for an imported XRGB Framemeister around the time they came out. That's for playing retro games – does it count? In my defence, I do still use it today. As for thrifty finds, I bought a boxed Japanese launch N64 with 9 boxed classic games (Mario 64, StarFox 64, etc.) all delivered for just £60. This was in the the halcyon days of bargain eBay, about 20 years ago.

And Finally: How many consoles do you own?

13: N64, Super Famicom, GameCube, PS2, Victor V-Saturn, Dreamcast, Nintendo DS, Wii U, GBA, Mega Drive Mini, Amiga A500 Mini, Super Famicom Mini, PlayStation Classic.

Thank you very much for your time and being part of ReCollect64 chats!
