ReCollect64 Chats No. 19

Mr Talida ~ Nov 21, 2024
@MrTalida

Welcome to ReCollect64 Q&A chats MrTalida! Let's begin this Q&A by describing your presence on social media:

I'm a retro game archivist and preservationist! I post an eclectic mix of things, like previously unseen game design documents, game source code, and other odds and ends. My most recent preservation effort the past couple years has been Keshi Corner, a project where I'm producing detailed 3D scans of vintage video game toy figures. I think merchandise like this is something that's rather ephemeral and it gets overlooked too often when we think about what we should be preserving from the game industry.

When did you get into gaming, any formative recollections?

My first console was the Intellivision, which I think my parents picked up on a whim from Sears sometime in the 1980s. I'm not sure why; they've never been gamers! But Intellivision titles like Astrosmash and Advanced Dungeons & Dragons were my entry point into the wonderful world of video games.

What does your retro gaming collection look like?

I was (and AM!) a Nintendo kid, so there's lots of Mario in my collection, and lots, lots of Zelda in particular; I'm a huge Zelda fan and I've got a fairly large collection of all the games and various merchandise.

Do you have a favourite console?

There's so many great ones, but I think the SNES will always have a special place in my heart. I got it when I was about 7, so it's the console that occupies that special, formative period of my childhood. And with games like Super Mario World, Link to the Past, Starfox, and countless more classics in a game library that numbers in the hundreds, it was a really special console for a lot of people, I think! What a great time in gaming...

What games do you play regularly?

I just finished Zelda: Echoes of Wisdom and loved it! I'm also playing the Advance Wars 1 & 2 remake. I've revisited Legend of Grimrock again recently, and earlier this Summer my wife and I had a blast playing the Riven remake.

What snacks/drinks keep you fuelled up for gaming?

Fruit snacks, by the handful!

Do you have a prized gaming possession?

I have some beloved gaming artifacts, like my full set of Legend of Zelda keshi gomu figures from 1986! One item that comes to mind is the super rare Twilight Princess trailer Nintendo DS game card that Nintendo gave to select attendees of E3 2005. Mine's signed by Koji Kondo!

Do you have any gaming unicorns left to obtain?

Someday I want that Super Mario Pinball machine Gottlieb released in the early 1990s... if only it weren't thousands of dollars!

Are you looking forward to any games in particular, regarding the recent N64 Recomp projects?

I think the whole recomp scene is super exciting. I haven't been keeping my finger to the pulse of what's been released and what's still in the works, but I nevertheless look forward to seeing N64's greatest classics augmented and revitalized as recomps!

You’re ordering pizza, you get three toppings, what’s your combo?

Mushroom and sausage for sure!

What’s your most expensive/thrifty retro purchase to date?

I think one of the most egregiously expensive gaming items I've spent money on, from a price to size ratio, is this 2.5" tall plastic Link statue from A Link to the Past that cost $200! I really wanted to scan this rare Zelda toy to make it available to everyone, so it was worth it. HERE

And Finally: How many consoles do you own?

NES, SNES, N64, GCN, Wii, WiiU, Switch, Switch OLED (does that count?), GBC, GBA, DS, DS Lite, DSi, 3DS, New 3DS XL, Famicom (+Disk System), TV Game 15, Intellivision, Intellivision II, CD-i, Xbox 360, Game Gear, and Tiger Gamecom! So let's call it 22.

Thank you very much for your time and being part of ReCollect64 chats!
